Huxley argued that just as the sciences evolved, so too must ethics adapt and grow under scrutiny. By challenging long-held beliefs, he sought to free morality from dogma, envisioning a landscape where ethical principles could evolve alongside human understanding. This was a radical departure from the fixed moral codes that had dominated previous thought, suggesting that humans could cultivate a more nuanced, adaptable ethical framework. Huxley’s insistence on reason as the guiding force for moral evolution represented a shift from tradition towards an empathetic rationalism, one that saw progress as not merely inevitable, but necessary for the survival of societal values.
