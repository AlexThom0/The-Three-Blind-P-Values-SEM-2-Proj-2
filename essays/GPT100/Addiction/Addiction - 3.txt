Viewing addiction primarily through a disease lens may overlook the complex interplay of environmental, psychological, and biological factors. Patients often grapple with a loss of agency associated with the illness narrative, which can hinder recovery. Emphasizing personal responsibility and empowerment can facilitate a more holistic approach. Furthermore, addressing the social contexts that contribute to various forms of addiction can lead to more effective interventions. Thus, it is crucial to broaden our perspective on addiction beyond traditional paradigms.
