Instead, personal histories and environmental factors play significant roles in shaping one's likelihood of developing an addiction. For example, early trauma, socioeconomic status, and cultural attitudes towards substance use can profoundly influence both the manifestation of cravings and the ability to resist them. Such multifaceted influences indicate that addiction is often a learned response shaped by individual circumstances, rather than merely a biological inevitability. Understanding these varied dimensions can lead to more effective prevention strategies and treatment approaches that address the broader context of addiction.
