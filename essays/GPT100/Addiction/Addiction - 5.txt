The concept of "tragic love" has evolved, where emotional struggle is not merely incidental but desired, almost romanticized. This shift reflects a broader cultural narrative that equates depth of feeling with authenticity, elevating agony to a valuable experience. Social media and popular culture frequently celebrate tales of unrequited love, emphasizing the beauty in melancholy, thereby embedding the idea that emotional turmoil is essential to meaningful connections. Such portrayals influence our expectations of intimacy, blurring the lines between love and suffering.
