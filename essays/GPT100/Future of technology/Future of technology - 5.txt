The results suggested that listeners often rely on the rankings as a heuristic for quality, prioritizing the songs at the top of the charts while ignoring those deemed less popular, regardless of their actual musical merit. This phenomenon is further compounded by the tendency of consumers to converge around choices that are already favored by others, solidifying the standing of hits even if their intrinsic quality might not warrant such acclaim.
