When evaluating historical populations, it is crucial to recognize that the narratives surrounding them are often shaped by contemporary biases. The categories we apply—civilized versus primitive, successful versus unsuccessful—reflect our own cultural values rather than the complexities of these groups' existences. For instance, while Neanderthals are frequently discussed in terms of their survival or lack thereof, the Hittites and Gauls faced their own unique challenges and adaptations in a rapidly changing environment. This nuanced perspective reveals that rather than searching for inherent flaws, we should examine how each society navigated the pressures of their times, influencing their trajectories significantly.
