This practice transcended mere survival; it fostered a sense of communal identity and responsibility. Hill observed how each member of the group played a distinct role, contributing to the well-being of all. The act of sharing was imbued with significance, turning sustenance into a symbol of unity. As he experienced these rituals firsthand, he began to grasp the complexities of their social structure, marked by a balance between individual effort and communal benefit.
