The traditional practices of the Marind, deeply intertwined with their ancestral lands, face erosion as these plantations proliferate. With little recourse to resist this encroachment, the Marind people's connection to their territory diminishes, leading to a loss of traditional knowledge and communal cohesion. Each year, as more land is claimed, the potential for sustainable coexistence between agribusiness and Indigenous ways of life diminishes, reflecting a tragic trade-off wherein immediate economic benefits overshadow long-term ecological and cultural consequences.
