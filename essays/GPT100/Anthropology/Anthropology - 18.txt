The Niha utilize this rich tapestry of storytelling to articulate their grievances and aspirations, embedding their cultural identity within oral traditions that resonate deeply. This dynamic reconfiguration of authority manifests not just in their legends but also in their contemporary discourse, which challenges dominant narratives and seeks recognition. By giving voice to their history and struggles, the Niha redefine their relationship with the past, transforming echoes of oppression into instruments of empowerment. The heart, once silenced, now beats with renewed vigor and purpose.
