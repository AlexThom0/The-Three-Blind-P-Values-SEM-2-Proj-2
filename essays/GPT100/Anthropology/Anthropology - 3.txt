As ocean levels gradually altered the geography, they also reshaped the lives and narratives of the people inhabiting these regions. The resulting maritime landscapes were not merely a backdrop but a vital component of cultural identity and memory. These indigenous peoples crafted intricate stories that reflected their experiences and observations, embedding environmental realities into their lore. Such narratives served dual purposes: they preserved historical knowledge of landscapes long submerged and acted as cautionary tales about the forces of nature. The significance of these stories extends beyond mere myth; they represent a deep connection to an ever-changing environment.
