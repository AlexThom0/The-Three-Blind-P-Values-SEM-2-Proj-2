Modern society's reliance on technology—especially in communication and navigation—means that even a minor cosmic event could lead to widespread disruption. The interconnectedness of systems creates vulnerabilities, where a solar storm could potentially disable satellites, interrupt global communications, and jeopardize power grids. Hence, understanding the implications of such solar activities is not merely academic; it raises critical questions about preparedness and resilience in an age where our civilizations rely heavily on continuous, uninterrupted technological support.
