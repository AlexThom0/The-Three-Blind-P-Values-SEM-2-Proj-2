While some advocate for a purely social model of disability, this perspective can overlook the complex interplay between biological realities and societal attitudes. For many, the lived experience includes navigating physical barriers and psychological challenges that cannot be entirely attributed to societal structures. It is crucial to recognize that the integration of personal narratives into the broader discourse on disability can illuminate these complexities. By embracing a multifaceted approach, we can better advocate for genuine understanding and support, acknowledging both the struggles and unique strengths that come from diverse lived realities.
