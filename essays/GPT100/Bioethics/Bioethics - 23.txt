This empiricist framework dismissed any notion of animal cognition as mere superstition, prioritizing observable behavior over subjective experience. As a result, the ethical considerations surrounding animal welfare were largely neglected, allowing mistreatment to proliferate. In this context, philosophers like Hobbes and Spinoza faced a formidable challenge in advocating for recognition of animal sentience, emphasizing the dissonance between scientific advancement and ethical responsibility. Their perspectives hinted at a growing rift in the philosophical landscape regarding the nature of life and consciousness.
