Moreover, the ecological impact of such fertilization is not entirely predictable; phytoplankton blooms can alter existing marine ecosystems, potentially leading to harmful algal blooms or disrupting local food webs. Researchers must therefore balance the immediate benefits of increased biomass with the long-term health of the oceanic environment. Understanding the responses of various marine organisms to this nutrient influx is crucial, as is assessing how these changes might influence carbon sequestration efforts in the broader context of climate change. The stakes could not be higher as we confront global warming.
