In addition to observational learning, the mechanisms through which insects process information might illuminate the cognitive parallels between these species. For instance, studies have indicated that certain neurochemical pathways in insects mirror those found in mammals, suggesting that the fundamentals of learning and memory may not be as unique to higher organisms as once thought. By examining the neural architecture of insects, researchers can better understand the evolution of cognitive abilities across species. This commonality challenges traditional distinctions between simple and complex brains, prompting a reevaluation of the cognitive capacities inherent in various forms of life.
