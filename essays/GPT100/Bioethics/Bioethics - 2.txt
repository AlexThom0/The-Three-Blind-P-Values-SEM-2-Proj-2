This nuanced understanding of risk prediction not only highlights the potential benefits of selecting embryos with lower disease susceptibility but also underscores the complexity of genetic interactions. Factors such as epigenetics and environmental influences further complicate predictions for both health and non-health traits. As researchers delve deeper into the human genome, the interplay of numerous genes—each contributing variably to a trait—becomes apparent. These intricacies challenge the notion that selection can reliably enhance outcomes beyond disease risk, emphasizing that while embryo selection may reduce disease likelihood, it does not straightforwardly translate to enhancements in broader characteristics or abilities.
