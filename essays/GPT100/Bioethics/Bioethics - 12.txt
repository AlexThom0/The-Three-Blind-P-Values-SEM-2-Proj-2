Advocates argued that, unlike the horrific practices of the past, modern technology could offer a chance to alleviate suffering without sacrificing moral integrity. These discussions often revolved around the distinction between therapeutic applications, aimed at preventing genetic disorders, and more contentious enhancements, such as selecting for traits deemed desirable. This nuanced dialogue was crucial in shaping public perception and policy surrounding genetic engineering.
