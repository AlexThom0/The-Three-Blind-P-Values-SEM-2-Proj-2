Modern practices, however, have evolved, often leading to more invasive procedures that remove significant portions of the foreskin and, in some cases, permanently alter the sensitivity and function of the penis. This raises ethical concerns about bodily autonomy and the capacity of infants to consent to such profound modifications. In this context, the comparison to female genital mutilation becomes more pronounced, challenging the societal norms that permit one while condemning the other based on cultural biases. The implications of these practices warrant a critical examination.
