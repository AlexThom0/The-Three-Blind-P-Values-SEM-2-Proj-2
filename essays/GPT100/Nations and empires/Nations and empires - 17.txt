The interplay of geographical boundaries and cultural identities among Armenians during this tumultuous period shaped a unique socio-political landscape. With the rise of modernity, transnational networks emerged, enabling not only the transfer of revolutionary ideas but also the forging of connections between dispersed communities. These networks facilitated communication and coordination among Armenian activists, who cherished their heritage while simultaneously engaging with broader movements for social and political change. As they navigated the complexities of their multi-imperial existence, their experiences enriched Armenian identity, blending traditional values with modern aspirations, thus redefining the contours of community and resistance in an age of uncertainty.
