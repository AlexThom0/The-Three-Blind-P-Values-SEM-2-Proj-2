This macabre obsession with anatomy was not merely a scientific inquiry; it was a moral justification for the further marginalization and oppression of Native Americans. The prevailing thought mischaracterized their lifestyle and values as primitive, thereby undermining their rights to land and sovereignty. As theories about race solidified, they reinforced existing prejudices, positioning Native Americans as obstacles to progress. This dehumanization fueled a brutal legacy that would shape policies well into the century, echoing the sentiments of those grappling with their belonging in a rapidly changing world.
