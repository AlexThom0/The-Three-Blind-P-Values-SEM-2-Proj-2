They sought to redefine Turkishness, embracing Western ideals while simultaneously reviving pre-Ottoman cultural elements as a means of asserting national pride. The reformers believed that by adopting modern, secular values, they could diminish the stigma associated with the ‘Terrible Turk’. This transformation was not merely superficial but aimed at fostering a sense of unity and progress within the new republic, challenging the entrenched narratives that had long characterized Turkic identity in the eyes of the West. The stakes were high, as they sought to reshape both domestic and international perceptions.
