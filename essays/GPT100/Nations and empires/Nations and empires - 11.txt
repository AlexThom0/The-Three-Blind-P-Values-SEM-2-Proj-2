This phenomenon was not merely a political fad; it reflected deeper societal anxieties about identity, security, and economic stability. Modi, like his European counterparts, tapped into a pervasive sense of dislocation, framing immigration and globalization as existential threats. The allure of asserting national pride resonated with voters weary of the complexities brought by multiculturalism. Such leaders often painted a stark dichotomy, situating their country’s revival against a backdrop of perceived threats, real or imagined, thus solidifying their power as defenders of the 'true' nation against all who were deemed outsiders.
