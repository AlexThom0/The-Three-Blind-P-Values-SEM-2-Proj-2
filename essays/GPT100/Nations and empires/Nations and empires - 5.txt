This backdrop starkly contrasted with the more pluralistic context of the Ottoman Empire, where Jews had a degree of communal autonomy. The coexistence of various religious groups fostered a different kind of interaction, enabling a framework where Jews could engage politically without the immediate threat of existential erasure. Such conditions shaped the discourse around identity and nationhood, complicating the very notion of Jewish self-determination in regions they had considered home for centuries.
