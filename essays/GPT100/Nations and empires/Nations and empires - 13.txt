In this context, the embrace of neoliberalism necessitated a delicate balancing act for Indian leaders, who sought to present economic growth while also invoking national pride. The narrative of modernization often co-opted traditional symbols of Indian identity, merging the aspirations of a burgeoning middle class with a broader, inclusive vision of national progress. This juxtaposition not only appealed to diverse demographics but also sought to reconcile deep-seated cultural values with the demands of a globalized economy. Consequently, the Indian government positioned itself as both a facilitator of foreign investment and a protector of national heritage, complicating the relationship between economic liberalism and nationalism.
