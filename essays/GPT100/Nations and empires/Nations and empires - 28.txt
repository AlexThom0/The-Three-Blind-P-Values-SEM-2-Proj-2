Intellectuals and politicians alike began leveraging racial theories to assert cultural superiority. The application of “Anglo-Saxonism” in this milieu served to reinforce not only the perceived dichotomy between empires but also to establish a moral justification for imperial expansion. The portrayal of Anglo-Saxons as the pinnacle of civilization positioned them as the rightful leaders of global progress, further entrenching the notion that cultural and racial hierarchies were natural and divinely ordained in the eyes of many proponents.
