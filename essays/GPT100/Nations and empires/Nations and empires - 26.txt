The strategic significance of arms manufacturing extended beyond mere commercial gain; it was also a means of exerting control over local rulers and militias. The intricate networks of production fostered not only skilled artisans but also a burgeoning black market for weapons, complicating EIC's attempts to monopolize the trade. This environment facilitated not just the proliferation of arms but also the intertwining of power dynamics, as local economies became reliant on both Indian and British weaponry. The interplay between local craftsmanship and European demand shaped the military landscape of the subcontinent.
