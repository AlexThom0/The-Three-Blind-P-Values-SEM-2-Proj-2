This rejection was emblematic of a broader movement among educated Indians who began to see these honours not as marks of success, but as symbols of colonial subjugation. As nationalist sentiments grew, many viewed participation in the honours system as contrary to their aspirations for self-governance. Figures like Gandhi and Tagore articulated a vision for India that was fundamentally at odds with the notion of hierarchical accolades imposed by a foreign power. Their resistance reflected a shift from seeking recognition through colonial structures to establishing a new identity rooted in independence.
