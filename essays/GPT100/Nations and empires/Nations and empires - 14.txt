Fukuzawa's perspective emphasizes the importance of cultural identity amidst the allure of Western advancements. He believed that mere imitation would dilute Japan's rich heritage and distinct values. Instead, he advocated for a thoughtful engagement, encouraging Japanese citizens to adapt select practices from the West that could enhance their own society, while remaining anchored in their traditions. This approach was not about wholesale adoption but rather about discernment and critical evaluation. By defining a unique path forward, Japan could selectively incorporate innovative ideas without losing its essence, forging a future that harmoniously blended aspects of both cultures.
