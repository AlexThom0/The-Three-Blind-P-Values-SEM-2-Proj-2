Dennett's comparison illuminates how our brains generate a similar user illusion regarding consciousness, presenting a cohesive experience while the intricate workings of thought and perception remain largely hidden. Just as the computer’s operations yield a façade of simplicity, our subjective experience of agency masks the complexity of neuronal interactions, which operate beneath the surface of conscious awareness. Thus, the conviction of self-directed intention may be as much a construct as the interface we engage with.
