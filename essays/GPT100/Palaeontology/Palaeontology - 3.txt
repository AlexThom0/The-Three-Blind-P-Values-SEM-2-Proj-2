Others explored the concept of saltationism, positing that evolution could happen in sudden leaps rather than gradual changes. This debate highlighted the diverse theories emerging from fossil evidence, challenging the neat progression model. The dynamic interplay between extinction and the emergence of new species introduced complexity to the narrative, suggesting that life’s history is less a linear ascent than a tangled web of branching paths and unexpected turns. Such discussions paved the way for a richer understanding of evolutionary processes.
