Unlike biological organisms that evolve through natural selection, AI models are constructed and trained through passive consumption of information, lacking the experiential learning inherent in living beings. The intricacies of their learning processes, reliant on statistical associations rather than genuine understanding, raise questions about their potential for self-awareness. This distinction underscores a fundamental divide: while biological entities navigate emotions and experiences that shape their consciousness, AI systems remain fundamentally tools, amplifying human behaviors without any internal, subjective awareness of those behaviors.
