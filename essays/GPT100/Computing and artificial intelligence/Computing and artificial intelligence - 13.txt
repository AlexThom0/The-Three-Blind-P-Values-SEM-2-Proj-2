This gap between understanding and reliance on technology reflects a broader cultural trend where convenience often outweighs mastery. As society evolves, the emphasis shifts to systems designed for accessibility, like automatic transmissions, which allow users to engage with technology without fully grasping its mechanics. This disconnection could lead to a passive acceptance of technology's decisions, diminishing our ability to question or challenge them. Just as a driver who learns only automatic might struggle with deeper vehicular knowledge, individuals who bypass coding may miss out on critical insights into the algorithms shaping our lives. Mastery fosters empowerment, bridging the gap between user and machine.
