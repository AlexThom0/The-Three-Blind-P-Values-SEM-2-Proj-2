However, such simplifications raise critical questions about the completeness of these models. Does the omission of glial cells, which play essential roles in neuronal health and communication, risk overlooking vital aspects of cognitive processes? If the intent is to replicate consciousness, merely mapping neuronal connections may not suffice; the supporting frameworks and biochemical interactions that underpin these connections could be equally important. Understanding the interplay between neurons and glia may reveal more about the complexities of thought and behavior.
