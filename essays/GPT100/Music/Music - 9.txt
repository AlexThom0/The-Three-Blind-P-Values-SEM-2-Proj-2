Unlike visual arts that can provide concrete imagery, music’s suggestive quality opens a space for personal reflection and insight, fostering a unique connection between the performer and the audience. As this dialogue unfolds, each listener becomes an active participant, drawing from their experiences and feelings. This mutual engagement reinforces the idea that while music lacks definitive representation, it thrives in its capacity to elicit a spectrum of responses, enriching the listening experience.
