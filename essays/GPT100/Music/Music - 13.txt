Cohen’s approach to songwriting reflects a broader tension between personal experience and formal religious doctrine. His lyrics often blur the lines between sacred and secular, inviting listeners into a contemplative space that transcends orthodoxy. This fusion can be seen in the way he uses biblical imagery not merely as a reference but as a canvas on which to project his own struggles with faith and doubt. Each iteration of ‘Hallelujah’ reveals layers of meaning, inviting introspection and interpretation that resonate with a diverse audience. Such complexity reinforces the idea that spirituality can be deeply personal and elude easy categorization.
