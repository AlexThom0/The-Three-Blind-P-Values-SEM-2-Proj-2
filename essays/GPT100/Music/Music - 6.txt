By utilizing a well-tempered approach, he created a framework in which each prelude and fugue could reflect the unique emotional and harmonic qualities of its specific key. This method allowed for a richer interplay of consonance and dissonance, encouraging expressive performances that highlight the nuanced relationships among pitches. The very architecture of the works embodies a dialogue between tradition and innovation, revealing Bach's deep understanding of both theoretical principles and the practical realities of music-making in his time.
