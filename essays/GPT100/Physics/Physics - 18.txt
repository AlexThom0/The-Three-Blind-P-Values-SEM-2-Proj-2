This preference for teleological explanations contrasts sharply with the atomistic views proposed by Democritus, who posited that the universe operates through random interactions of indivisible particles. Such radical materialism introduced a framework that stripped away the notion of intrinsic purpose, challenging the philosophical norms of the time. By prioritizing observable phenomena and natural laws over metaphysical speculations, Democritus effectively paved the way for a scientific approach that would later flourish during the Enlightenment. His ideas forced subsequent thinkers to grapple with the implications of a universe devoid of predetermined pathways, thereby influencing the trajectory of Western philosophy and science for generations.
