The implications of dark matter extend beyond mere absence of light. Its gravitational influence affects the motion of galaxies and galaxy clusters, suggesting a vast, unseen scaffolding that shapes cosmic architecture. Observations indicate that dark matter forms a web-like structure, influencing the distribution of ordinary matter throughout the Universe. This gravitational pull might also provide clues to the mysteries surrounding galaxy formation and evolution. Researchers theorize that understanding dark matter could open doors to insights about other fundamental forces and components, potentially shedding light on the very fabric of reality, which remains tantalizingly elusive.
