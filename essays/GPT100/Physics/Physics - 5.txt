Similarly, in complex biological systems, constraints emerge not only from physical laws but also from environmental interactions and biochemical pathways. These constraints influence how proteins fold and how neurons fire, suggesting that while fundamental equations govern behavior at the micro level, the emergent properties at the macro level introduce a form of unpredictability. This interplay raises fundamental questions about agency, pushing us to consider whether true autonomy can exist within such a tightly regulated framework of physical and biochemical interactions.
