Dewey’s approach emphasized that the same cognitive skills employed in everyday tasks could be harnessed for more complex intellectual endeavors. This perspective invited educators to consider the continuity between ordinary and academic thought, suggesting that teaching should not merely focus on abstract concepts but also connect to students’ lived experiences. By integrating practical problem-solving into educational practices, Dewey sought to cultivate a more engaged and reflective learner. His emphasis on experiential learning challenged traditional pedagogical methods, advocating for an educational framework that would not only foster critical thinking but also empower students to navigate real-world challenges effectively.
