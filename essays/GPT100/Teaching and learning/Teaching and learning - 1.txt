As educators began to explore different cognitive frameworks, the idea that learners have distinct preferences gained traction, leading to an oversimplification of complex learning processes. This movement coincided with an increasing push for differentiation in classrooms, as teachers sought innovative ways to engage diverse learners. Consequently, learning styles became a convenient, if flawed, heuristic for addressing individual needs, obscuring the more effective strategies grounded in evidence-based practices.
