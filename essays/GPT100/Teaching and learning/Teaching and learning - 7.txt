This phenomenon of neural reorganization demonstrates the brain's remarkable adaptability at various stages of life, but it raises profound questions about the implications for treatment and rehabilitation in humans. For individuals with visual impairments or those recovering from injuries, knowing when and how the brain can compensate offers critical insights. Therapeutic interventions could be designed to harness these windows of plasticity, optimizing the timing and nature of stimuli to encourage recovery. Such strategies could redefine approaches to visual rehabilitation and enhance the quality of life for many.
