For instance, individuals or societies that have faced recurrent scams often devise strategies to recognize patterns of deceit and establish protective measures. Over time, these learned behaviors forge a cultural resilience, allowing communities to share information about past threats and collectively enhance their defenses against future attempts. Like biological immunity, this social adaptation facilitates a more robust response to predators, whether they manifest as viruses or con artists, thereby reinforcing the importance of collective awareness and knowledge sharing.
