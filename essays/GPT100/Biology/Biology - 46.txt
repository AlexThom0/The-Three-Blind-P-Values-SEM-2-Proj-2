This perception fosters a belief that if we do not intervene swiftly, irreversible harm will befall not only the Earth but also our immediate future. This lens narrows the focus of environmental issues, making them personal and urgent. Such framing may obscure the interconnectedness of ecosystems and the long-term processes of environmental change, simplifying complex discussions into a series of urgent mandates. In doing so, we risk overlooking the need for more nuanced, sustained engagement with the world around us.
