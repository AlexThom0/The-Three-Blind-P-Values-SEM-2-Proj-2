The wartime propaganda often used imagery of native species to evoke feelings of resilience and unity among citizens, transforming these animals into symbols of defiance against the enemy. This not only reinforced the cultural significance of British wildlife but also blurred the lines between national identity and the natural world, compelling people to reassess their relationship with local fauna. As Knight and others highlighted the intrinsic value of native species, the narrative surrounding wildlife became inseparable from larger themes of patriotism and moral fortitude.
