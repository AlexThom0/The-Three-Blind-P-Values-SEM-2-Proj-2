These events underscore the harsh reality that the act of rehabilitation does not guarantee survival in the wild. For every success story, countless others face the challenges of re-acclimatization, territorial disputes, or simply finding food. Often, the efforts to restore these animals to a natural state overlook crucial factors, such as their learned behaviors and the environmental threats that persist in their habitats. This disconnect can lead to tragic outcomes, revealing the complexities surrounding wildlife rehabilitation and the inherent risks involved in such interventions.
