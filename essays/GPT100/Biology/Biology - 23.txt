As new technologies emerge, they often bring unforeseen consequences, reshaping not only industries but also societal structures and human behavior. Each leap forward can amplify existing disparities or introduce entirely new challenges, while the solutions crafted in response can become new sources of contention. In many cases, societies grapple with the ethical implications of these technologies long after their introduction, often only realizing the extent of their impact in hindsight. The cyclical nature of this evolution reveals a pattern: as we chase progress, we must also confront the complexities it inevitably unfolds.
