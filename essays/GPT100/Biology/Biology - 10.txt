The unpredictability of behaviour highlights the interplay between genetic predisposition and environmental influence. While genes lay the groundwork for certain traits, it is the organism’s interaction with its surroundings that shapes decision-making and adaptability. For instance, social animals often modify their behaviours based on the dynamics of group interactions, suggesting that agency is not only inherent but also context-dependent. Consider the strategies employed by ravens, which adjust their actions based on previous experiences and the behaviours of their peers. This adaptability empowers them to navigate complex challenges, emphasizing that agency extends beyond mere biological determinants.
