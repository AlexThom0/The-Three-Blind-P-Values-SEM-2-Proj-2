Instead, the ability to extract and analyze markers from baleen presents a non-invasive means of interpreting long-term physiological changes. By examining these structures, scientists can gain insights into an animal's diet, migratory patterns, and exposure to ecological shifts or contaminants over time. This innovative approach not only enhances our understanding of whale biology but also underscores the broader implications of using keratin-based tissue as a historical record of an animal's environmental interactions and health. Such findings could illuminate trends that are crucial for conservation efforts.
