Instead of being a cohesive unit capable of undergoing natural selection, the biosphere functions more like a complex web of interactions, where individual species thrive independently. This dissociation highlights the distinction between levels of selection; while individual organisms can evolve through their own reproductive success, the overarching community does not conform to the same rules. The rich diversity and intricate relationships among species suggest that the dynamics of selection operate differently when viewed through the lens of community and ecosystem interactions, adding layers of complexity to our understanding of evolution.
