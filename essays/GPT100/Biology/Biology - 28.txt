As RNA proliferated, it likely experienced mutations and evolutionary pressures that favored more stable structures, leading to the emergence of DNA. This transition illustrates a significant shift in biological complexity, as DNA not only improved genetic stability but also facilitated more efficient means of information storage and transfer. The formation of proteins from these nucleic acids further enhanced the versatility of life, enabling organisms to adapt to diverse environments. The interplay between these molecular developments marks a pivotal moment in the narrative of life's evolution, paving the way for the intricate web of biological systems that would later dominate the planet.
