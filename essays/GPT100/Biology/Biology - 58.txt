Proponents like Lowell created an elaborate narrative that captivated the public imagination, blending dreams of advanced civilizations with a romantic vision of extraterrestrial life. Nevertheless, this vision ignored fundamental geological and atmospheric realities. Geological studies of Mars revealed a landscape shaped by processes vastly different from those on Earth. The enthusiasm for Martian canals became emblematic of a broader trend in science: the tension between observation and interpretation, where human imagination sometimes overshadows empirical evidence. Thus, the very question of life on Mars evolved, paving the way for modern astrobiology.
