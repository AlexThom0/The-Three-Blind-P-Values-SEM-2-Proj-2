the complex relationship between humans and insects has deep roots in both historical context and cultural narratives. Insects have often been portrayed as demons or agents of divine wrath, reinforcing negative perceptions that permeate various societies. This duality can elucidate why particular species evoke fear or distaste—ranging from the biblical descriptions of swarms to the pestilence associated with their presence. The fear of insects extends beyond theology into folklore and agriculture, where they are seen as both vital for ecosystems and threats to livelihood. Understanding this dichotomy helps unpack the societal implications of our longstanding animosity towards these creatures.
