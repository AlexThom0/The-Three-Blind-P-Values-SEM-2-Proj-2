This paradigm shift led thinkers to prioritize empirical observation over teleological explanations, casting aside the notion of inherent life forces directed by a divine architect. As mechanistic models gained prominence, they framed the universe as a collection of parts that could be understood through mathematics and physical laws. Yet, even in this new worldview, echoes of Aristotle’s final causes persisted, revealing a tension between the emerging scientific rigor and the age-old quest for meaning in the natural world. Exploration of this tension highlights the complexities and paradoxes of scientific thought.
