The hippocampus plays a crucial role not only in forming new memories but also in retrieving those long-standing, olfactory-associated ones. This relationship underscores its significance in encoding experiences tied to the senses, particularly smell. When specific scents trigger memories, the hippocampus connects these sensory inputs to emotional states and contextual details, reinforcing the idea that our past experiences are intricately woven into the fabric of our neural architecture. This deep-rooted bond between odour and recollection is likely why mere whiffs can transport individuals back in time, eliciting vivid images and feelings from years gone by.
