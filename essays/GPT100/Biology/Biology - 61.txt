A similar dynamic appears in the development of antibiotic resistance, where even minor mutations impart significant advantages to bacteria. As these resistant strains proliferate, they outcompete their counterparts, dramatically altering the landscape of infectious disease. The speed at which these adaptations occur illustrates a crucial aspect of evolution, showing that while we may perceive the processes of change as gradual over time, certain conditions can catalyze rapid advancements, making the consequences of our interventions increasingly apparent. Understanding this can illuminate future approaches to managing health threats.
