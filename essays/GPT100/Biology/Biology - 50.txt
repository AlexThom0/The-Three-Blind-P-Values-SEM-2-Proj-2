the growing body of research highlights various external factors that contribute to obesity and weight management beyond individual choices. Environmental influences such as the availability of high-calorie foods, socioeconomic status, and marketing strategies have all been shown to play significant roles in shaping dietary habits. Additionally, genetic predispositions can complicate the equation, intertwining biology with lifestyle decisions. This multifaceted perspective challenges the oversimplified notion that personal responsibility alone dictates weight outcomes, urging a broader examination of societal and structural influences on health. Understanding obesity necessitates acknowledging these complex interactions rather than attributing blame solely to individual behavior.
