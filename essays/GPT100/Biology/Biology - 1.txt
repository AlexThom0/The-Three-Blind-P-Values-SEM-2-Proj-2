In many societies, the directions we assign to time—past, present, future—are influenced by prevalent cultural narratives. For instance, in cultures where community and tradition hold significant value, time may be perceived as a shared journey rather than a personal pursuit. This collective view fosters a relationship with time that prioritizes events occurring in harmony rather than in isolation, further shaping individuals' metaphors.
