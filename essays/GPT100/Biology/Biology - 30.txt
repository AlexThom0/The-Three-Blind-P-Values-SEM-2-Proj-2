Their isolation fosters unique adaptations, yet this same seclusion can render them vulnerable. The introduction of non-native species can rapidly upset the delicate balance, leading to cascading effects throughout the food web. This vulnerability highlights the importance of habitat protection and restoration efforts specific to island ecosystems. Understanding these dynamics not only aids in conserving current populations but also emphasizes the need for proactive measures to ensure the survival of endemic species in the face of mounting environmental pressures.
