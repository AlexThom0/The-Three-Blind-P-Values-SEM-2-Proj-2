As technology encroaches on daily life, it reshapes not only individual habits but also societal norms. The pervasive presence of smartphones creates an environment where disengagement from the physical world is not only accepted but expected. Social interactions are increasingly mediated through screens, leading to a diminished capacity for real-life communication and presence. This shift in behavior normalizes a state of distraction, where people feel more connected online while becoming increasingly isolated offline. The implications of this cultural change extend beyond personal habits, influencing educational systems, work environments, and community interactions, ultimately questioning our relationship with both technology and each other.
