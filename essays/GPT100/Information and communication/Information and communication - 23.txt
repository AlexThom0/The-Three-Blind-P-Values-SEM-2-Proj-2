As historians increasingly concentrated on microhistories, the risk emerged of losing sight of the larger narrative that contextualizes those smaller stories. The ability to engage with sweeping historical arcs, once a hallmark of the discipline, diminished alongside the rise of rigid methodological constraints. This shift not only altered the landscape of historical inquiry but also affected how future scholars approached the vast tapestry of human experience.
