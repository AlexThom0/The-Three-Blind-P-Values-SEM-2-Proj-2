This preference for a more restrictive notion of nature may reflect a desire to connect with an idealized version of the world, free from the complexities introduced by human influence. Environmental psychologists often emphasize the benefits of untouched landscapes for mental well-being, advocating for a return to pristine environments as a remedy for modern stresses. Yet, one must consider how our interactions with urban spaces and technology also shape our psychological frameworks. Acknowledging the relationship between nature and human innovation can lead to a richer understanding of what constitutes our environments and how they impact our psychological experiences.
