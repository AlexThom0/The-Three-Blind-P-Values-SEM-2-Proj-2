Eyal’s four-step model, consisting of trigger, action, variable reward, and investment, is meticulously designed to foster habit formation. By strategically employing triggers, whether external, like notifications, or internal, such as feelings of boredom, designers ensure users are continuously nudged toward engagement. This psychological manipulation is further amplified by the allure of variable rewards, which injects an element of excitement and anticipation. Users become conditioned to return, seeking the next rewarding experience. The business model thrives not just on users’ engagement but on their emotional entanglement, which is particularly evident when examining platforms designed to hold attention indefinitely.
