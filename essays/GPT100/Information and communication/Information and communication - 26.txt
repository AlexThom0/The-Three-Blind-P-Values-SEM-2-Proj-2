In a society where self-worth is often measured against the achievements of others, Rousseau’s vision of children raised free from the taint of amour-propre presents a compelling yet complex challenge. While fostering independence from external validation is vital, it raises questions about the role of healthy competition and recognition in personal development. Children need to be equipped not just to avoid envy, but to understand and appreciate their unique contributions in relation to others. This balance could encourage resilience and motivation, allowing them to navigate a world that inherently involves comparison while also cherishing their own worth.
