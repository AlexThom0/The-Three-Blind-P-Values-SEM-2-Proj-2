This conundrum leads physicists to explore alternative interpretations that seek to provide a more coherent understanding of reality. One such approach is the Many-Worlds Interpretation, which posits that all possible outcomes of a quantum event actually occur, each in its own branching universe. In this view, there is no collapse; instead, every possibility unfolds, thereby preserving the probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics without introducing the awkwardness of measurement-induced collapse. While this theory elegantly sidesteps the Measurement Problem, it also raises profound questions about the nature of existence and observation, challenging the boundaries of what we consider to be real.
