The Standard Model effectively explains and predicts a wide range of particle interactions, despite lacking a complete understanding of gravity or the unification of forces. This reliance on empirically adequate frameworks raises important questions about the nature of scientific progress. Can science truly advance if it operates within a realm of provisional theories? While these models are successful in a practical sense, they may deliver only a partial view of reality. Ultimately, this perspective challenges the traditional notion that scientific success is synonymous with truth.
