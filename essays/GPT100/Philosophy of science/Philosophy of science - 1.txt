This tactic served to undermine legitimate discussions about the limitations of scientific inquiry, suggesting that dissent equated to ignorance or anti-intellectualism. By framing scientism as merely a reaction to scientific overreach, these scholars failed to engage with the nuanced perspectives brought forth by both science and the humanities. Such a dismissal risks stifling fruitful dialogue and exploration of how different disciplines can enrich one another, fostering a more interdisciplinary approach that acknowledges both science's strengths and its boundaries.
