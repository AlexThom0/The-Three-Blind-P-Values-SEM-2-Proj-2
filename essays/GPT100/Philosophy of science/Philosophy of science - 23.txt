This approach emphasizes the importance of context and interaction across different levels of analysis, acknowledging that reductionism can overlook the emergent properties of complex systems. Rather than viewing disciplines as a hierarchy, it is crucial to recognize the ways in which cultural phenomena are shaped not just by biological factors but by historical, social, and psychological contexts. Such interconnections illuminate how human behavior cannot be fully understood through any single lens. Exploring these dimensions allows for a richer interpretation of how diverse elements in culture interact and evolve.
