In quantum mechanics, particles can exist in multiple states simultaneously, meaning that even if we know the final state, the path taken to reach that state can be obscured. This inherent uncertainty mirrors our experience with incomplete information in everyday life. Just as one might assume a friend delivered flowers based on their past behavior, we can assign probabilities to various particle interactions based on their characteristics and expected behaviors, thereby allowing us to construct plausible narratives even in the absence of a definitive sequence of events.
