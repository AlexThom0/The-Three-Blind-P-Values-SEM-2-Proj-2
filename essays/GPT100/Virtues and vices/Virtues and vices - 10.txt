The Loving Saint, through an unwavering commitment to ethical principles, is portrayed as blissfully ignorant of the richness of non-moral experiences. This seemingly effortless happiness raises questions about the nature of fulfillment. Conversely, while the Rational Saint might sacrifice personal joy for a sense of obligation, her awareness of what she lacks could lead to an underlying tension and dissatisfaction. This conflict reveals deeper implications for understanding morality in relation to personal well-being, challenging the notion that ethical living must wholly exclude other values.
