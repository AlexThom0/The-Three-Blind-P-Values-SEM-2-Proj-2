No longer did sufferers refer to their anguish in the context of divine punishment or existential trials; instead, they spoke in the clinical terminologies of medicine. Pain management replaced spiritual solace, resulting in a more mechanical understanding of suffering. Patients became consumers of pain relief, navigating healthcare systems to find the right medications and therapies. This evolution in discourse signifies a broader cultural shift, where the personal narrative of suffering is overshadowed by the impersonal intervention of science and technology, altering both the experience and expression of pain in profound ways.
