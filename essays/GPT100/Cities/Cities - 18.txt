Urban planning often prioritizes vehicular efficiency over the rights and safety of pedestrians, implicitly suggesting that those who venture into traffic at unmarked areas bear the responsibility for their own misfortune. This perspective not only dehumanizes individuals like Nelson but also reinforces a culture where critical infrastructure for walking is sorely lacking. As a result, the myth of freedom associated with car culture starkly contrasts with the lived reality of many citizens simply trying to reach their destinations.
