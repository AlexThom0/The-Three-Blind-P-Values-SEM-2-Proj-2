Unlike the village customs deeply rooted in tradition, city dwellers often navigate a complex blend of modern influences and ancestral rituals. This interplay creates a unique environment where traditional beliefs about death coexist and sometimes clash with contemporary practices, leading to varying degrees of acceptance and adaptation within urban communities.
