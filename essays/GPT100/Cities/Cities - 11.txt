Buildings, streets, and park layouts are not neutral backdrops but are imbued with meanings shaped by cultural narratives and historical contexts. Each intersection, each public space, carries a weight of intention and social dynamics that can affect how individuals feel and behave. Dismissal of this complexity leads to an unexamined acceptance of the urban setting as mere scenery, obscuring the rich tapestry of interactions it fosters. Thus, the challenge lies not merely in recognizing these elements but in actively deciphering the stories they tell.
