Berman illustrates the irony that while cities are designed to facilitate movement and progress, they can simultaneously entrap marginalized populations within cycles of decay and neglect. This dissonance brought to light my own experiences and memories of urban disinvestment, resulting in a growing awareness of how infrastructure decisions ripple through time. The stark reality of pollution and dislocation makes it evident that such planning practices are not merely theoretical; they have devastating, tangible impacts on lives within these affected neighborhoods.
