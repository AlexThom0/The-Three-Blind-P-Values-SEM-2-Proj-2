Initial experimentation involved the selective cultivation of naturally occurring plants, leading to a gradual understanding of their growth cycles and requirements. This process was rife with trial and error, as communities learned to manipulate their environment under the pressures of changing climates and fluctuating food availability. Early farmers might have viewed their efforts as a means to secure their food sources, but the eventual dependence on cultivated crops was not instantly apparent. Rather, it emerged slowly, as societies began to weigh the immediate benefits against the more labor-intensive demands of agriculture, marking a pivotal shift in human dietary practices and lifestyle.
