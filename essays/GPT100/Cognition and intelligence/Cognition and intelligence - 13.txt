It suggested a deterministic view of human potential, where socioeconomic factors and cultural contexts were entirely overshadowed by supposed biological imperatives. As the psychological testing movement gained momentum, it became increasingly entangled with notions of racial superiority, leading to policies that emphasized segregation rather than inclusion. This troubling legacy of intertwining intelligence assessment with social Darwinism shaped educational systems and societal structures for decades, echoing through public discourse on meritocracy and capability.
