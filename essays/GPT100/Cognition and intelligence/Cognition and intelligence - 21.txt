This phenomenon, often referred to as training specificity, raises questions about the methods of translating skills learned in one context to other domains of cognitive functioning. Despite rigorous training regimens, individuals show minimal transfer of skills, suggesting that targeted practice may only enhance performance in closely related tasks. The implications for educational techniques and cognitive rehabilitation programs are profound, as they highlight a need for integrated approaches that build transferable skills rather than isolated task proficiency. Understanding the boundaries of cognitive training is crucial for developing effective interventions.
