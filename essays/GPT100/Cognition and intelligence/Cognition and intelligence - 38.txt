This distinction underscores a broader societal tendency to correlate emotional depth with physical presence. For instance, while entities like corporations are seen as rational decision-makers, their lack of a physical form renders them devoid of the emotional complexity that humans perceive in one another. This disconnection raises questions about the implications of attributing agency to non-human entities, pushing us to consider how our intuitions shape not only our understanding of consciousness but also our interactions with various forms of intelligence.
