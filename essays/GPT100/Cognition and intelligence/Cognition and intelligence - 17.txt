Kliger’s quiet revelation served as a chilling reminder of how deeply personal experiences inform one’s understanding of systemic oppression. It highlighted the psychological mechanisms by which individuals might surrender their agency and conform to authoritarian dictates. This interplay between individual psychology and collective behavior became increasingly significant as I delved deeper into theories explaining the dynamics of control and submission.
