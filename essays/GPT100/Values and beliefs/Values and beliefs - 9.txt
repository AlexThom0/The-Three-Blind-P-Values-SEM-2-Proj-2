the transition from a broadly inclusive humanist outlook to a more rigid, self-defined Humanism raises critical questions about belief and dogma. While traditional Humanism promotes a spirit of inquiry and respect for diverse perspectives, the modern iteration often adopts a dismissive stance towards those who hold differing views. This shift can lead to an insular community that prioritizes orthodoxy over open dialogue. As Humanism evolves, it risks becoming a belief system itself, bound by the same constraints it seeks to critique. Such a transformation challenges the very essence of what it means to champion human understanding and freedom.
