Intensification of livestock production not only amplifies the dependency on external feed sources but also exacerbates the environmental repercussions tied to large-scale agricultural practices. As traditional mixed systems are pushed aside, the resulting monocultures diminish biodiversity and soil health, leading to a more fragile ecosystem. The reliance on synthetic inputs further complicates the scenario, creating a cycle where the need for pesticides and fertilizers grows alongside livestock output. This unsustainable model prioritizes short-term gains over long-term stewardship, threatening the resilience of farming communities and the integrity of food systems. Sustainable solutions must integrate diverse practices that enhance local ecosystems.
