This framing not only narrows the discourse but also positions citizens as passive recipients of scientific knowledge rather than active participants in the conversation. By prioritizing technical expertise, the public can feel disempowered, inadvertently diminishing their ability to influence policy and advocate for substantial change. Moreover, such a focus on scientific authority can mask the underlying social, economic, and ethical dimensions of climate change, which demand a more inclusive dialogue. Without addressing these factors, efforts at grassroots mobilization risk becoming limited to superficial actions, deviating from the broader systemic changes essential to effectively combat climate change.
