Communities often defined their survival through collective management rather than isolation through privatization. In many Indigenous societies, for instance, land is viewed not as a commodity but as a living entity that must be cared for and respected. This perspective fosters a deep-rooted sense of responsibility, creating a balance between human needs and ecological health. The dynamics of these relationships illustrate that the wellbeing of individuals is intricately connected to the health of their shared environment, presenting a compelling case for re-evaluating modern notions of ownership.
