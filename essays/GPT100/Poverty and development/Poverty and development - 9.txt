As oil interests intensify, land previously used for herding is being repurposed for extraction and related industries. This encroachment disrupts not only the migration patterns of pastoralists but also their cultural connection to the land. With fewer grazing areas available, families that have relied on their livestock for generations are confronted with stark choices: adapt or perish. In this volatile environment, the balance between tradition and modernity is precarious, leaving communities vulnerable as they navigate a future increasingly dictated by external economic forces.
