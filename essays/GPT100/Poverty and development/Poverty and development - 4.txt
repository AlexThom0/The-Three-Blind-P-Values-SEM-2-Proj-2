The value of RCTs lies in their ability to provide clear, empirical evidence which policymakers can rely upon to allocate resources effectively. However, the interplay between academic rigor and practical application presents challenges, as researchers often grapple with ensuring that findings translate into real-world solutions. As these methodologies gain traction, ongoing reflection on their limitations and potential biases becomes essential to maintain the integrity of the evidence they produce.
