This behavioral approach aims to create a clear distinction between acceptable and unacceptable actions, reinforcing the need for attendance as a non-negotiable expectation. However, this method often overlooks the underlying emotional and psychological factors contributing to school refusal. Children may be grappling with anxiety, depression, or trauma, and merely enforcing attendance without addressing these issues can lead to increased resistance and resentment. Understanding the root causes of school refusal is crucial in developing a more empathetic and comprehensive strategy that fosters long-term engagement with education.
