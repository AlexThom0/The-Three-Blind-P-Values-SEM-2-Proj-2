This reinterpretation of classical texts exposed the stark contrasts between the ideals of democracy celebrated by the elite and the harsh realities experienced by the marginalized. Engaging with these works allowed working-class readers to find resonance in their own struggles, transforming ancient narratives into tools for contemporary political and social critique. As they connected their plight with those of historical figures, they began to envision a future that aligned more closely with the principles of equality and justice espoused by these classical thinkers.
