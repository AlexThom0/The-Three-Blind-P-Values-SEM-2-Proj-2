This shift towards quantification not only redefined the nature of scholarly inquiry but also aligned academic pursuits with disciplinary standards increasingly influenced by external funding sources and institutional policies. The scholar's role transformed from a solitary thinker to one who navigated a complex network of partnerships and funding applications, further embedding them into the bureaucratic machinery of academia. As universities expanded and diversified their missions, the very essence of what it meant to engage in scholarship began to reflect the demands of society and the market, rather than the intrinsic pursuit of knowledge.
