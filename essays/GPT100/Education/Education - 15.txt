Most institutions operated under minimal oversight, leading to variances in academic standards and curricula that further blurred the line between genuine education and vocational training. As faculties often lacked advanced degrees, their capacity to provide a rigorous intellectual environment was limited. This resulted in a paradox where the sheer number of colleges suggested an abundance of educational opportunity, yet the quality of education remained suspect. Students frequently opted for college as a means of social mobility without a clear understanding of its true benefits, complicating the narrative around higher education's value in American society.
