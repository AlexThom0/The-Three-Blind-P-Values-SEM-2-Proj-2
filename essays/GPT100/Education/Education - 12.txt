This deluge of accolades serves a dual purpose: it feeds the institutional need for prestige while providing individuals with a tangible sense of accomplishment. Yet, the value of these awards often feels transient, as they hang upon the walls of an office or lay forgotten in a drawer, ultimately leading to the question of what constitutes genuine merit in the academic landscape. The increasingly competitive environment fosters an obsession with accolades, transforming intellectual pursuits into a series of checkboxes rather than a holistic educational mission.
