This trend has transformed the pursuit of knowledge into a form of financial investment, driven by the narrative that degrees equate to higher earning potential. As a result, educational institutions increasingly emphasize outcomes over intellectual curiosity, with curricula shaped more by labor market demands than by the intrinsic merit of the subjects themselves. The shift towards quantifiable success in education parallels the commercialization seen in other domains, where the emphasis on practical skills can overshadow the broader, enriching aspects of learning.
