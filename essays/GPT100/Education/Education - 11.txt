This discrepancy in backgrounds creates an environment that can be alienating for first-generation students. The expectation to navigate a culture marked by privilege and historical legacy can lead to feelings of inadequacy and isolation. Many first-generation students must simultaneously adapt to a competitive academic atmosphere and confront the pressures of their familial and community obligations. This dual burden might manifest in lower levels of engagement and academic performance, further widening the gap. Understanding these dynamics is crucial for developing targeted support systems, enabling equitable access to opportunities and resources that can empower these students to thrive in environments that were not designed with them in mind.
