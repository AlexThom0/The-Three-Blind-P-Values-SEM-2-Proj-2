If machines are programmed to filter content based on learned biases, the question becomes: who is responsible for the moral judgments made by these systems? The potential for errors in interpretation could lead to unjust consequences, amplifying societal biases rather than alleviating them. Just as the self-driving car's choices hinge on ethical frameworks, so too do the algorithms we employ to curate our online experiences. This intersection of technology and morality necessitates a careful examination of the values we instill in our digital tools.
