The relentless drive for profit pushed many into deplorable conditions, where exploitation was rife, and voices for reform began to emerge. Labor movements gained momentum, advocating for rights and better conditions, awakening a collective consciousness among workers. This burgeoning sense of solidarity countered the bleak predictions of both Malthus and Marx, revealing that change was not solely dependent on crisis. Instead, it demonstrated the potential for organized resistance against oppressive systems, laying the groundwork for future negotiations that would reshape labor relations and societal structures.
