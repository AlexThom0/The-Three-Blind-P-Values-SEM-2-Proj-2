This emphasis on cuteness influences not only consumer choices but also breeding practices. Breeders, responding to market demands, often prioritize aesthetics over health, leading to a troubling cycle where physical traits are exaggerated at the expense of the dog's well-being. This aesthetic-driven approach raises ethical concerns as breeders and pet owners alike may overlook important considerations such as temperament, health, and the suitability of the breed for individual lifestyles. The pressure to conform to trends leaves many potential dog owners unaware of the implications of their choices, creating a disconnection between desire and responsibility.
