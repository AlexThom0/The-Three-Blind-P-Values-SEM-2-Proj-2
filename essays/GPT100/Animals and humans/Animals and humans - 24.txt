It became apparent that the bonds they form with their owners significantly influence their reactions to perceived threats or competition. These connections are enriched through daily interactions, shaping dogs’ responses to rivals. Additionally, neuroimaging studies in other species suggest that similar mechanisms may be at play in canines, where the brain's reward system is activated by social cues. This intertwining of social emotions and instinctive reactions opens new avenues for understanding the depth of canine emotional lives.
