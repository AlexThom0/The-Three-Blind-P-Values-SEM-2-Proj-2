the distinction between the capacities of agents and non-agents lays the foundation for assessing ethical considerations in our interactions with different life forms. When we recognize that I possess the ability to plan, reflect, and adapt my goals according to shifting circumstances, it becomes clear that my well-being hinges on a respect for my autonomy. Conversely, a worm's existence is defined by simpler biological imperatives, revealing a fundamental disparity in the interests that motivate our lives. This hierarchy of needs underscores the importance of recognizing the unique value of agency in discussions about welfare and moral responsibility.
