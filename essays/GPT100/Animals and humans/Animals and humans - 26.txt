The growing discipline of ethology further propelled this shift, encouraging researchers to document instinctual actions rather than engage in the romanticism that characterized earlier narratives. As a result, the scientific community increasingly viewed animal behavior through the lens of evolutionary biology and adaptive significance, eschewing the notion of emotional depth or introspection. The philosophical implications of this transition reverberated through the field, challenging the romantic relationship between humans and the natural world.
