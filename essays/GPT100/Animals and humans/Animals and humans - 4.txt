In exploring the ethics surrounding the treatment of animals, it becomes evident that the current welfare frameworks are fundamentally flawed. They aim to mitigate suffering without addressing the underlying moral implications of animal exploitation. This paradigm permits the ongoing captivity and utilization of animals based solely on human benefit, prioritizing economic interests over the intrinsic value of animal lives. As long as welfare regulations allow for the justification of confinement and harm under the guise of necessity, the real issue remains unchallenged. A shift toward recognizing animals as beings with their own rights demands a profound reevaluation of our interactions with them.
