The introduction of managed honeybees can lead to a dilution of genetic diversity among native species and exacerbate competition for resources. For instance, honeybees may outcompete wild bees for nectar and pollen, potentially driving down wild populations already stressed by habitat loss. Moreover, the spread of diseases and parasites associated with domesticated bees can further threaten the survival of native pollinators, complicating conservation efforts. Understanding these dynamics is crucial for developing strategies that prioritize both agricultural productivity and the preservation of wild bee populations.
