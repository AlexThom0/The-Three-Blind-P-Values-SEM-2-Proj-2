This alliance was not merely transactional; it was underpinned by a shared vision of manifest destiny, where the expansion of settlers was seen as divinely sanctioned. This ideology fueled the belief that American Indians were obstacles to progress, justifying increasingly brutal measures to ensure dominance over the land. As tensions rose, the boundaries between civilian and military action blurred, allowing ranchers to operate with near impunity in their quests for retribution and control. In this volatile environment, the Newcombs, like many others, navigated a precarious existence marked by both opportunity and violence.
