Núñez argues that the interpretation of these neural responses may overlook the crucial role of cultural factors in shaping numerical understanding. He emphasizes that while the brain may exhibit certain foundational capacities for number processing, the methodologies through which we engage with numbers are extensively influenced by the cognitive frameworks fostered within specific cultural contexts. For instance, various societies employ distinct numerical systems and cultural norms that can profoundly impact how individuals conceptualize and manipulate numerical information. This interplay between biology and culture suggests that our cognitive abilities are not fixed but are instead dynamically shaped by our experiences and environments throughout development.
