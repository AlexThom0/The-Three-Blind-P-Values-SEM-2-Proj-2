By empowering localities with access to data and communication, the OGAS network aimed to harness the expertise of regional managers who could respond more swiftly to the demands of local markets. This dynamic approach promised not only to streamline production processes but also to foster a sense of ownership among workers, potentially enhancing productivity and accountability. Such optimism regarding decentralisation reflected a broader debate within Soviet ideology about balancing unity with diversity in achieving economic goals.
