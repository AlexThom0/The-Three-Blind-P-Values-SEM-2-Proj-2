Mechanical advancements, though significant, often remained at odds with prevailing attitudes that viewed science as beneath certain social classes. Consequently, while clockmaking flourished as an artisan craft, the pursuit of precision took a backseat to aesthetics and status. Wealthy patrons prized their ornate timepieces not merely as tools for measuring time but as reflections of their social standing, further entrenching the divide between status and technical expertise.
