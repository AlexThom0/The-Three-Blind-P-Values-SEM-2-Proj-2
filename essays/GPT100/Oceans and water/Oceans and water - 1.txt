The decline of fish populations not only imposed economic hardships on fishing communities but also intensified geopolitical tensions. Nations began to recognize the dire consequences of unsustainable harvesting and the need for international cooperation. Efforts emerged to regulate fishing practices through treaties and agreements aimed at sustainable management of marine resources. Initiatives like the Common Fisheries Policy in the EU sought to rebuild fish stocks and promote responsible fishing, engaging local communities in conservation efforts. These steps laid the groundwork for innovative solutions that balanced ecological health with the livelihoods of those dependent on the sea.
