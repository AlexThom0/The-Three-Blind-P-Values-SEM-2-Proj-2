Factors such as the rate of symbiont turnover, the ability to manage stress-induced reactive oxygen species, and the capacity for thermal acclimatization play crucial roles in a coral's survival. Additionally, genetic diversity within coral populations can enhance the likelihood of some individuals possessing these advantageous traits. As environmental conditions continue to change, the selective pressures on these organisms may further shape their evolutionary trajectories, illustrating the dynamic nature of coral reef ecosystems. This ongoing process highlights the importance of monitoring and supporting coral diversity.
