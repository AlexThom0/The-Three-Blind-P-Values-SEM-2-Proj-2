Studies indicate that remaining neurons can adapt and reorganize, forming new synaptic connections to mitigate deficits. This plasticity challenges traditional notions of brain function and supports the idea that intelligence is not solely a matter of quantity. Instead, it is the intricate interplay between various neural circuits that underpins cognitive abilities. Such findings not only deepen our understanding of neurological resilience but also suggest that the architecture of the brain is as vital as its sheer cellular density in shaping our experiences and behaviors.
