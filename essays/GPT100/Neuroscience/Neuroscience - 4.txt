This foundational work underscores the vast potential of connectomes to illuminate not only the structure but also the functioning of nervous systems across species. By studying these simpler systems, scientists can develop models that help predict more complex interactions in higher organisms, including humans. The implications extend beyond basic biology, touching on aspects of neural diseases and disorders, revealing pathways that could lead to breakthroughs in treatment and understanding mental health. Thus, elucidating connectomes provides profound insights that bridge evolutionary biology and medical research.
