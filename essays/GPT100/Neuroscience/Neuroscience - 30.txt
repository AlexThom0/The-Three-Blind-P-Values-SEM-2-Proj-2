This phenomenon underscores a trade-off inherent in the acquisition of knowledge: while expertise may streamline our decision-making and enhance performance, it simultaneously dulls our perception. By neglecting to engage deeply with our familiar environment, we risk losing not only the joy of routine experiences but also the innovative insights they can provoke.
