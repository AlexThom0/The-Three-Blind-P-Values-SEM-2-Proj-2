When we recall a memory, it does not merely retrieve a static image but rather reconstructs it in the moment. This process often leads to alterations, as the original context may be lost or modified. Consequently, our memories can be colored by emotions, current beliefs, or even suggestions from others. This phenomenon highlights the intricate interplay between recollection and reality, revealing the extent to which our cognitive apparatus shapes the past rather than merely reflecting it.
