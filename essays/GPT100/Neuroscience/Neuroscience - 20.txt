the mechanisms underlying central sensitisation reveal a complex interplay between the peripheral and central nervous systems. In particular, the spinal cord becomes a focal point for this heightened sensitivity, amplifying pain signals even in the absence of ongoing injury. Factors such as inflammation, genetic predisposition, and psychological states can exacerbate this phenomenon. This network of interactions complicates the clinical approach, as traditional assessments often overlook the underlying neuroplastic changes that contribute to persistent pain. Understanding these changes is crucial for developing effective treatment strategies. Addressing both the physical and psychological aspects may offer a more comprehensive solution for those affected.
