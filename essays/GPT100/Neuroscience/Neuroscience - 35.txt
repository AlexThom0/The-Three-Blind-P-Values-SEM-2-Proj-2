Researchers have demonstrated that BCIs can empower users to control prosthetic limbs, enhancing autonomy and integrating them more fully into daily activities. This technology is especially promising for patients who have lost motor function, as it not only facilitates movement but also fosters a sense of agency and self-determination. As both HP and BCI technologies advance, they hold the potential to redefine what is possible for individuals with neurological impairments, bridging the gap between medical science and the restoration of personal identity.
