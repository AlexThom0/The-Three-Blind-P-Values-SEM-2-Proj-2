This integrative role of the hippocampus became evident not only in the study of amnesic patients but also through various neuroimaging techniques. For instance, findings from functional MRI scans have shown that the hippocampus activates during tasks that require recalling specific associations between memories. This suggests that while individual memories may reside in localized areas, the ability to synthesize them into a coherent context relies heavily on the functional integrity of the hippocampus and its interconnected regions. Thus, its importance in the memory process cannot be overstated.
