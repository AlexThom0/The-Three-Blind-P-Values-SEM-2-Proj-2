Despite his wisdom and benevolence, Chiron's immortality became a curse, illustrating that the quest for eternal life may not equate to true fulfillment. This duality raises profound ethical questions regarding human interventions in aging, pushing us to consider whether extending life could inadvertently lead to prolonged suffering. The narrative of Eos and Tithonus, along with these mythical figures, provokes introspection about what it means to live a life worth living, highlighting the importance of meaningful existence over mere longevity.
