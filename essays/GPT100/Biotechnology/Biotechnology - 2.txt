This cultural shift has likely been fueled by a combination of media portrayals and consumerism, where the ideals of youth and beauty dominate public consciousness. The allure of eternal youth creates a fertile ground for pseudoscientific claims to flourish, drawing parallels to ancient beliefs rooted in magic. People become increasingly susceptible to the promise of products that claim to restore vitality and reverse the signs of ageing. Thus, the desire for rejuvenation not only reflects a societal preference for youth but also reveals deeper anxieties about mortality and the natural course of human life.
