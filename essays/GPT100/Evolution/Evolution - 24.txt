Archaeological findings, including intricate carvings and complex tool use, indicate that modern humans began to innovate in ways that were unprecedented among Neanderthals. This divergence raises questions about the cognitive capabilities of both groups and their adaptability to changing environments. As the evidence mounts, the landscape of early human cultural development becomes increasingly complex, demanding a reevaluation of how we perceive interactions between Neanderthals and modern humans. Understanding these nuances is essential for constructing a comprehensive narrative of our shared prehistoric past.
