For example, overly accurate mimicry might expose the mimic to higher predation risk from its own kind or limit its ability to thrive in diverse environments. Furthermore, the learning capacity of predators can vary, suggesting that some might adapt more rapidly than others. Understanding these dynamics adds depth to our knowledge of mimicry's success and its implications for both predator-prey interactions and the evolutionary trajectories of these species.
