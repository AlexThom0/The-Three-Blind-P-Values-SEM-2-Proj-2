This suggests that while incremental evolution certainly occurs, significant transformations could arise in response to environmental pressures or catastrophic events. Moreover, these moments of upheaval may lead to the emergence of new species, illustrating how external factors can accelerate evolutionary processes. As such, understanding the dynamics of these shifts is crucial for comprehending the broader mechanisms of evolution. This insight reshapes how we interpret evolutionary time and the development of complexity in life forms.
