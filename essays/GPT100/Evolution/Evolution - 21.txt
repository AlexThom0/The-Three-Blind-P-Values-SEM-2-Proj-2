Trivers’s insights prompted a deeper investigation into human mating strategies and the underlying psychological mechanisms. This evolving understanding suggested that not only do biological imperatives drive sexual selection, but cultural factors play a significant role in shaping preferences and behaviors. For instance, societal norms and gender roles can influence how individuals perceive attraction, commitment, and infidelity. As researchers began to explore these dynamics, they recognized that the interplay between evolutionary urges and cultural contexts adds complexity to sexual behavior, highlighting a spectrum of strategies adopted by both sexes. Such nuances underscore the multifaceted nature of human sexuality.
