These therapies, designed to target specific aspects of cancer cells or their environment, have shifted the focus from a one-size-fits-all methodology to a more personalized treatment strategy. By understanding the genetic makeup of different tumors, researchers can tailor interventions that maximize efficacy while minimizing side effects. This paradigm has not only enhanced survival rates for various cancers but also has opened avenues for research into previously hard-to-treat malignancies, thus revolutionizing the patient experience and outcomes in cancer care.
