This focus underscores a troubling trend in modern medicine, where the emphasis on lowering specific biomarkers can overshadow a holistic understanding of patient health. Many individuals have been subjected to aggressive treatment without sufficient justification, often influenced by marketing rather than clinical necessity. Furthermore, the potential long-term effects of statin use remain inadequately explored, leaving patients caught between the risks of undertreatment and overtreatment. As we reassess our approach, it becomes essential to prioritize individualized care over a one-size-fits-all model.
