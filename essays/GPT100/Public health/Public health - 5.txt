Communities quickly learned that while switching to OPV reduced the incidence of paralytic polio, it also required careful monitoring of vaccine-associated complications. Public health officials became increasingly aware of the need for robust surveillance systems to identify VAPP cases and prevent outbreaks. This adaptation underscored the importance of balancing vaccine benefits against potential risks, prompting further innovations in immunization technology. These ongoing adjustments reflected a broader understanding of the necessity for a multifaceted approach to eradicating polio and protecting vulnerable populations from both the disease and its vaccine-related consequences.
