Many individuals experience overlapping symptoms that defy traditional categorization, making the reliance on specific diagnostic labels problematic. These labels can oversimplify the lived experiences of patients, potentially leading to misdiagnosis or inadequate treatment. Furthermore, the constraints of diagnostic categories often hinder innovative approaches to understanding and treating mental health issues, as researchers may feel compelled to adhere to established nomenclature rather than exploring more integrative or individualized models. This raises the question of whether the current frameworks can evolve to better reflect the nuances of mental health.
