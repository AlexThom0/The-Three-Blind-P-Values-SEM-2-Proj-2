As the burgeoning field of psychology began to explore the intricate relationship between individual identity and societal expectations, anorexia emerged not merely as a physical ailment but as a reflection of deeper existential struggles. Researchers began to document how societal pressures regarding beauty, self-worth, and success often exacerbated the condition. This recognition shifted the approach to treatment, incorporating cognitive and behavioral therapies aimed at addressing the underlying psychological issues in tandem with nutritional rehabilitation. Such multifaceted understandings paved the way for more holistic treatment methodologies.
