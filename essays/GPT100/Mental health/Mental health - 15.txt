This approach seeks to mitigate biases that can skew results, particularly in conditions like depression, where subjective experiences can heavily influence outcomes. Furthermore, the interplay between expectation and actual therapeutic benefit complicates the interpretation of results. While some patients may experience significant improvements with placebos, others may not show any change, highlighting the variability of individual responses and the challenge of isolating the true impact of medications in clinical settings.
