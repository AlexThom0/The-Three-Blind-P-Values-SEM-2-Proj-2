As mental health professionals, we grappled with the complexity of intervening in a context where trauma was not merely a past event but an ever-present reality. The challenge was not only to address the psychological needs of individuals but also to consider the collective impact on communities enduring prolonged suffering. This ongoing crisis forced us to rethink our approaches, emphasizing resilience and coping mechanisms as critical components of care. Such insights were crucial for developing strategies that recognized the fluidity of trauma in a war zone, where the notion of recovery often eluded those still in peril.
