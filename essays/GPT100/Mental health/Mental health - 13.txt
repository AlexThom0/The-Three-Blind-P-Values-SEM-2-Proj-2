This heightened state of anxiety aligns with evolutionary theories suggesting that increased vigilance during critical periods, such as after childbirth or during adolescence, may serve adaptive purposes. The interplay of hormonal fluctuations and environmental stressors can further amplify these tendencies, leading individuals to engage in compulsive behaviors as a means of self-protection. These responses, while potentially maladaptive in modern contexts, underscore the biological and psychological roots of OCD as an exaggerated response to legitimate threats.
