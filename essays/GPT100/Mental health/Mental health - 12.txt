These questions challenge the very foundations of therapeutic practices that prioritize an unyielding pursuit of happiness. By suggesting that depression might provide a clearer lens on reality, we confront the possibility that our emotional struggles become routes to deeper truths. This perspective invites a reevaluation of how society views emotional distress, not merely as a disorder to be treated, but as an experience that can foster resilience and understanding. Recognizing the value in discomfort may shift the therapeutic goal from merely achieving happiness to embracing a more authentic exploration of our human experience. Thus, genuine insights may arise from accepting our struggles.
