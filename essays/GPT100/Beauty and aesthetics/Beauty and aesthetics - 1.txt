In this milieu, poetry emerged as a refuge for the ineffable, where language could articulate sentiments that reason often stripped of clarity. Poets began to embrace the paradox that while some truths eluded empirical grasp, they could still evoke profound emotional and aesthetic resonance. The interplay between the rational and the lyrical fostered a fertile ground for exploring human experience, leading to the realization that beauty could serve as a connective tissue among those elusive truths. This advent of poetic inquiry not only revitalized the art form but also established a counterpoint to the rigid abstractions of Kant's critiques.
