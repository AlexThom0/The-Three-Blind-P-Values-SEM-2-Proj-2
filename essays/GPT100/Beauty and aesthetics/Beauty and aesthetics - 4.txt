Gobineau's theories emerged during a time of intense colonial expansion, where European powers often justified domination over non-European societies through claims of racial superiority. This ideological backdrop provided fertile ground for his arguments, as they resonated with a populace keen to rationalize its imperial pursuits. By linking racial purity to cultural and scientific superiority, Gobineau not only influenced contemporary thought but also laid the groundwork for future racial ideologies that would permeate 19th and 20th-century politics and social sciences, reinforcing harmful stereotypes that persist to this day.
