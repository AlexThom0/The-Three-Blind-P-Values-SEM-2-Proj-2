The archetype of the Jezebel not only confined black women to a narrow role but also contributed to a societal perception that undermined their autonomy. The demonization inherent in this stereotype led to a dismissal of their agency, reducing them to mere symbols of lust rather than complex individuals with their own narratives. This lack of recognition facilitated a brutal cycle of violence and exploitation, allowing the continuation of racial and gender inequalities. As generations passed, these stereotypes became ingrained in cultural narratives, influencing perceptions and treatment of black women in both public and private spheres, reinforcing their marginalization even in contemporary society.
