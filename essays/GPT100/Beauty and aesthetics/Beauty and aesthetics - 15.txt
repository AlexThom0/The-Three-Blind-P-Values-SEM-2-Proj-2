This cultural shift not only glorified strength but also created a new archetype for masculinity, one that melded physical capability with moral superiority. The superhero narrative took advantage of this context, presenting protagonists who embodied both physical strength and ethical fortitude. By portraying these characters as not only protectors but also as symbols of societal values, these stories resonated with audiences seeking a deeper validation of masculinity. This interplay between strength and virtue illustrates how definitions of manhood have evolved and become entwined with cultural expectations.
