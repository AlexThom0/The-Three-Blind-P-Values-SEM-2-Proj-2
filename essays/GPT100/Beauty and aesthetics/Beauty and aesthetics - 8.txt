Human perception is intricately linked to context, and the negative connotations associated with forgery may deeply entrench our judgments. Cultural narratives surrounding authenticity create a framework where an artwork’s perceived value is inextricably tied to its origin story. This interplay suggests that much of our aesthetic appreciation is conditioned by social cues rather than purely visual or tactile elements. Exploring this dynamic could provide insights into how perceived authenticity shapes our emotional and intellectual engagement with art.
