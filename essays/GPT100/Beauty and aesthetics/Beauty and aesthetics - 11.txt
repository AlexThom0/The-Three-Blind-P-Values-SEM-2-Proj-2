The rise of social media and bite-sized content consumption further amplifies this tendency, making it increasingly difficult for audiences to engage deeply with artistic works. The demand for immediate gratification and quick consumption fosters a culture where narrative coherence takes a backseat to sensational moments. This pervasive commodification of attention cultivates an environment where the richness of artistic expression is undermined, leading to a shallow understanding of complex themes and ideas. Consequently, what we experience is not art in its fullest form, but rather a series of engaging spectacles that lack meaningful connection.
