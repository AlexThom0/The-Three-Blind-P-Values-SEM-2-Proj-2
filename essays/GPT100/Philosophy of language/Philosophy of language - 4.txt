Fiction invites readers into a constructed world, allowing them to willingly suspend disbelief and engage with characters and events that do not adhere to reality. This engagement requires a shared understanding between the author and the audience; the author provides a narrative framework, while the audience agrees to immerse themselves in this imaginative experience. Unlike deception, where trust is fundamentally undermined, fiction thrives on the creation of a safe space for exploration and reflection. The relationship between author and reader is thus transformed, fostering a collaborative engagement with the text.
