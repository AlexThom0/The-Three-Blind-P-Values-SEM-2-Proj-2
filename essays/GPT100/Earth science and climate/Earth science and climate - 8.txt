Dawson's conviction was rooted in a deep-seated belief that beauty in nature signified purpose. He posited that such intricate forms were not mere outcomes of random processes but instead evidence of an intelligent creator's design. This perspective served not only to challenge Darwin's evolutionary framework but also to elevate his own interpretations of fossil evidence. Dawson insisted that the complexity he observed in Eozoön canadense contradicted the notion of gradual change over time. Thus, his arguments provided a platform for those who sought to reconcile scientific discovery with a view of life imbued with intentionality and meaning.
