As certain plants adapt to resist or even benefit from fire, their existence is tied intrinsically to its presence. This relationship fosters resilience in ecosystems, allowing species to coexist through competition and dependence. As fire shapes the landscape, it also shapes evolution, influencing which species thrive and which dwindle. Understanding this complex interplay helps clarify why fire is not just a destructive force, but a crucial component of ecological balance and health.
