As this demographic transition unfolds, the pressures on natural ecosystems fluctuate. Urbanization and agricultural expansion often lead to habitat fragmentation and biodiversity loss, even in regions with declining fertility rates. The challenge lies in reconciling the need for sustainable resource use with the ecological footprints left by growing urban populations. Innovative conservation strategies can help mitigate these impacts, such as integrating green infrastructure in city planning and enhancing connectivity between fragmented habitats. These efforts not only support wildlife but also ensure that urban areas can manage their resource demands while fostering coexistence with nature.
