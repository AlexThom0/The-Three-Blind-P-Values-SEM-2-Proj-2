the benefits of silvopasture extend beyond mere land use; they encompass enhanced biodiversity and improved ecosystem services. By integrating trees, crops, and livestock, these systems promote soil health and water retention while providing habitats for various species. Additionally, they offer resilience against climate fluctuations, supporting farmers in adapting to changing conditions. The cultural significance of these traditional practices also cannot be overlooked, as they embody the knowledge and heritage of communities that have stewarded the land for generations. This interconnectedness of ecological health and cultural identity is crucial in the modern context of environmental sustainability.
