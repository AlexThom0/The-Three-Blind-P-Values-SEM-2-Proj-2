This idea suggested that the very fabric of the cosmos was governed by mathematical laws akin to those found in music. Just as musical notes can be arranged in harmonious chords, celestial bodies were believed to be positioned in a way that they resonated with each other. This harmony extended beyond mere mathematics; it embodied a profound philosophical vision where the universe was inherently ordered and interconnected, reflecting the divine intelligence that structured both music and existence.
