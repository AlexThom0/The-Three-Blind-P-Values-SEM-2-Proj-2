By positioning the wavefunction as a tool for personal belief rather than an objective entity, QBism opens doors to a more pragmatic understanding of quantum phenomena. It invites scrutiny of the relationship between observer and observation, suggesting that our interactions shape the nature of what we consider real. Such a view redefines the role of the observer in the quantum realm and suggests a model where knowledge itself becomes a central player in the unfolding reality.
