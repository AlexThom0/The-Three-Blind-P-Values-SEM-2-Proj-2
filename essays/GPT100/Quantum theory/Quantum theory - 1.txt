Quantum mechanics introduces principles such as superposition and entanglement, which fundamentally reshape our understanding of how particles interact. As electrons occupy orbitals, their probabilities form intricate patterns around nuclei, leading to phenomena such as hybridization and resonance. These behaviors reveal that molecular structures arise not merely from static arrangements of atoms but from dynamic interactions governed by probabilistic laws. Consequently, the transition from classical to quantum perspective is crucial for a comprehensive grasp of chemical properties and the behaviors that underpin the vast diversity of molecular compounds.
