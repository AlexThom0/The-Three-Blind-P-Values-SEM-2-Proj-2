She notes that both experiences activate overlapping neural pathways, emphasizing the interconnectedness of emotional and physical responses. This suggests that the sense of loss can trigger profound physiological reactions, reinforcing the primitive survival instincts within us. In situations of bereavement, the brain prioritizes immediate emotional reactions over rational thought, creating a landscape where the grieving individual may feel disoriented and vulnerable. Understanding this interplay highlights the necessity of compassionate support during times of profound sorrow.
