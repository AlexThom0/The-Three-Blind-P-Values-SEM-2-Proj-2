This observation, alongside Friedmann's early hypotheses, laid the groundwork for modern cosmology. It prompted scientists to reconsider the structure of the cosmos and our place within it. The implications of an expanding universe challenged prior notions of a static cosmos, provoking inquiries into the forces driving this expansion.
