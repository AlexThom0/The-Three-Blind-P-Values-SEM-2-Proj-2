These fluctuations arise from quantum fluctuations amplified during cosmic inflation, creating a landscape of highs and lows in density. As density variations emerge, certain regions might gravitationally collapse, forming the seeds of primordial black holes. The scale of these inhomogeneities can vary; larger fluctuations might lead to more massive black holes, while smaller ones could produce lighter counterparts. Understanding this diversity not only illuminates the processes behind black hole formation but also sheds light on the conditions that shaped the evolution of the Universe itself.
