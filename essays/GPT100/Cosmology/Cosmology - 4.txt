Milgrom postulated a modified gravity theory known as MOND, or Modified Newtonian Dynamics, which sought to adjust gravitational laws at low accelerations. Rather than assuming the presence of unseen mass, MOND suggests that the force of gravity itself changes under specific conditions, particularly in the outskirts of galaxies. This innovative approach not only aimed to resolve the rotation-curve anomaly but also sought to unify the behavior of all galaxies without invoking dark matter. Thus, Milgrom's theory represented a significant departure from traditional cosmological models, inviting both intrigue and skepticism from the scientific community.
