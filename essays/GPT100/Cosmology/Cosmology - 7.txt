the concept of 'now' is relative and influenced by factors such as the speed of light and the vast distances separating events in the cosmos. For instance, when we observe a supernova, we are not witnessing an event as it happens, but rather light that has traveled across vast cosmic expanses, taking years—if not millions—of years to reach us. This disconnect between the moment we perceive and the actual occurrence complicates our understanding of time itself. The interplay of light, distance, and observation challenges the clarity with which we assign moments to their respective ‘nows’.
