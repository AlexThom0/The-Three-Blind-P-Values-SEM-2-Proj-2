American leaders drew upon Rome’s legacy not only to inspire but also to rationalize their own societal structures. The allure of Roman ideals often obscured the brutal truths of the American experience, particularly the exploitation of enslaved peoples and the marginalization of nonwhite citizens. This selective appropriation served to create a narrative where liberty and governance were intertwined with a rigid racial hierarchy. Such convictions reinforced the notion that certain races were inherently superior, perpetuating a dangerous mythos that justified oppression. As these historical figures admired Rome's republican virtues, they simultaneously perpetuated systems of inequality that echoed the very injustices they sought to transcend.
