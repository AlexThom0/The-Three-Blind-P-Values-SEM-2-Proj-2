This sharp contrast underscores not only Ashoka's personal transformation but also serves as a critique of previous rulers' values, where excess and domination defined power. The juxtaposition of such large numbers draws attention to the pervasive normalization of violence in governance. By reducing the need for bloodshed, Ashoka sought to redefine leadership, emphasizing morality over might. His edicts create a narrative around compassion as a governing principle, challenging the status quo and encouraging a more humane society. This shift invites reflection on the ethical implications of rule in ancient times.
