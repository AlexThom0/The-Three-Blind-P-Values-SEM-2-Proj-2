Odysseus' transformation under Athena's guidance raises further questions about the fluidity of identity in epic poetry. The colors attributed to characters often defy our modern perceptions, suggesting a symbolic resonance rather than a literal representation. By examining these shifts, we can discern not only the physical attributes of the heroes but also the deeper cultural meanings they evoke. The interplay of shades reflects not just individual characteristics but also broader themes of vitality, transformation, and the interplay between human and divine realms in Homeric storytelling. This complexity invites us to reconsider the implications of color in the understanding of character and narrative.
