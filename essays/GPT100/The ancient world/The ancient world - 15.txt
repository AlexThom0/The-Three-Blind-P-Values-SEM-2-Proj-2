The necessity to track trade, inventory, and ownership led societies to refine these symbols further, paving the way for abstract writing systems. As merchants and officials sought efficiency, the representation of quantities transformed from physical tokens to more sophisticated pictographs, ultimately contributing to the establishment of a standardized written language.
