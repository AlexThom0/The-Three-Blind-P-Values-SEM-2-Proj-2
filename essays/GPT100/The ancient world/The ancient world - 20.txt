This duality in management—between the carrot of incentive and the stick of punishment—mirrors modern workplace dynamics where leaders often seek to assert control while minimizing direct conflict. The emotional and psychological manipulation seen in Roman practices finds echoes in contemporary corporate culture, where team-building exercises can mask deeper hierarchies and inequalities. Just as Roman masters felt compelled to exhibit dominance, today’s leaders navigate the delicate balance between authority and the need to foster a cohesive and motivated workforce, often under the guise of collaboration and camaraderie.
