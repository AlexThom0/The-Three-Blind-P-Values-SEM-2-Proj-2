Critics argue that this rejection of a Greek origin stemmed more from nationalistic ideals than linguistic evidence. Evans's approach often overshadowed alternative theories that suggested an evolutionary link between the languages, with proponents of the Greek hypothesis gathering momentum as new artifacts emerged. This created a battleground for scholarly debate, where linguistic methodologies evolved, emphasizing the need to reassess assumptions in light of emerging data and comparative studies across the Mediterranean. Thus, the narrative of Minoan language began to shift.
