Each city-state operated under its own set of customs and governance, often collaborating yet competing with neighboring ports. As trade thrived, these local differences grew more pronounced, and the resulting interactions with other civilizations influenced their artistic expressions and societal structures. This dynamic environment fostered a sense of regional rather than collective identity, where the unique contributions of each city-state shaped a complex mosaic instead of a unified whole. Such distinctions underscore the challenges in categorizing the Phoenicians as a monolithic entity.
