The experience of flight also alters individual psyches, prompting travelers to reconsider their relationship with distance and connectivity. In the confined space of an airplane, the compression of time and space fosters a unique introspection. Passengers are suspended between destinations, often reflecting on the spaces they leave behind and the ones they approach. This duality encourages a broader contemplation of identity and belonging, resonating with themes of transience and permanence as people traverse vast landscapes, both geographically and existentially.
