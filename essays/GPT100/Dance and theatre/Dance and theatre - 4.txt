This prolific output not only served as a form of therapy for Gomes but also provided insight into the nuanced inner worlds of individuals grappling with mental illness. By transforming personal suffering into tangible expressions of creativity, she challenged societal perceptions of madness. In this context, her work stands as a testament to the power of art to transcend pain and foster understanding.
