Inuit understanding emphasizes a relational approach to nature, where knowledge is dynamic and embedded in lived experience rather than static or merely historical. This epistemology champions patterns, adaptations, and interactions within the ecosystem, highlighting the significance of context and environment in shaping Indigenous perspectives. As such, the Inuit perspective invites a reconceptualization of knowledge itself, urging recognition of the expert voices rooted in place. It challenges prevailing Western frameworks that often prioritize quantifiable data over narrative and local insight, reinforcing that knowing is a communal endeavor that thrives on shared experiences and deep connections with the land.
