Socialization plays a crucial role in shaping interest and opportunity in chess from a young age, with boys often encouraged to engage more deeply in competitive environments. The lack of historical representation and mentorship for women in chess further exacerbates this disparity. Studies indicating that the training environment, resources, and media representation significantly impact participation highlight that performance differences may stem not from innate ability but from systemic inequality within the chess community.
