It’s possible that overly simplistic labels of low or high self-esteem may not capture the full spectrum of a child’s self-perception. The role of context is crucial; a supportive environment, even with non-inflated praise, might help children internalize successes and failures in a balanced way. Moreover, the impact of peer interactions and parental modeling cannot be overstated, as these factors often shape a child’s understanding of their own worthiness. In this light, fostering a nuanced approach to praise becomes essential.
