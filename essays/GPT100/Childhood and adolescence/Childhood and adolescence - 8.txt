This reflects an emerging understanding of oneself as both vulnerable and powerful, grappling with an existential tension that shapes identity. The stories I tell thus evolve from mere warnings to complex narratives of survival, where death becomes a character we can negotiate with rather than a certainty we must accept. Children begin to see life as a series of choices, and this conscious engagement with mortality creates a sense of agency, even as it complicates their understanding of loss. The interplay between fear and courage becomes the essence of their narrative journey.
