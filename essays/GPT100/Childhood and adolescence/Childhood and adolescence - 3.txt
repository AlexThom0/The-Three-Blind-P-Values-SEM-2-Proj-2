When faced with inconsistent care, children may learn to prioritize self-sufficiency, favoring autonomy over dependence. Understanding these diverse attachment trajectories sheds light on the importance of contextual factors in shaping relational patterns, suggesting that even seemingly maladaptive behaviors may carry underlying functional significance in the context of survival and development.
