These institutions aimed to provide mentorship, education, and recreational activities to channel youthful energy into constructive outlets. By fostering a sense of belonging and purpose, reformers believed they could combat the allure of crime and exploitation. Programs designed to engage at-risk youth offered not only vocational training but also emotional guidance, creating a safety net for those threatened by the harsh realities of urban life. Such initiatives marked a pivotal shift in society's approach to juvenile delinquency, framing youth as a vital resource rather than a liability.
