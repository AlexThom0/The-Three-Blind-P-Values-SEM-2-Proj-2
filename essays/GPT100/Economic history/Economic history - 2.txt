The Church, while advocating for the spiritual ascendance of the soul, simultaneously faced the challenge of addressing the insatiable desires of its followers. As commercialism took root, a paradox emerged: devotion could become intertwined with consumption, transforming piety into a commodity. This complex interplay reshaped theological discourse, prompting scholars to explore the moral implications of wealth accumulation within a religious framework. Thus, the tension between spiritual ideals and material realities became a central theme, influencing both ecclesiastical teachings and economic thought.
