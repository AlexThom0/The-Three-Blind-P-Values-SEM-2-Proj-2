This redistribution was not merely a response to the devastation of the war but also an ideological push against the militaristic and imperialist past. By dismantling entrenched power structures, the reformists aimed to foster a new sense of civic responsibility among citizens. The changes were accompanied by measures to promote educational access and social mobility, laying a foundation for future growth that emphasized both productivity and fairness in opportunity. The transformation underscored a commitment to rebuilding society from the ground up.
