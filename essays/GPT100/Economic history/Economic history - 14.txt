Sicilians, feeling marginalized and disillusioned by the political upheaval, began to seek alternatives to the formal authorities that seemed indifferent to their plight. Many turned to local figures who, while operating outside the law, promised protection and support against the rising tide of crime and instability. This reliance on unofficial power structures signaled a shift in community dynamics, as trust in government dissipated. Furthermore, the economic challenges facing farmers and laborers exacerbated this trend, fostering a sense of loyalty towards these emerging power brokers who claimed to embody their interests and safeguard their livelihoods.
