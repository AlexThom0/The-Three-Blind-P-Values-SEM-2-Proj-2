The shift towards privatization not only altered the affordability of education but also commodified the college experience. Students were increasingly viewed as consumers, navigating a landscape where their worth was measured against escalating tuition fees and the burden of debt. This reconfiguration of the educational landscape fed into the larger narrative of individual responsibility, further distancing students from the communal support structures that had once nurtured their academic pursuits and personal development.
