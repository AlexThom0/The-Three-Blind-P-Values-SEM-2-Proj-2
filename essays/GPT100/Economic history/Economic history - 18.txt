This choice underscored the earlier navigational errors, highlighting not only the unpredictability of maritime travel in the era but also the limitations of contemporary knowledge about the seas. Sailors relied on scant charts and oral traditions, often leading them astray or into dangerous territories. The reliance on wind patterns and currents, while crucial, also meant that voyages depended heavily on chance. Such uncertainties shaped the fate of explorers, who risked everything on their understanding of these vast, dynamic environments. The ocean was both a pathway and a formidable barrier.
