This monumental task not only reflected a desire for control but also embodied the ideological stance that human behavior could be quantified and predicted in a deterministic manner. As a result, statistical methods became tools of statecraft, designed to manage society rather than reflect its complexity. The rejection of randomness further reinforced a worldview where order prevailed over chaos, leading to oversimplifications in understanding social dynamics. Such rigid methodologies inevitably shaped the interpretations and policies that followed.
