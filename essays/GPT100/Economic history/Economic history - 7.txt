As governments struggled to stabilize their financial systems, they imposed tariffs and restrictions that further isolated national markets. This shift not only postponed the recovery of export-driven growth but also intensified regional trade rivalries, as countries vying for recovery reoriented their economic strategies. Concurrently, the emergence of new economic powers began to reshape the dynamics of global trade, introducing a complexity that would redefine the next phase of international economic relations.
