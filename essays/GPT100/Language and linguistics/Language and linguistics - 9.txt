This strategy reflects the broader phenomenon of linguistic experimentation in medieval writing, where authors often blended multiple languages to convey spiritual truths. Hildegard’s use of her constructed language can be seen as an act of creation itself, mirroring God's own creative power. By stepping outside the constraints of known language, she invites her audience to experience the divine in visceral ways, challenging both their understanding and their connection to the sacred. Such innovative approaches were not uncommon among mystical writers of her time.
