In contrast, the development of Latin punctuation did not initially provide the same nuanced guidance for oral performance. Instead, early Medieval manuscripts largely relied on spaces between words, with minimal indication of how phrases should be articulated. As the medieval period progressed, scribes sought to enhance readability and clarity through innovations such as the use of dots and various ligatures. This gradual evolution signified an increasing awareness of the text not merely as a written artifact but as a vehicle for communication, anticipating the later incorporation of more complex systems intended to convey both rhythm and meaning in written form.
