When we elevate personal taste to an untouchable status, we stifle the discourse necessary to challenge ideas and refine our cultural sensibilities. The goal should not be to create a homogenized landscape of accepted views, but rather to foster a vibrant exchange where differing opinions can be articulated and contested, which ultimately enriches our understanding of societal values and individual expression. Balancing personal liberty with communal discourse is crucial for a healthy democracy.
