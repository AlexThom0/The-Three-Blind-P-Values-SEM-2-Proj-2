Just as scientists rigorously assess data before forming conclusions about extraterrestrial life, so too should we apply a similar level of scrutiny to miraculous events. The historical context surrounding such claims often lacks not only empirical support but also reliable witness accounts. This disparity in the quality of evidence leads us to challenge the validity of widespread beliefs about miracles, urging a more robust philosophical inquiry into what constitutes justification in both scientific and religious contexts. The requirement for substantial proof remains unequivocal.
