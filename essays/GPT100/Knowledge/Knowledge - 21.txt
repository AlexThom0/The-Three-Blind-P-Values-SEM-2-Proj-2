While DNA evidence relies on mathematical probabilities and a clearer empirical foundation, fingerprint analysis often hinges on subjective human evaluation. This disparity illustrates a broader issue within forensic science, where reliance on human judgment can introduce uncertainties that newer methodologies seek to eliminate. Despite the advancements offered by DNA technology, the integration of forensic disciplines must still contend with the potential for human error. Consequently, the pursuit of absolute certainty in evidence collection remains an ongoing challenge, underscoring the importance of continual refinement in forensic practices.
