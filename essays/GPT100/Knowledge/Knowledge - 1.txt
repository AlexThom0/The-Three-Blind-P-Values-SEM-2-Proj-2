This collective introspection reveals a shared anxiety about the implications of our pursuits. Each discipline grapples with its own set of boundaries—structural limitations, ethical considerations, and the specter of futility. In the sciences, the quest for knowledge is often driven by tangible objectives, promising discoveries that aim to solve pressing problems. In stark contrast, the humanistic inquiry often remains more esoteric, with few definitive conclusions in sight. Yet, as interdisciplinary dialogues flourish, it becomes evident that both domains are inextricably linked. Knowledge production, regardless of its form, aims to illuminate the human experience, enriching our understanding of what it means to exist.
