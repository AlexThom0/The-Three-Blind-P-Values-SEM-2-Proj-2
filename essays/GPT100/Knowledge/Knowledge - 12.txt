Central to this distinction is the nature of certainty vs. belief in legal contexts. While statistical evidence offers a probabilistic view, it often lacks the qualitative certainty that personal knowledge provides. A jury might ponder over mathematical calculations, questioning the implications of statistical margins, but they resonate more with a compelling narrative presented by an eyewitness. The narrative evokes a sense of immediate reality, creating a more robust connection between the evidence and the jury’s deliberation process. This emotional and cognitive engagement shifts the perception of guilt from mere probability to a shared understanding, reinforcing the validity of their verdict.
