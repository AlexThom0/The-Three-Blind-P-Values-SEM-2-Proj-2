This framework, however, encounters significant challenges when confronted with complex systems that exhibit non-linear interactions and feedback loops, characteristics often overlooked in traditional models. The rigidity of classical assumptions fails to account for phenomena like market bubbles or crashes, which illustrate the limitations of deterministic thinking. By prioritizing isolated variables, economists may neglect the interconnectedness that can dramatically alter outcomes. The advent of behavioral economics further complicates the picture, revealing that human decision-making often deviates from rationality, influenced by cognitive biases and social dynamics. This divergence from classical theories highlights the necessity for a more nuanced understanding of economic behavior.
