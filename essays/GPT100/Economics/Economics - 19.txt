This rhetoric crafted a narrative where military personnel were perceived as the true embodiment of American values—sacrifice, courage, and discipline. As a result, public perception shifted, creating a stark divide between the valor associated with military life and the perceived frivolity of civilian existence. This cultural elevation of service members reinforced the idea that their sacrifices warranted not just recognition, but an expansive safety net that would further entrench their status in society. Consequently, the military welfare state solidified a unique social contract, privileging servicemen and women above others in the economic landscape.
