These accolades not only validate certain methodologies but also create a hierarchy of ideas that favor mainstream approaches over alternative ones. As prestigious institutions award economists for neoclassical contributions, a feedback loop emerges, where funding, publishing opportunities, and academic appointments increasingly favor work aligned with these accepted paradigms. Consequently, promising alternative theories struggle to gain traction within an environment that values conformity and mathematical rigor over interdisciplinary exploration and nuanced understanding. This has led to a profound intellectual imbalance that continues to influence both teaching and research in the field.
