This technology promised transparency and security, but it also introduced layers of complexity that could obscure understanding. Investors, entrusting their capital to this automated system, faced a paradox: while the absence of intermediaries minimized human error, it also removed accountability. If something went wrong, recourse was limited not just by design but by the very nature of decentralization. These dynamics reflected a fundamental shift in how individuals perceive trust and ownership within digital economies, challenging traditional financial systems in profound ways.
