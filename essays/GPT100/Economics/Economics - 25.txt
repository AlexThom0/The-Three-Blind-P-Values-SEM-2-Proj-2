This era marked a significant shift in economic thought, as policymakers began to recognize the importance of not just output but also the well-being of the workforce. As unemployment soared, the focus expanded from merely quantifying production to assessing the broader implications of economic activity on society. The interplay between production capacity and employment levels underscored the need for comprehensive measures that could inform effective policy decisions. This evolution in perspective laid the groundwork for modern economic indicators, emphasizing the intricate connections between economic performance and social welfare.
