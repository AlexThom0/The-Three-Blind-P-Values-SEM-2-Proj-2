By reframing employment as a source of psychological fulfillment, the potential for exploitation increases, as the value of labor is divorced from its economic necessity. This new paradigm risks romanticizing precarious work, positioning it as a pathway to well-being despite the lack of stability or fair compensation. Consequently, policymakers face the challenge of addressing both the emotional significance of work and the pressing need for equitable economic reforms.
