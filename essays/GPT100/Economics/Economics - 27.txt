Kopparberg's approach exemplifies the paradox of modern marketing, where discontent with excessive choice is increasingly harnessed to promote new products. By referencing a time when options were simpler, they create a nostalgic longing that subtly validates consumer frustrations. This strategy not only elevates the brand but also leverages a collective sentiment—underscoring a shared disillusionment with the overwhelming array of choices on supermarket shelves. In this way, they not only position themselves as pioneers of innovation but also bring consumers together through their common critique of dilution in the cider category, making them part of an exclusive community of discerning drinkers.
