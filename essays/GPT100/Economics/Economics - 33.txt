The rapid urbanization of these populations not only intensified competition for jobs but also strained resources and infrastructure in the burgeoning cities. As individuals moved to urban centers seeking employment, they encountered a stark reality; wages stagnated or declined due to the sheer number of job seekers. This influx often led to overcrowded living conditions, inadequate sanitation, and increased social tensions as communities struggled to adapt. Furthermore, the mingling of diverse workforces introduced cultural and economic exchanges that sometimes sparked resentment among native workers, further complicating the labor landscape. Consequently, the socio-economic fabric started to fray under the weight of unrelenting demand.
