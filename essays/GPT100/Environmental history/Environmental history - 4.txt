The escalating demands of the mining industry exacerbated environmental degradation, resulting in compromised land management practices. Settlers often prioritized short-term gains over sustainable practices, leading to the depletion of vital resources. As population density increased, so did the pressure on already fragile ecosystems. Fields that once supported diverse flora and fauna fell victim to extensive clearing and industrial waste. This relentless transformation not only threatened local biodiversity but also diminished the land's resilience to withstand climate adversities. The consequences of this exploitation were perceived in the persistent dust storms that enveloped the region, signaling a profound ecological imbalance.
