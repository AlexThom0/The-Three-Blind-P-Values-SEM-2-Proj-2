These plantations, characterized by their uniformity and rapid growth, reshaped not only ecological dynamics but also local economies. Communities began to rely on the cultivation of Cunninghamia lanceolata for timber and resin, creating a feedback loop between human demand and forestry practices. This set of interdependencies highlights how the adaptation of a single species can have far-reaching implications, influencing everything from biodiversity to social structures within those human communities.
