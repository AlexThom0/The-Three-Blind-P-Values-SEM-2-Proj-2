While electric processes can help in refining and shaping, the initial extraction and high-temperature processing still depend heavily on fossil fuel inputs. Moreover, many chemical processes essential to manufacturing require heat beyond what today's electrical systems can sustainably provide. This necessity presents a fundamental challenge in reimagining an industrial landscape through an electrical paradigm. Without advancements in generating high-temperature electrical outputs, the core of heavy industry will remain tied to combustion, highlighting the enduring influence of fossil fuels in modern development.
