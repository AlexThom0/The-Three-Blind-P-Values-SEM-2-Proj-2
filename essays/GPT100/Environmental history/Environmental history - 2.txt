This interdisciplinary approach aimed to assess not only the immediate impacts of nuclear detonations but also the long-term ecological consequences. Research focused on the resilience of various species and the potential for ecosystem recovery, yet many habitats showed alarming signs of irreversible damage. Marine life, in particular, demonstrated unexpected vulnerabilities; the bioaccumulation of toxins severely disrupted local food webs. As these studies unfolded, it became increasingly clear that the consequences of radiation and chemical exposure extended far beyond human health, affecting entire ecosystems and challenging established paradigms of ecological resilience and recovery.
