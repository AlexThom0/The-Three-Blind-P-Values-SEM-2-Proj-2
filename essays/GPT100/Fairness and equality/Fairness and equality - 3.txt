This disregard for meritocracy gives rise to stagnation, as a lack of fresh perspectives contributes to a resistant culture that fails to adapt. Consequently, the original intentions behind hierarchies are corrupted, transforming a structure meant to elevate others into a system of entitlement. Thus, even well-meaning hierarchies risk becoming counterproductive, undermining their foundational role of facilitating mutual benefit and collective progress. Adaptability must be woven into the fabric of these structures to ensure that they serve their purpose of fostering growth and development for all.
