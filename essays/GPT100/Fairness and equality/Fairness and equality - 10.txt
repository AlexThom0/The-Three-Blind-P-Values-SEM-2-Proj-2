This shift acknowledges the limitations inherent in direct democratic participation, particularly as societal issues become increasingly multifaceted. As the populace grapples with diverse interests and expertise, representatives, ideally chosen for their competence and understanding, must interpret and advocate for the varied perspectives of their constituents. Consequently, the evolution from direct to representative democracy reflects not only a practical adaptation but also a deeper recognition of the nuances of effective governance in a complex world.
