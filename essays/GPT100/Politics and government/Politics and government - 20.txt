As the colonies grew increasingly restless, Nicholson sought to wield authority not just through repression but by promoting a sense of loyalty and mutual benefit. He encouraged local assemblies to engage in dialogue about governance and sought to highlight the advantages of allegiance to Britain. By fostering alliances with influential colonial leaders, he aimed to restore a semblance of peace and establish a more cooperative relationship between colonists and the imperial powers. In doing so, he hoped to mitigate further unrest and reinforce the legitimacy of royal rule.
