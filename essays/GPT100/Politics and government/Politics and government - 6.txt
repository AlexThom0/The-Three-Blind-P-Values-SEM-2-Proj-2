This race to the bottom creates a precarious balance for governments, which must navigate the demands of their citizens while appeasing powerful economic actors. As social unrest grows due to rising inequality and perceived governmental failures, political leaders find themselves trapped in a cycle where the needs of the public clash with the interests of capital. The increasing polarization in political landscapes reflects this tension, resulting in populist movements that exploit discontent and challenge the established order.
