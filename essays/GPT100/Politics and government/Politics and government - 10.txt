Scholars are increasingly interrogating the foundational principles of liberalism, questioning whether they still resonate or if they have become relics of a bygone era. These discussions reveal a growing sense of urgency about the need to redefine the social contract in light of contemporary challenges. Factors such as inequality, identity politics, and climate change have fractured the once-unified liberal narrative, prompting both advocates and opponents to rethink their strategies. The future viability of liberal democracy may depend on its ability to adapt to these pressing realities.
