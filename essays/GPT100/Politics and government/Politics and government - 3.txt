In this respect, Orbán's political maneuvering embodies a broader trend where contemporary leaders adapt historical figures to lend legitimacy to their agendas. By invoking St Stephen, he not only legitimizes authoritarian tactics but also frames them as necessary for national unity in the face of perceived cultural decay. This historical appropriation serves to galvanize support among those who feel threatened by pluralism and globalization, thereby reinforcing a narrative of a besieged Christian identity. The reimagining of St Stephen's legacy exemplifies how past narratives can be leveraged to justify present actions, ultimately reshaping the civic landscape.
