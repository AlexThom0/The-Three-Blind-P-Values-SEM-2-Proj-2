Critics argued that such a framework not only stifled innovative thinking but also led to a simplistic understanding of complex phenomena. As scholars like Fludd and Daneau sought new interpretations influenced by biblical texts, they challenged the notion that empirical observation alone could capture the intricacies of divine creation. Their attempts to synthesize faith with reason opened doors for alternative methodologies that acknowledged the limitations of human understanding while promoting a deeper exploration of nature within a theological context.
