Illustrations became simplistic and bold to withstand the rigors of repeated use, often sacrificing detail for durability. These prints circulated widely among the populace, democratizing access to information and visual culture. As a result, the interaction between art and audience shifted, fueling a demand for more sensational and easily digestible content. This dual production model not only reflected the varied tastes of society but also shaped the future of print, influencing both artists and writers in their approach to craft and communication.
