This separation not only led to a fragmentation of knowledge but also fostered a climate where philosophical inquiry became increasingly introspective and disconnected from empirical grounding. In this environment, questions about meaning, existence, and ethics were debated in abstract terms, often detached from practical realities. As various disciplines emerged, they adopted rigorous methodologies that emphasized empirical research, further widening the chasm between philosophical speculation and scientific practice. The evolution of these fields highlighted the necessity of evidence-based reasoning, leaving philosophy grappling with its identity and relevance in a rapidly advancing intellectual landscape.
