Navigators were not merely passive recipients of knowledge; they actively engaged with the content through exercises that required them to apply theoretical concepts on real voyages. Furthermore, Van den Broucke emphasized the importance of observation, urging sailors to closely monitor celestial movements and the changing positions of stars. By merging theory with practical tasks, he cultivated a culture of experiential learning, which proved crucial for those who relied on accurate navigation to explore uncharted waters and return safely home.
