During this period of intellectual resurrection, Arab scholars meticulously translated and expanded upon Greek texts, creating innovative commentaries and integrating experimental observations. This fusion of ancient knowledge with their own advancements led to significant innovations, such as the mechanical clock and enhanced hydraulic systems. Key figures like Al-Jazari synthesized various disciplines, including engineering and mathematics, laying the groundwork for future mechanical understanding. These efforts not only revived ancient concepts but also paved the way for a more systematic approach to mechanics, ultimately influencing both the scientific community in the Middle Ages and the Renaissance that followed.
