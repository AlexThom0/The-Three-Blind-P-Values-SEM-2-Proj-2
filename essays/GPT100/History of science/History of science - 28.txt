This exploitation not only raises ethical concerns but also diminishes the potential value of citizen science, as participants may feel disconnected from the outcomes of their efforts. Furthermore, the commodification of data undermines the collaborative spirit intended by citizen science initiatives. Instead of fostering a sense of community and shared purpose, current frameworks skew towards profit generation, which can alienate both participants and the scientific community. This shift complicates the interplay between public engagement and scientific integrity, risking a deeper entrenchment of mistrust in the very institutions that seek to restore it.
