Increasingly, scientific knowledge was viewed with suspicion, as the public became aware of its complicity in the machinery of war and oppression. To many, the once-celebrated figures in lab coats now appeared to embody a cold rationality that prioritized efficiency over ethics. This shift in perception raised urgent questions about the role of scientists in society. Were they merely facilitators of technological advancement, or could they serve as guardians of moral responsibility? The dialogue surrounding the ethical implications of scientific research became more crucial than ever, challenging the very foundations of their authority.
