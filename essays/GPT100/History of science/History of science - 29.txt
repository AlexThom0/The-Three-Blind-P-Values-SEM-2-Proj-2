As the integrity of research is compromised, scholars increasingly face a dilemma between adhering to ethical standards and meeting the relentless demands of publication metrics. The internalization of these perverse incentives not only influences individual behavior but also reshapes institutional priorities, where funding bodies and academic journals inadvertently reinforce this cycle. Consequently, the environment becomes one where the pursuit of knowledge is overshadowed by the race for prestige and citation counts. This shift not only discourages innovative thinking but also cultivates an atmosphere of mistrust among researchers, ultimately undermining the foundational values of academia.
