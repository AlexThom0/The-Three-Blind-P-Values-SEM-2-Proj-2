Natural historians and collectors held a dual role as both chroniclers and creators, merging observation with imagination in their pursuit of knowledge. Each incomplete specimen sparked inventive interpretations, leading to an explosion of myths surrounding the natural world. The practice of conflating reality with fantasy became common, resulting in elaborate descriptions that captivated the public's interest. This blending of fact and folklore allowed the creation of a taxonomy that, while often inaccurate, reflected the cultural anxieties and fascinations of the time. Thus, the line between the known and the mysterious blurred, giving rise to an ever-evolving narrative of discovery and wonder.
