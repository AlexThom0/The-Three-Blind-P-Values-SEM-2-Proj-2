Central to this perspective is the belief that our descriptions in quantum mechanics serve not as definitive portrayals of nature but as tools for navigating the complexities of observed interactions. The equations and models we use do not claim to unveil an intrinsic essence of particles; instead, they reflect the limits of our measurements and the uncertainty inherent in the quantum realm. By focusing on the statistical outcomes of experiments rather than the particles themselves, we embrace an understanding that prioritizes observed patterns over speculative ontologies.
