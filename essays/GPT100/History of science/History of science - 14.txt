In this framework, Hume's distinction between impressions and ideas serves not merely as a cornerstone of epistemology but also as a practical tool for evaluating discourse. The immediacy of impressions provides a foundation for intellectual inquiry; ideas derived from these impressions carry weight only to the extent they faithfully reflect that original experience. By scrutinizing the origin of our concepts, we can identify when language strays into ambiguity. It becomes essential to trace back these terms to their experiential roots, ensuring that philosophical discussions remain grounded in tangible understanding rather than abstract speculation. This rigorous inquiry leads to greater clarity in philosophical discourse.
