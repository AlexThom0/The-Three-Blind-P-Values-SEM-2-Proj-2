Carnegie’s obsession with these ancient giants spurred not only his personal collection but also a broader societal fascination that permeated the art and literature of the time. The rivalry for the largest or most complete skeleton became symbolic of larger cultural currents, reflecting the American ethos of expansion and competition. Museums transformed from mere exhibition spaces into battlegrounds for prestige, where paleontologists raced to outdo one another while shaping public perception of these magnificent creatures. This fervor ultimately laid the groundwork for paleontology as a significant scientific discipline.
