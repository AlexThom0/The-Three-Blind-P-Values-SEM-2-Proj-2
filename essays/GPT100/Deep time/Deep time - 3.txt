Fire, unlike ice, often eludes immediate perception. It flickers, consumes, and vanishes, leaving traces rather than tangible forms. As ecosystems respond to the Fire Age, we witness pervasive landscape transformations, marked by scorched earth and altered habitats. Species once adapted to cooler climates struggle, while others thrive in the newly imposed warmth. The very fabric of life is frayed, revealing connections previously obscured by the stability that ice provided. In this new reality, the implications of fire weave through every aspect, reshaping not just the environment but also human societies and their future prospects.
