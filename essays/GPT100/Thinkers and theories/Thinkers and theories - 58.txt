This intellectual environment created fertile ground for the ideas that shaped Lucretius’ own work, aligning with a broader trend of rejection toward more cosmologically complex explanations. Furthermore, the potential for aligning nature with human perception and experience would have greatly appealed to Lucretius. By advocating for a flat Earth, he not only adhered to Epicurean doctrine but also fostered a sense of accessibility in philosophical discourse, making abstract concepts easier for the common person to grasp and engage with.
