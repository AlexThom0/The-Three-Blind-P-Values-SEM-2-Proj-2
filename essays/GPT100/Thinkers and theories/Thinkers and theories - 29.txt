She argues that recognizing individuality should lead not just to a recognition of personality but to an understanding of the deeper suffering and needs that each person embodies. By delving into the existential struggles inherent in the human condition, she emphasizes that ethical relations must transcend mere acknowledgment of presence. This deeper engagement calls for an empathetic response informed by a shared experience of vulnerability and strength.
