Each scientific advance is intertwined with societal values and cultural narratives, reflecting the biases and priorities of its time. This intertwining leads to the realization that what is considered 'fact' is often shaped by the context in which inquiry occurs. Furthermore, actor-network theory broadens our understanding by revealing the complex networks of relationships that influence knowledge production, thus challenging the notion of an unbiased pursuit of truth. Such an approach not only democratizes knowledge but also emphasizes the responsibility that comes with its construction.
