In his view, the allure of pleasure often distracted individuals from virtuous pursuits essential for a harmonious state. Thus, his disdain for the lifestyle he observed was not just personal—he saw it as symptomatic of deeper philosophical failures. The roots of political instability, in Plato's philosophy, could be traced back to a society more invested in ephemeral delights than in the cultivation of wisdom and justice. This understanding shaped his subsequent engagements and writings on ethical leadership.
