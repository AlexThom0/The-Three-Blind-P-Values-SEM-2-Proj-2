His essay critiqued the convoluted nature of existing laws, advocating for a system grounded in rationality and fairness. Beccaria proposed that laws should be transparent, aligning with the principles of enlightenment thought, which emphasized reason as the foundation for justice. This approach not only aimed to demystify legal language but also sought to empower citizens, enabling them to understand and engage with the legal system. Such philosophical underpinnings were essential to catalyzing the necessary shifts in penal policy and societal attitudes toward justice.
