This disillusionment with myth mirrored broader societal shifts, as critiques emerged around the very foundations of religion, morality, and art. Writers began to explore how these myths, once serving as authoritative narratives, became subjects of ridicule and skepticism. In the process, they not only dismantled previously revered stories but also raised critical questions about the nature of truth and belief in a world increasingly defined by reason. The allure of myth as a pedagogical tool faced a dramatic reevaluation.
