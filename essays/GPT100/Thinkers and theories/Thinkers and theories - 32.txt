By asserting that women should be educated, Manon advocated for their intellectual empowerment as a means to elevate the entire social fabric. This duality highlighted her complex view: while she adhered to traditional gender roles, she also challenged the notion that women's capabilities should remain unrecognized. In her eyes, an educated woman could contribute significantly to the moral and civic development of her family and, by extension, the community at large. This perspective reveals her nuanced understanding of societal dynamics.
