Holst’s emphasis on education as a foundational aspect of vocation aligns with the broader philosophical assertions regarding the potential of women in intellectual spheres. She contended that denying women access to educational opportunities inherently undermines societal progress and the enrichment of knowledge. This perspective was revolutionary, as it not only challenged existing norms but also advocated for a re-evaluation of what constitutes a true vocation. In doing so, Holst initiated a discourse that questioned the restrictive definitions of capability and intellect that were prevalent among her male peers, positioning women as not just passive subjects but active contributors to the ongoing pursuit of knowledge and societal betterment.
