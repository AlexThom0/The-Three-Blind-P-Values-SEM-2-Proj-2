Critics argued that their participation diluted the rigor of academic thought, relegating them to roles of passive consumers rather than active contributors. This ongoing narrative not only marginalized women from intellectual circles but also perpetuated a cultural ethos that deemed highbrow philosophical engagement as an exclusive domain for men.
