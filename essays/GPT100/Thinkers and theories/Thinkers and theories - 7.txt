Dewey’s influence on Ambedkar was not merely academic; it was transformative. The core tenets of pragmatism, particularly the emphasis on democratic ideals and the importance of experiential learning, resonated deeply with Ambedkar’s own struggles against caste discrimination and social injustice. This ideological alignment provided Ambedkar with philosophical grounding as he crafted his vision for social reform in India. Dewey’s views on democracy as a way of life echoed in Ambedkar’s assertion that social change must be rooted in lived experiences rather than abstract theories. This connection fostered a mutual respect that shaped Ambedkar’s philosophical and practical frameworks for advocacy.
