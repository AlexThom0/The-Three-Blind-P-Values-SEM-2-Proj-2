Bateson began to recognize that rationalistic approaches to science often overlooked the complexities of human experience. In shifting his focus, he embraced a more holistic view, one that acknowledged the interplay of biology, society, and individual perception. This new framework allowed him to explore the nuances of human behaviors and relationships, drawing connections between actions and their broader implications within ecosystems. Such insights would not only inform his research but also challenge prevailing assumptions about the nature of knowledge itself.
