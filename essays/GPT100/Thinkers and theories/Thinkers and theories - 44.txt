McTaggart’s challenging conclusion forces us to reconsider fundamental notions of existence and change. If time itself is an illusion, then our understanding of events, causation, and even personal experience must be reevaluated. This brings into question the nature of reality itself and whether we can ever truly comprehend it without the distortions introduced by our temporal framework. Thus, the implications of his argument extend far beyond mere chronology.
