The advent of gas lighting in the 19th century marked a pivotal transition, illuminating not just streets but reshaping social dynamics. Public spaces became more accessible after dark, encouraging nightlife and urban activities previously constrained by the fear of darkness. This democratization of light allowed diverse social classes to move freely at night, igniting new forms of interaction and community. Light became a symbol of progress, intertwining with economic growth and urbanization, sealing an intrinsic bond between brightness and social cohesion.
