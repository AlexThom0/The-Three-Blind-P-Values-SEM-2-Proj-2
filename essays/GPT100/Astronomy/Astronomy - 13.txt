At this point, theories about cosmic evolution suggest a range of potential outcomes based on various factors, including the precise amount of dark energy influencing the Universe's expansion. One possibility is a scenario known as "heat death," where the Universe continues to expand indefinitely, resulting in a state of maximum entropy. In such a reality, galaxies drift further apart, stars extinguish, and essential processes like star formation cease altogether. In this bleak landscape, any remnants of life would struggle against an increasingly desolate environment, highlighting the fragility of existence amidst an ever-expanding void.
