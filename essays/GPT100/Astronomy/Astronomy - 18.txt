This early innovation was the result of a decade-long quest to overcome the limitations of traditional mirror-making techniques, which often produced imprecise surfaces unsuitable for modern astronomical applications. By experimenting with different materials and methods, Angel discovered how to achieve the necessary balance between thickness and curvature, crucial for enhancing light collection. The revolutionary approach significantly reduced production time and improved the accuracy of mirrors, instilling newfound confidence in the potential for giant telescope designs. As the technology evolved, so too did the ambitious vision of astronomers eager to push the boundaries of space exploration.
