Desch's model suggests that, rather than merely searching for water, future efforts in the search for extraterrestrial life should focus on the geological and chemical conditions that promote essential nutrient cycling. In essence, even if a planet appears to be water-rich, without the right geological processes, it may lack the fundamental ingredients necessary to sustain biological activity. This nuanced understanding reshapes our criteria for potential habitability in exoplanet exploration.
