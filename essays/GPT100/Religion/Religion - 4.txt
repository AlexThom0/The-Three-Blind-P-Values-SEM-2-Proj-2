Christian vision emphasized not just the physical space of worship but also the entire community’s engagement in this spiritual cleansing. As church leaders wielded their authority, they effectively transformed these spaces into strongholds for devotees, reinforcing the notion that the divine presence sanctified the environment. This ritualistic redefinition flourished not only in temples but also extended to everyday life, encouraging believers to abandon old practices and embrace new forms of devotion. The act of consecration involved more than architecture; it called for a collective shift in understanding spirituality and the realms inhabited by both the divine and demonic.
