Inoue's philosophy of revolution hinged on a paradoxical use of violence as a tool for greater compassion. He envisioned a transformed society where the act of assassination would be a necessary evil, purging the corrupt elite with minimal collateral damage. This utilitarian approach aimed not only to strike fear into those in power but also to inspire a broader ideological awakening among the masses. By framing their actions within a narrative of selflessness, Inoue and his followers sought to position themselves as martyrs for the greater good, grappling with the ethical implications of such deliberate violence in pursuit of their ideals.
