This exchange transforms social connections into networks of transactional autonomy, where relationships are redefined by market dynamics rather than communal bonds. As commodities pass between hands, they accumulate stories and meanings that transcend their original use-value, yet strip away personal connections. This commodification creates a façade of individuality, obscuring the social ties woven into their production and distribution. In a system that values profit over human experience, the essence of what it means to relate to one another diminishes, leaving behind mere transactional echoes devoid of deeper significance.
