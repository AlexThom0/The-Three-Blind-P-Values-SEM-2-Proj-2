Instruction wasn’t merely academic; it also sought to embed the principles of discipline, hard work, and morality central to Calvinist thought. This educational framework provided a foundation for both religious and civic life, empowering individuals with knowledge that reinforced their identity as Calvinists. Congregational members often took on teaching roles, creating an environment that emphasized communal responsibility and collaboration. Through these efforts, the Dutch Calvinists effectively expanded their influence and established a legacy that would endure far beyond their initial settlements.
