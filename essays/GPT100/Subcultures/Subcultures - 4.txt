This image of the salaryman—as both an emblem of corporate loyalty and a symbol of cultural insularity—was juxtaposed against the backdrop of an increasingly globalized economy. The rise of Japan's business dominance made it a target of both admiration and disdain, amplifying the perception of a nation that was both thriving and threatening.
