This shift not only shaped individual identities but also altered collective consciousness, reinforcing a narrative where worth was measured by material possessions. Consequently, objects became transient, desired for their novelty rather than their utility. This relentless pursuit of the new often overshadowed the environmental and social costs associated with excess production and waste. As the cycle of consumption intensified, the notion of sustainability began to emerge, challenging the once-accepted norm of disposability and raising crucial questions about the future of consumerism in society.
