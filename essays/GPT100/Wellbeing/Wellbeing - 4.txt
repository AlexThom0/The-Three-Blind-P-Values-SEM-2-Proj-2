Beyond the acute trauma, patients often report a disconnection from reality while under heavy sedation, creating a discord between their conscious memories and fragmented experiences. This phenomenon raises troubling questions about the ethics of sedation protocols and the balance between relief and the potential for long-term psychological harm. As these patients are rendered vulnerable by their condition, what safeguards exist to prevent such distressing outcomes? The challenge lies not only in the immediate management of their physical health but also in addressing the emotional scars that can linger long after the tubes are removed.
