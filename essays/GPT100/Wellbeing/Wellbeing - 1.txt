According to Giorgi, a more rigorous adherence to the philosophical underpinnings is essential to preserve phenomenology's integrity, emphasizing a systematic investigation into the core structures of consciousness. This insistence on maintaining a foundational philosophical perspective raises pertinent questions about the accessibility and applicability of phenomenological methods in nursing research, particularly when practitioners may lack the philosophical training necessary to engage with these complex concepts effectively. Thus, the debate centers around the balance between rigor and practical utility.
