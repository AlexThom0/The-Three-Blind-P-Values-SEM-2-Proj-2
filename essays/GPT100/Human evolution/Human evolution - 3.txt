Analyses of isotopic signatures suggest that early hominins potentially exploited diverse environments, including mixed and transitional zones where forest and savannah intersected. This versatility in diet would have provided crucial adaptive advantages, allowing these ancestors to thrive in varied ecological niches. The reliance on a broader range of food sources not only suggests a more intricate relationship with their habitats but also prompts a reevaluation of what drove their evolutionary trajectory during critical periods of climate change and environmental flux.
