Neanderthals, however, exhibited a complex social structure that has only recently begun to be appreciated. Evidence from archaeological sites suggests they engaged in cooperative behaviors, shared resources, and perhaps even cared for their injured or elderly. These findings challenge the notion of Neanderthals as mere primitive or solitary beings, painting a picture of them as capable of sophisticated group dynamics. Furthermore, recent analysis of their tool-making techniques indicates a higher degree of cognitive ability than previously attributed. Engaging in symbolic activities, such as creating art or using ornaments, hints at a cultural depth that rivals early Homo sapiens.
