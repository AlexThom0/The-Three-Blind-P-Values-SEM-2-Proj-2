While examining the skeletal remains and genetic material, researchers must also account for the potential for variation in these classifications. The complexities of biological sex and the social constructs surrounding gender identity suggest that Neanderthals may have experienced their own fluidity in these categories. Archaeological evidence, such as burial practices and material culture, offers further insight into how Neanderthals might have expressed identity. Artifacts found at various sites indicate that social roles could have crossed boundaries defined by strictly enforced norms, allowing for a richer understanding of their societal structures and interpersonal relationships.
