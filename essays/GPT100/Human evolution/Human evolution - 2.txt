The bird must invest an incredible amount of energy into producing this substantial egg, which necessitates a careful balance between resource allocation and her own health. This investment exemplifies a critical trade-off: the potential for a more developed offspring at hatching comes at the cost of the mother's mobility and energy. Such evolutionary strategies highlight the diverse ways species adapt their reproductive methods to maximize offspring survival while managing their own well-being.
