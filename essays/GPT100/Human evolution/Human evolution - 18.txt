Unlike other primates, humans have developed intricate familial and social ties that often transcend mere genetic ties. Hence, the motivations behind violence and dominance in human interactions cannot be reduced to simplistic biological imperatives. Cultural factors, individual experiences, and social dynamics play crucial roles in shaping behaviors. When we associate human actions too closely with those observed in animal species, we risk overlooking the profound influence of context, morality, and personal agency in our relationships and societal conflicts.
