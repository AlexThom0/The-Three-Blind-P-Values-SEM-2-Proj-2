The study of primate behavior provided insights into cooperation, competition, and bonding, revealing that these dynamics were not exclusive to humanity but rather a shared trait across species. Such findings challenged the simplistic narratives of superiority and progress that often accompanied discussions of human evolution. By examining the complexities of primate societies, researchers began to question the linear models of development that disregarded cultural variation and historical context. This shift prompted a reevaluation of assumptions about progress and the nature of human social organization.
