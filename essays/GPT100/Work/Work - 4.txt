This efficiency is often touted as a key advantage of the capitalist system over more centralized economic models. Yet, this approach creates a paradox: while firms are intended to operate in the interest of profit maximization, the hierarchical structure can stifle innovation and employee engagement. In this tension between individual autonomy and organizational control, we find a critical examination of how power dynamics within firms reflect broader societal values around ownership and governance. Understanding this dynamic is essential to grasp how the capitalist framework shapes workplace experiences.
