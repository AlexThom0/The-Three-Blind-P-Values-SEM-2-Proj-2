This freedom allows humans to infuse their works with meaning, reflecting personal experiences, emotions, and intellectual exploration. Unlike instinctual behaviours that govern non-human creatures, human artistic expression emerges from a complex interplay of culture, society, and individual agency, resulting in a diverse range of outputs that can challenge conventions and provoke thought. Thus, the intrinsic value of art lies not solely in its aesthetic appeal but in the intentionality behind its creation.
