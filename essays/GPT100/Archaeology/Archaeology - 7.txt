Many scholars believed that uncovering physical evidence of biblical narratives would not only validate Scripture but also unite a fragmented society around a shared history. This approach often led to selective interpretations of findings, emphasizing artifacts that resonated with biblical accounts while overlooking those that contradicted them. The quest for a tangible connection to the sacred texts fueled a dynamic interplay between archaeology and belief, drawing both admiration and criticism from different factions within the newly emerging academic landscape. The implications of such work extended far beyond the sands of time.
