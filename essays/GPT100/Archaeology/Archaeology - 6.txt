The details in her features reflect not only the skill of Thutmose’s artisans but also the cultural significance of their subject. Unlike the frequently represented deities of the time, the bust of Nefertiti signifies an intimate glimpse into the aesthetic ideals of the period, mixing realism with idealism. Such choices in representation suggest a deliberate effort to elevate the queen's status, reinforcing her divine connections and presence. This approach reinforces the notion that works created in Thutmose’s workshop served dual purposes—mortal and immortal.
