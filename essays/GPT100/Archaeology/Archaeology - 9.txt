Early hominins may have utilized organic materials like animal hides or plant fibers that left no trace over millennia. Additionally, environmental pressures, such as fluctuating climates, may have prompted the adoption of clothing far earlier than the lice data suggests. Thus, while the genetic evidence provides a clear timeline for modern clothing, it must be integrated with archaeological findings to construct a fuller picture of early human adaptation and innovation regarding clothing.
