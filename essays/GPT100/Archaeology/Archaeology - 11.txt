This interplay between materiality and human cognition is not merely theoretical; it is evident in various historical contexts. For instance, the ancient Roman practices of collecting and storing goods reflect a complex relationship with wealth and power. The items held in storage were not just commodities, but symbols of status, aspiration, and identity that influenced social hierarchies. As elite Romans amassed possessions from conquered lands, these objects became agents of cultural exchange. They not only enriched personal lives but also raised questions about societal values, creating ripples through the collective consciousness that shaped public and private realms alike.
