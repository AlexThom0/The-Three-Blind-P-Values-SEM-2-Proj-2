The limited number of participants facilitated direct engagement and discussions, allowing decisions to reflect the collective will more accurately. Moreover, such small-scale governance structures enabled a greater sense of accountability and transparency among leaders, as community members were more likely to be familiar with one another. This intimacy fostered a participatory culture, where every voice could contribute to the dialogue, influencing outcomes in ways that would be less feasible in larger, more bureaucratic systems. Thus, the size of the polity fundamentally shaped its democratic processes.
