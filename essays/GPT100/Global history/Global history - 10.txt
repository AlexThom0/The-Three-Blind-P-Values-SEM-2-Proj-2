Unlike the more localized disruptions of previous pandemics, COVID-19 exposed how global interdependencies can amplify vulnerabilities. Nations previously insulated by geographic and economic strength found themselves unable to respond effectively to a crisis that was as much about public health as it was about economic stability. This intersection of health and economics invites a reassessment of modern resilience and fragility, suggesting that underestimating potential threats in times of apparent strength may lead to dire consequences.
