The transition from rural to urban life was marked not only by the imposition of factory schedules but also by a profound disconnection from traditional community ties. Families that once shared daily routines around agricultural cycles now found themselves at the mercy of erratic factory hours. This upheaval disrupted not only individual lives but also the social fabric, as evenings once filled with communal gatherings were replaced by fatigue and isolation. The relentless demands of industrialization reshaped their identities, challenging them to navigate a world where time had become a commodity, owned by the rhythms of production rather than the natural cycles they once knew.
