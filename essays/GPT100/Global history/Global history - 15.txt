This shift mirrored a growing moral reckoning with addiction and exploitation, pushing colonial powers toward a re-evaluation of their roles. Underneath the surface, conflicts emerged among various factions within these administrations, reflecting tension between profit-oriented policies and emerging humanitarian concerns. A campaign against opium thus not only aimed to cure societies of their drug dependence but also to reassert colonial legitimacy in a rapidly changing world, where the moral authority of European nations was increasingly questioned both at home and abroad.
