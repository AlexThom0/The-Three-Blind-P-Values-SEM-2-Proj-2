The influence of African empires, such as the Mali, Songhai, and Great Zimbabwe, extended across vast regions, establishing connections with Europe and Asia long before colonial ambitions reshaped the landscape. They forged alliances, traded goods, and exchanged knowledge, illustrating a rich tapestry of interactions. To ignore these historical realities is to overlook the active role Africans played in shaping their own destinies and those of neighboring regions. Such narratives challenge the simplistic view of colonialism as a unilateral imposition from Europe.
