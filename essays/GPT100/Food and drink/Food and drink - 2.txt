This transformation coincided with broader cultural shifts, where food and drink began to signify status and refinement. The consumption of chocolate was not merely about indulgence; it became a performance of wealth and sophistication. As recipes spread and evolved, the drink morphed into a symbol of privilege, often served in ornate vessels that further emphasized its luxurious nature. The way chocolate was prepared and presented now conveyed messages about the household's sophistication, distinguishing those who embraced this trend from those who could not partake in its lavish rituals.
