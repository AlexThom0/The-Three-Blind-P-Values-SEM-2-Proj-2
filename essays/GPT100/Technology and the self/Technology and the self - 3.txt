The challenge lies in designing tools that enhance our connections rather than simplify them into mere data exchanges. For instance, incorporating elements that resonate with our physical experiences can foster a sense of presence and engagement, allowing participants to feel more connected to each other and their environment. This approach values the richness of interpersonal interactions, recognizing that meaning emerges from our shared experiences and the contexts that shape them. Thus, the success of these technologies is measured not solely by functionality but by their capacity to uphold the intricacies of our relationships with one another and the world around us.
