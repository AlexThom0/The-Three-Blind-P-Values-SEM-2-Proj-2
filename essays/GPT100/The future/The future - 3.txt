This optimism not only shaped scientific discourse but also deeply influenced society's understanding of biodiversity and the resilience of nature. It fostered a worldview in which the cycles of creation and extinction were intertwined, leading to the conviction that all life was destined to reemerge, albeit after a protracted interlude. Such views highlighted a profound ambivalence towards both progress and decay, as humanity sought comfort in the idea of nature's inexhaustible creativity.
