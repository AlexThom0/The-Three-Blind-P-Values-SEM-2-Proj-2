Critics often argue that while SSRIs may alleviate symptoms for some, they can fail to provide relief for others, leading to frustration and a sense of hopelessness. This inconsistency is compounded by the neurochemical complexity of depression, which cannot be entirely addressed through pharmacological means. Furthermore, the long-term implications of SSRI use, including potential side effects and withdrawal challenges, add layers of complexity to their application. As such, individual responses to treatment reveal the necessity for tailored approaches in managing mental health.
