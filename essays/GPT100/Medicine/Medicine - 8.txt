Additionally, antibiotics offered the promise of a broader spectrum of action, targeting multiple bacterial species simultaneously. This versatility was particularly appealing to physicians facing the challenge of polymicrobial infections, where several bacterial types could coexist. While phage therapy had shown potential, particularly in certain Eastern European countries, the production and regulatory hurdles associated with phages made them less accessible for widespread clinical use. Consequently, the appeal of antibiotics grew stronger, cementing their place in modern medical practice.
