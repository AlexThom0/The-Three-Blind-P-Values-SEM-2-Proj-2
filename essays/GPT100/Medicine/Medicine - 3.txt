Early anatomists like Vesalius laid the groundwork, yet it was the successors in the 17th and 18th centuries who began to piece together the significance of air and respiration. The refinement of experiments, such as those conducted by Robert Boyle and later Joseph Priestley, shaped a new understanding of gases. These efforts catalyzed discussions about air's composition, leading to a gradual acknowledgment of its crucial role in sustaining life. Each breakthrough built upon prior knowledge, paving the way for a holistic interpretation of respiratory mechanics.
