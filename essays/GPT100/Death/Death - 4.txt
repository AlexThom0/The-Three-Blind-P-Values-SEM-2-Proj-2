They argue that by acknowledging mortality as a constant aspect of life, one cultivates a resilience that enables acceptance rather than raw sorrow. This preparation transforms grief into a more profound understanding of love's impermanence. Therefore, for those who embrace this philosophy, the practice of acknowledging death and loss becomes a vital part of nurturing genuine affection, enhancing their ability to respond to loss with a tempered but authentic sense of mourning.
