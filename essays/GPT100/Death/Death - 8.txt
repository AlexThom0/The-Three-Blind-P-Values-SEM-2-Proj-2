Individuals sought solace and hope, longing to bridge the gap between the living and the dead. The mediums, sensing this vulnerability, cleverly exploited psychological cues and emotional triggers, often creating elaborate theatrical experiences that captivated their audiences. This environment of credulity allowed for the rapid spread of both dubious claims and genuine belief, a juxtaposition that both fascinated and horrified those who later grappled with the implications of such deceptions for society’s understanding of grief and the afterlife.
