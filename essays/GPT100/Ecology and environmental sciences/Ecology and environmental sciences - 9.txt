Urban environments, while offering new opportunities for survival, also strip away the natural behaviors that define them. Their families may be disrupted, and their roaming instincts compromised, as they navigate fragmented habitats and human developments. Such pressures raise critical ethical considerations about our coexistence with wildlife. Understanding these dynamics can foster a deeper respect for their struggle and the complexity of their experience amid our human landscape.
