This drive for uniformity led to a loss of genetic diversity, as breeders began to prioritize characteristics that would yield the most profitable birds. The result was a push toward a narrow definition of the "ideal" chicken, sidelining traditional breeds in favor of those engineered for scalability and efficiency. This shift not only affected the physical attributes of chickens but altered the landscape of poultry farming itself, compelling many small farmers to abandon their heritage breeds in favor of high-yield hybrids.
