This disaster unfolded as fragmented landscapes, altered by years of anthropogenic interventions, converged with the explosive potential of intense drought. The results were catastrophic, not just in terms of economic impact but also in long-term ecological ramifications. Species adapted over millennia were faced with a rapidly changing environment, struggling against a fire regime far removed from their evolutionary history. This illustrates the dire consequences of disjointed conservation efforts and misguided land management practices.
