The romanticization of history prioritizes idyllic imagery over troubling truths, inviting us to selectively remember. This distortion cultivates an image of a unified national identity, while simultaneously perpetuating divisions borne from colonial practices and socio-economic disparities. The allure of a bucolic existence fuels a narrative that, though comforting, may hinder our ability to confront present-day challenges and reckon with those elements of our heritage that do not fit neatly into the fantasy. Embracing a complex history, with all its imperfections, offers a more authentic connection to our identity.
