Each creature serves not only as a character but as a mirror reflecting our fears, desires, and vulnerabilities. The act of observing animals can become a lens through which we scrutinize our own behaviors and relationships. Just as Lévi-Strauss highlights, animals are not mere subjects of desire or affection; they represent the intricate connections we forge with the natural world and the subconscious truths we often overlook. In this blend of observation and introspection, the boundaries between human and animal blur, enriching our understanding of both.
