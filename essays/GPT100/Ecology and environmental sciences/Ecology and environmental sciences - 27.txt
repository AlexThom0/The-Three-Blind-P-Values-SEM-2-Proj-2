This raises an urgent question about our ethical responsibilities: if we engineer our climate, how do we account for the countless species and ecosystems that exist independently of human intervention? By prioritizing human needs and desires, could we inadvertently trigger ecological imbalances we do not yet understand? The reliance on technology to fix problems of our own making runs the risk of deepening our estrangement from the very ecosystems we seek to modify. As we forge ahead, the imperative to reflect on the interconnectedness of all life becomes increasingly critical.
