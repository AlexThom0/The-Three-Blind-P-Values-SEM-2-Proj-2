Yet, this perspective underestimated the role of fire as a natural catalyst for renewal and biodiversity. Research increasingly demonstrates that fire plays a crucial role in maintaining healthy forest ecosystems, promoting the growth of fire-adapted species and opening up the canopy to sunlight. The unintended consequences of suppressing fire not only transformed the landscape but also disrupted the delicate balance that existed between the giant sequoias and their environment, leaving them vulnerable to infestations and disease that thrived in overcrowded conditions.
