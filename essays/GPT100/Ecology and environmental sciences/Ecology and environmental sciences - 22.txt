As fish populations dwindled, the livelihoods tied to the ocean began to unravel, revealing a stark contrast between the once-thriving community and the encroaching economic despair. The influx of large-scale operations intensified competition, pushing small-scale fishermen into the shadows. Yet, some locals adapted by shifting their focus to alternative means of support, such as eco-tourism, reimagining their roles as guardians of the coast instead of mere harvesters. This shift, albeit difficult, sparked a movement aimed at sustainable practices, suggesting that a balance between environmental stewardship and economic survival might be possible, though it required deep systemic changes and community resilience.
