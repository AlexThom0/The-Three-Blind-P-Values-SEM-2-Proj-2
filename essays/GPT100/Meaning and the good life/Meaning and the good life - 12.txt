Each new scenario a child encounters can trigger a deep discomfort, forcing us to confront our uncertainties and fears. This instinct to protect can inadvertently stifle a child's natural exploration, curbing their ability to build resilience through experience. As they navigate the world, children need spaces where they can take risks and learn from their mistakes, even if that means letting go of our tightly held beliefs about safety. Embracing this chaotic potential encourages growth, both for them and for us.
