the human experience has always been entwined with notions of mortality. From ancient philosophies to modern narratives, our understanding of life and death shapes our values and aspirations. This interplay prompts a reevaluation of what it means to exist meaningfully. If eternal life loses its appeal upon deeper contemplation, one might question whether our fears and desires regarding death stem from an innate misunderstanding of existence itself. Indeed, as we confront the limits of our lifespan, the urgency to create and connect intensifies, revealing that our mortality may, paradoxically, enhance the richness of our experiences and relationships.
