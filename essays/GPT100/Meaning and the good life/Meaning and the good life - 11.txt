As Ghosh points out, the prosperity of a few hinges on the disenfranchisement of many, further compounding existing inequalities. The narrative of progress is thus fundamentally flawed, as it implies that the wealth produced in the West is a direct consequence of enlightenment values rather than the systematic subjugation of others. By ignoring this historical context, we risk perpetuating the same cycles of exclusion that shaped modernity.
