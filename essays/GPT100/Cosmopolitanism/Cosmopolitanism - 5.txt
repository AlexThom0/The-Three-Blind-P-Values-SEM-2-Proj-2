This distinction underscores the complexity of how we perceive and categorize the world around us. In the case of dehumanisation, individuals may cling to essentialist views that emphasize fixed traits over the dynamic, multifaceted nature of human identity. When people reduce others to mere characteristics, they disregard the rich tapestry of experiences, behaviors, and emotions that shape an individual, leading to a dangerous oversimplification that jeopardizes empathy and understanding. The danger of such reductionism cannot be overstated.
