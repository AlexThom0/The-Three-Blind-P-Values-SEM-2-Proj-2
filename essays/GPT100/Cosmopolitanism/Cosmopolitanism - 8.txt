To achieve this, Hierocles suggested practical strategies: we should consciously expand our empathy, engage with those farthest from us, and actively participate in the welfare of distant communities. This process demands a deliberate effort to challenge our instincts, embracing the discomfort that comes with broadening our moral focus. Through shared experiences and mutual interests, we begin to dissolve the boundaries separating us from others, facilitating a transformation of our relationships. By recognizing the interconnectedness of human experience, we foster a sense of responsibility that transcends local concerns, gradually reshaping our worldview to encompass all of humanity.
