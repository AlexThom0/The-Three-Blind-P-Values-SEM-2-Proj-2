We must consider the complexities involved in creating such a global governance structure. The historical precedents of international cooperation reveal that unity often emerges from protracted negotiations rather than hasty agreements. Misplaced trust amongst nations, particularly those with divergent political ideologies, could lead to a fragile arrangement, easily disrupted by internal dissent or external pressures. Moreover, entrenched interests may resist ceding sovereignty, further complicating the path toward effective governance. In a world where nuclear capabilities are intertwined with national identities, finding a solution requires not just a theoretical framework, but also a deep understanding of the historical tensions that shape state interactions.
