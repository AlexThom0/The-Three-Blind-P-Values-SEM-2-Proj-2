However, Kosawa proposed that a more profound guilt stems from unresolved familial bonds and societal expectations, which can shape religious experiences. This deeper guilt is not merely about compliance but reflects a search for authenticity in one’s beliefs. By engaging with these concepts, Kosawa aimed to expand the discourse on religion’s psychological underpinnings, suggesting that understanding such guilt could lead to a richer, more nuanced appreciation of faith beyond Freud's limitations.
