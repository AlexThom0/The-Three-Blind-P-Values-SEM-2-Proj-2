However, this overarching trend doesn’t diminish the concerns associated with particular therapeutic practices or the experiences of specific populations. Many clients report feeling unheard or invalidated during therapy, particularly those from marginalized backgrounds. These negative experiences can stem from a lack of cultural competence among practitioners or from rigid adherence to methodologies that do not align with clients’ lived realities. Therefore, while psychotherapy can be effective, it is crucial to address the discrepancies in therapeutic outcomes to ensure equitable mental health care for all individuals.
