In spite of the ambitious ideals, many asylums faced insurmountable challenges that hindered their ability to fulfill their intended purposes. The influx of patients, driven by both societal stigma and economic hardship, placed immense pressure on available resources. This resulted in a fragmented institution where the promise of individualized care often succumbed to the harsh realities of overcrowding and underfunding. As these facilities struggled to maintain any semblance of order, the very principles of moral treatment, meant to engender respect and dignity, began to erode. Consequently, the environment that was envisioned as nurturing became, for many, a reliving of trauma rather than a path to recovery.
