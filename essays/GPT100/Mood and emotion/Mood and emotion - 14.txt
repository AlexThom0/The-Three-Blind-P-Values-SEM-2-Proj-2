In the first analysis, a noticeable trend emerged, indicating that individuals identified as highly creative often faced a higher prevalence of various mood disorders, such as depression and anxiety. This correlation prompts a deeper examination of the underlying mechanisms that might tie creativity to emotional vulnerability.
