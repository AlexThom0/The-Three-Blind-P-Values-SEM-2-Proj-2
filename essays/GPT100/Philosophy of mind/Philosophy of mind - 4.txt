Unlike other animals that adhere to instinctual feeding habits, humans infuse their relationship with food with meaning, tradition, and identity. This variability complicates any attempt to reduce human nutrition to a simple biological framework. Instead, what is deemed 'normal' or 'defective' in human eating patterns often reflects broader societal values.
