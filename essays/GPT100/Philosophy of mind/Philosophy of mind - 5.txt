This allure stems from the argument's capacity to provoke deep introspection about consciousness and its relationship to the physical world. Advocates for the zombie hypothesis often point to thought experiments as a means to explore what consciousness entails. By positing a world where physical processes align perfectly without any subjective experience, they challenge the intuition that consciousness is an inevitable consequence of having a body. The urge to separate the tangible from the intangible becomes a focal point of inquiry, raising essential questions about the nature of self-awareness and the qualitative aspects of experience. Such complexities complicate the neatness of the original assertion.
