Are practitioners seeking genuine self-understanding, or merely a quick fix to their contemporary stresses? The integration of mindfulness into therapeutic settings may inadvertently promote a superficial engagement with spirituality, stripping away the profound philosophical underpinnings that define Buddhist practice. As mindfulness continues to gain traction in various sectors of society, it challenges us to reflect on whether the practice can coexist with its historical roots, or if the modern interpretations dilute its original intent.
