By labeling certain conditions as 'mental,' psychiatry inadvertently reinforces stigma and marginalization, particularly for those whose issues might stem from social or environmental contexts rather than purely psychological ones. The reliance on classifications that exclude physical health interpretations can lead to a disconnect in treatment approaches. Thus, understanding mental illness through a more integrated lens could foster better outcomes, promoting a holistic view of well-being that acknowledges both psychological and physical health as interrelated components of patient care.
