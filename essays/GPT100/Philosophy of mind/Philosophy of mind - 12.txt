Understanding how the visual system interprets colour requires an appreciation of the complex interplay between surface properties and light. The brain not only considers the wavelengths reflected by an object but also incorporates contextual information, such as surrounding colours and light sources. This contextual analysis allows it to adapt to varying lighting situations, leading to consistent perceptions of colour. Factors like shadows, highlights, and the colour of adjacent objects all influence how we perceive a specific hue. Consequently, the visual system employs sophisticated processing techniques to achieve a stable perceptual experience despite the variations in external conditions.
