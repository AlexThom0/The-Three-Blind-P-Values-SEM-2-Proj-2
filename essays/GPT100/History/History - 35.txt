The joy derived from cooking and eating became secondary to the pursuit of health and economy. Consequently, culinary traditions began to wane as recipes morphed into formulas that prioritized nutrients over taste. The pleasures associated with diverse flavors and textures were overlooked, leading to a homogenization of cuisine that echoed broader societal shifts toward industrialization and efficiency. The result was a stark tension between culinary pleasure and nutritional imperatives.
