Salisbury predicted a world where the promise of electricity would dismantle the traditional barriers of industry and geography. No longer confined to bustling factories, workers could harness energy from afar, thus fostering individual creativity and entrepreneurship. This shift echoed the broader societal changes ushered in by the telegraph, as people would connect and collaborate irrespective of their physical locations. In this envisioned landscape, rural areas could thrive alongside urban centers, as access to power was democratized. The implications were vast, suggesting a new era of freedom and innovation that could redefine labor and community.
