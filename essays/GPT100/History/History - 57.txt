Critics argued that the immersive nature of novels could induce intense emotional states, causing readers to lose their sense of self and reality. This susceptibility was not confined to women; men, too, were believed to experience destabilizing impacts. The idea that literature could affect one's mental well-being spurred a growing concern about societal norms and individual responsibilities, leading to calls for stricter regulations on published works. As authors explored complex emotional landscapes, they inadvertently contributed to a cultural climate rife with fears about literature's potential dangers.
