This belief extended to various ailments, where symptoms were often attributed to creaturely influences rather than purely physical or environmental factors. Inhabitants of the medieval world understood health within a framework rich with superstition and humor theory, leading them to identify specific foods and behaviors that could either attract or repel these unwelcome guests. As dietary habits were scrutinized for their potential to foster or combat parasitic infections, people became increasingly entangled in the beliefs surrounding bodily integrity and vulnerability. This understanding shaped their medical practices and cultural narratives.
