This blending of medical inquiry and social perception highlighted a shift in understanding mental health, from an isolated affliction to a communal responsibility. As public discourse around insanity evolved, fear and curiosity fueled an unprecedented interest in familial patterns. Such investigations often ignored the complex interplay of environment and psychology, focusing instead on the tangible, calculable aspects of inheritance that could be dissected and duplicated in endless charts and tables.
