While Pericles articulates a vision of civic virtue and collective responsibility, the reality of political life in Athens was rife with inequalities and exclusions, particularly for women and slaves. This disparity raises critical questions about the extent to which these ideals can be genuinely embraced. The idealized portrayal serves to elevate the narrative of democracy, yet it risks neglecting the vital discourse surrounding those who were systematically marginalized. Thus, the oration encapsulates both an aspirational ethos and a fundamental dissonance within the democratic framework itself.
