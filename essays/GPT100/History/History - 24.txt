Propaganda campaigns emphasized valor and collective responsibility, encouraging citizens to remain vigilant and prepared for possible invasion. The government promoted stories of everyday heroes, effectively transforming ordinary citizens into symbols of national strength. Such narratives affirmed the belief that every individual's actions contributed to a larger cause, galvanizing morale during a time of uncertainty. This cultural production of heroism played a crucial role in maintaining the spirit of resistance, further entwining the threat of invasion with a strengthened national identity.
