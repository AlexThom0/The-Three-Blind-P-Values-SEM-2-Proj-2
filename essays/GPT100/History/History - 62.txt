US officials often underestimated the complexities of tribal loyalties in Afghanistan and the sectarian divides in Iraq, which fueled insurgent movements. Instead of fostering inclusive governance, efforts frequently exacerbated existing tensions, alienating local populations. This detachment from ground realities not only hindered military effectiveness but also undermined any prospects for sustainable peace and stability. As a result, the narrative of inevitable victory drove policies that were ultimately detached from the lived experiences of those in these war-torn regions.
