This intersection of psychology and nation-building positioned the mind not only as a subject for academic inquiry but also as a tool for ideological and political engineering. The allure of psychological manipulation drew leaders who believed that reshaping individual thought could fortify national identity and cohesion. In this context, both Sun and Chiang recognized the potential of psychological theories to underpin their revolutionary agendas. As social upheaval swept across China, these leaders sought methods to galvanize their populace, intertwining psychological constructs with political rhetoric to mobilize collective action and instill a renewed sense of national purpose.
