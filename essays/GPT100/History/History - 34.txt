Physicians often linked excessive sexual activity to imbalances in the humours, equating promiscuity with a decline in one's physical and mental well-being. This approach underscored a broader societal anxiety regarding lust and its impact on noble lineages. The narratives surrounding these figures served both as cautionary tales and reflections of cultural values, framing sexual restraint as not only a personal virtue but a necessity for maintaining social order. The intertwining of health and morality in these discussions reveals the complex attitudes toward sexuality in medieval society.
