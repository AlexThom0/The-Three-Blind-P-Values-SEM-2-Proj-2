This dynamic interplay influences which voices are amplified and which are muted, leading to a selective tradition that can either reinforce or challenge prevailing power structures. Acknowledging the diverse array of influences—spiritual and secular—during America's formative years is critical, as it can illuminate the various contributors to its complex identity. The erasure of certain groups, particularly in matters of faith, not only distorts historical understanding but also impacts contemporary debates about inclusion and representation in democratic discourse. Thus, the ramifications of this selective memory extend far beyond mere academic discussion.
