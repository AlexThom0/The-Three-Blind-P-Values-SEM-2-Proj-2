The systematic legal framework established by Charles II’s administration not only facilitated the expansion of slavery but also laid the groundwork for a political economy deeply intertwined with human exploitation. The colonial leaders, empowered by royal decree, sought to maximize economic gain through the commodification of labor. This led to the institutionalization of slavery as a normal practice, with implications that reached far beyond superficial governance. Even as thinkers like Locke would later grapple with notions of liberty and rights, the prevailing economic interests created a stark contradiction; the flourishing of colonial wealth relied on the very oppression of enslaved peoples.
