The army's oppressive grip on society extended beyond mere governance; it shaped the very identity of the people. The echoes of past struggles reverberated through the streets, as citizens grappled with a narrative molded by decades of military influence and repression. This was not solely a rejection of individuals in power, but a profound yearning to reclaim agency over a national identity distorted by authoritarianism. As revolutionaries emerged, they sought to dismantle the frameworks that had stifled their voices, envisioning a future free from the burdens of an army that had long served as both protector and oppressor.
