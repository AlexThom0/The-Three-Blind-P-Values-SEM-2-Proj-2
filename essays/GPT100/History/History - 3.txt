This perspective was deeply entrenched in societal norms, reflecting a belief that a woman's primary purpose was her role as a caregiver. Consequently, the pressure to conform was immense, as mothers faced not only health ramifications but also social stigma. The implications of this insistence were substantial, creating a pervasive environment where women felt their worth was intrinsically linked to their ability to breastfeed. This intertwined the, often harsh, realities of medical advice with moral judgments, impacting women's choices and perceptions of motherhood.
