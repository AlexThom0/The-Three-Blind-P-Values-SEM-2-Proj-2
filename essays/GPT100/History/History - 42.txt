By redefining critical thinking in terms of empirical evaluation, institutions could apply Allen’s criteria to various aspects of scholarship and personal conduct. This approach risked reducing complex human experiences to mere data points, undermining the nuance that traditionally informed philosophical and ethical discourse. As such, the implication that all forms of knowledge production adhere strictly to scientific rigor became a contentious point, igniting debates about the integrity and diversity of thought necessary for a truly vibrant academic community.
