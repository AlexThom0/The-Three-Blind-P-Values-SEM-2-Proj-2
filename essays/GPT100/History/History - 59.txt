As these communities adapted to a mobile existence, they created complex social structures and shared practices that emphasized group identity and cooperation. The spirit of the hunt fostered a deep connection to the land, reinforcing the belief that their well-being was intrinsically tied to the migration patterns of the bison. This interdependence cultivated a rich tapestry of oral traditions and stories that celebrated their relationship with nature and the vastness of the plains.
