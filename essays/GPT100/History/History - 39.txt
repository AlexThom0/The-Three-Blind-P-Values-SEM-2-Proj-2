The pursuit of honour through violence also reflects broader societal norms and expectations surrounding masculinity. This dynamic often put pressure on men to engage in aggressive acts, particularly in public displays that reaffirmed their status. As communities developed, these expectations shifted, emphasizing not just personal honour but also collective identity. Hence, violence became a way to assert dominance not just individually, but on behalf of one's class or social group. This transformation underscores how aggression, previously a universal trait among men, began to take on distinct meanings based on social stratification, influencing how masculinity was constructed within different contexts.
