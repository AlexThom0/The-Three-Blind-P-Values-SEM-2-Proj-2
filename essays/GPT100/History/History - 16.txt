Unlike traditional depictions of malevolent dragons, these household spirits often operated within the domestic sphere, influencing the lives of those who invoked them. This distinction highlights a nuanced understanding of witchcraft, wherein practitioners straddled the line between beneficial and harmful magic. The mystique of the 'brides of the dragon' encapsulates this duality, reflecting both fear and reverence for the complex relationships that formed between witches and their otherworldly patrons.
