This shift not only elevated the status of artists but also reflected deeper societal changes in the perception of individual genius. The emergence of the artist as a singular creator paralleled developments in philosophy and humanism, suggesting a growing belief in the power of the individual to shape and influence reality. As artists began to claim their identities and uniqueness, their works transformed into personal statements, imbuing art with emotions and experiences that resonated on a profound level. Thus, the evolution of art became intertwined with the narrative of human aspiration.
