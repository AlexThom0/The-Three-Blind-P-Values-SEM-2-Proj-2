The digital realm has transformed how artworks are perceived, often detaching them from their historical contexts while simultaneously offering new platforms for personal expression. This democratization of art challenges traditional hierarchies, blurring the lines between creator and consumer. As images circulate across social media, they become ephemeral commodities, subject to constant reinterpretation. In this landscape, the viewer's gaze is not merely passive but active, engaging in a dialogue that redefines ownership and authorship, prompting a reconsideration of what it means to view art in a rapidly evolving visual culture.
