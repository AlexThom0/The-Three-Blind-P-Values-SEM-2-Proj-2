Through strategic patronage and the cultivation of an artistic collection, the Attalids were able to craft a narrative that aligned them with esteemed predecessors and divinely sanctioned rulers. This appropriation of art was a powerful mechanism for asserting authority, as it allowed them to visually communicate their lineage and ambitions. Consequently, the art housed within their palaces became a cornerstone of their political identity, reflecting both a personal ambition and a broader cultural legacy.
