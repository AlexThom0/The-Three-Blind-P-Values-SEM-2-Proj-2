While art may enrich individual lives, EAs often prioritize measurable, quantifiable outcomes over emotional or aesthetic contributions. This raises a crucial question about the role of culture and creativity in a society striving for maximized welfare. If we reduce everything to mere metrics of happiness and fulfillment, do we risk overlooking the intrinsic value of human expression? The debate highlights a tension between efficacy and the richness of human experience.
