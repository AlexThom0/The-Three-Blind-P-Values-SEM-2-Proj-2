In this context, nudity transcends mere representation of the body; it evokes a deeper commentary on mortality and the loss of agency. The act of stripping away clothing strips individuals of their identity and power, making their status as defeated or deceased painfully clear. Furthermore, the artistic choices made by Greek creators reflected and reinforced societal values surrounding honor and shame, where the naked body was both a symbol of human fragility and an object of aesthetic contemplation.
