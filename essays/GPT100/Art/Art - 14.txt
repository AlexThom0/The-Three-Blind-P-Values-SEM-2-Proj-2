This phenomenon reflects not only the enduring legacy of the original artist but also the complex interplay between authenticity and reproduction in the art world. Many collectors and institutions grapple with the distinction between an original work and its derivatives, which can sometimes obscure the lines of artistic heritage. As Salvator Mundi's market trajectory demonstrates, the value placed on an artwork often hinges on its perceived authenticity, provenance, and the narratives constructed around it.
