As she grappled with her new limitations, Louise found herself reflecting on how her art had once expressed fluidity and movement. The transition to hyper-realism marked not just a shift in technique but also an emotional pivot as she confronted the reality of her condition. Her earlier brushstrokes that had danced across the canvas now felt like remnants of a past self. This change was not merely aesthetic; it signified a deeper struggle for identity, as the interplay of physical ability and creative expression became increasingly fraught. Each new work served as both a challenge and a catharsis, inviting viewers to witness her evolving narrative.
