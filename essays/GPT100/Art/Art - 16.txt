By orchestrating exhibitions that highlighted the emotive qualities of these artworks, he transformed them into symbols of elite sophistication. His strategies combined a keen awareness of public taste with an ability to position art as both a status symbol and a gateway to modernity. Consequently, the popularity of Monet's work became intertwined with evolving societal values, making art a critical component of social aspiration and identity in an increasingly industrialized world.
