This transition from a personal act of love to a public spectacle illustrates a complex interplay between societal norms and individual agency. While the daughter’s actions initially provoke scandal, they ultimately validate the deeply ingrained cultural expectation for women to nurture. In celebrating this maternal instinct, the narrative simultaneously reinforces the patriarchal structures that require women's labor—be it emotional or physical—to be justified in terms of duty and societal contribution. The very act of sustenance becomes both a private rebellion and a means of public acknowledgment.
