When art is viewed as a collective endeavor rather than a solitary act, the barriers between original and copy begin to blur. We find ourselves grappling with the value of authorship, not just in terms of who created the piece, but also in how artistic intention and collaboration contribute to its significance. The emotional and intellectual responses elicited by artworks can thus be reframed, inviting us to reconsider our perceptions of authenticity and originality.
