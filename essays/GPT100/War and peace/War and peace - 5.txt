However, proponents contended that investigating frameworks for peaceful resolution could illuminate pathways to stability, urging scholars to extend their inquiries beyond historical conflicts. As interdisciplinary approaches emerged, integrating insights from sociology, psychology, and international relations, peace studies gradually gained credibility. This burgeoning field began to reshape not just academic discourse, but also practical diplomacy, emphasizing the importance of dialogue, understanding, and reconciliation in a world still grappling with the legacies of war.
