Chomsky’s influence reached beyond the confines of academia, sparking a blend of fascination and skepticism within military circles regarding the applicability of linguistic concepts to technology. The belief was that if language could be reduced to fundamental structures, then machines could eventually decode human commands with precision. This notion blurred the lines between theoretical linguistics and practical implementation, raising ethical questions about the military's embrace of linguistic research for combat efficiency. By envisioning a system capable of interpreting natural language, Chomsky’s theories inadvertently contributed to a paradigm shift in how language was perceived—not merely as a means of human communication, but as a tool for operational supremacy.
