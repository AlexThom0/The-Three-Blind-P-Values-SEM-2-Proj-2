This brutal practice was not merely an act of retribution; it functioned as a public spectacle meant to instill fear among the populace and deter dissent. The cultural fascination with such displays highlighted a paradox in European norms, where a deep-seated reverence for virtue was often intertwined with graphic depictions of suffering.
