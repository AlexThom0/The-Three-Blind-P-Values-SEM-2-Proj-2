The inability to adapt has led to a pervasive belief that the Constitution is an unassailable fortress, shielding the government from the changes that society continually demands. This paradox raises profound questions about the legitimacy of its authority. With an electorate that evolves and diversifies, the rigidity of a centuries-old document often clashes with contemporary values and realities. As constituents watch their needs go unmet, frustration mounts, revealing a stark disconnection between the ideals enshrined in the text and the lived experiences of the populace. The result is a growing disillusionment that challenges the very foundations of American democracy.
