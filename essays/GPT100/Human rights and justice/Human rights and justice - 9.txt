By framing their interventions as necessary for civilising the natives, colonial authorities sought not only to legitimize their authority but also to reshape social dynamics within these communities. While the colonial administration promoted certain customs as legitimate, it frequently disregarded or altered practices that threatened colonial interests. This manipulation highlights the tension between imposed legal frameworks and indigenous realities, demonstrating how customary law was weaponized to uphold colonial objectives rather than to reflect the true customs of the communities it purported to govern.
