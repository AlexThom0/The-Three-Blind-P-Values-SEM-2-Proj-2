While the Boston Tea Party is romanticized, other protests are often depicted as chaotic and threatening, despite similar motivations rooted in resistance to oppression. This selective storytelling creates a double standard in how movements are legitimized or demonized, influencing everything from media coverage to law enforcement tactics. Such disparities reinforce existing power dynamics and shape societal responses to dissent, transforming the meaning of collective action depending on who is telling the story and how they choose to portray it.
