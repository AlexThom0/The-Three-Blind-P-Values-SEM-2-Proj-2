As the need for resources grew exponentially, Tosquelles and Balvet devised increasingly ingenious methods for sustenance. They forged alliances with local farmers, who shared their stocks for a promise of future protection against the authorities. This collaboration, however, extended beyond mere survival; it fostered a sense of community and purpose among the hospital's residents. The lines between staff and patients blurred as everyone was mobilized in a unified effort. This shared mission not only enhanced resilience but also created a safe haven amid chaos. Such acts of solidarity became essential, reinforcing the moral fabric of their collective struggle.
