These emerging female antislavery societies often aimed to address both the moral imperative to end slavery and the social hierarchies that persistently marginalized Black individuals, even within their ranks. Despite their shared goals, deep-seated prejudices often compromised their unity. White women leaders, while passionate in their advocacy, frequently adhered to societal norms that prioritized their racial identity over a commitment to genuine equality. This created a complex dynamic where solidarity in the fight against slavery coexisted with exclusionary practices that undermined the broader struggle for justice, highlighting the pervasive challenge of reconciling personal beliefs with systemic racism.
