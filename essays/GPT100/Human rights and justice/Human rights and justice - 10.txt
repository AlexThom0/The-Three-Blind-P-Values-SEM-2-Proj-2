The precarious nature of migrant labor in these Gulf states is compounded by the lack of transparency in labor laws and enforcement. Many workers are trapped in exploitative contracts that limit their rights to complain about unsafe conditions. The kafala sponsorship system further exacerbates their vulnerability, tying workers to their employers and thereby stripping them of autonomy. Consequently, when accidents occur, workers often feel compelled to remain silent, fearing reprisals or deportation. This silencing effect not only perpetuates unsafe conditions but also facilitates a culture of impunity for employers, who can prioritize profit over the health and safety of their workforce.
