These findings prompted a shift toward understanding the broader spectrum of sexual violence beyond the prison population. Researchers began exploring the motivations of perpetrators in various contexts, recognizing that many sexual assaults were committed by men who were not driven by unchecked sexual desire or psychological deviancy. Instead, social, cultural, and situational factors were seen as significant contributors. Studies increasingly highlighted the role of entitlement, power dynamics, and socialization in fostering attitudes that justify such violence. This multifaceted approach aimed to capture the complexity of behaviors, moving beyond the simplistic narratives that dominated earlier research.
