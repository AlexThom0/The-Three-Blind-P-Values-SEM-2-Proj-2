Economic hardship, exposure to violence, and systemic inequality often intersect, pushing many toward criminality. This pattern underscores the necessity for a justice system that recognizes the potential for rehabilitation rather than solely focusing on punishment. Engaging with offenders as complex individuals, rather than one-dimensional representations of crime, allows for a deeper understanding of how their life experiences shape their actions.
