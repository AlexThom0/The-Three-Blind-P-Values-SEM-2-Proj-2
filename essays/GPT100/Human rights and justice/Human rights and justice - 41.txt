early 20th century, social attitudes towards substance use began to shift dramatically, influenced by a growing awareness of addiction and public health. Campaigns against alcohol consumption laid the groundwork for more stringent attitudes toward all drugs. This movement not only stigmatized certain substances but also fostered a cultural narrative that positioned drug use as a moral failing rather than a health issue. The legal actions against various substances reflected and reinforced these shifting perceptions, effectively criminalizing behaviors that were previously commonplace. Thus, the transition from acceptance to condemnation created a framework where drug use was increasingly associated with deviance.
