Revolution had shattered the idealism of many artists, including my father. The fervor that once inspired him to pursue his passion for painting turned into a source of shame and regret. Amidst the chaos, creativity was muffled, with the regime labeling artistic expression not as a form of freedom but as a potential threat. It became clear that the vibrant, dynamic identities of artists were under siege, replaced by fear and compliance. This atmosphere shaped not only my father's experience but also our family dynamic, infusing our daily lives with an underlying tension that stifled any spark of rebellion.
