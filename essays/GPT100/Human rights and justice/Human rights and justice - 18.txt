the narrative of disease often intertwines with societal values, reflecting underlying prejudices that infect our views on both health and morality. In this context, the archetype of the ‘dangerous other’ becomes a fundamental aspect of the social response to outbreaks. The very act of naming a disease not only identifies a medical crisis but also reinforces a cultural mythos that marginalizes certain groups. This stigmatization serves to fortify existing social hierarchies, suggesting that those at the periphery of society—be it through class, ethnicity, or lifestyle—are inherently more susceptible to both contagion and moral decay.
