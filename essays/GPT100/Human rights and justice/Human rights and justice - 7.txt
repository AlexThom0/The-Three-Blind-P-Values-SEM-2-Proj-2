The dividing line between lawful consequences and a primal urge for vengeance blurred, prompting individuals to conflate punishment with morality. It became increasingly permissible to treat the accused, regardless of guilt, as pariahs. This reaction reflected a societal hunger for safety at any cost, often overshadowing the complexities of individual circumstances. The narrative surrounding crime devolved into a stark dichotomy, one where empathy for the accused was seen as weakness, leaving little room for rehabilitation or reform. Such a culture fosters fear rather than understanding.
