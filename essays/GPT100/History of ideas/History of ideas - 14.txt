This oversight not only diminishes the richness of philosophical discourse but also reinforces a monolithic worldview that undermines the legitimacy of non-Western philosophies. Such an approach perpetuates a cycle of exclusion, where the contributions of thinkers from marginalized backgrounds are rendered peripheral or dismissed altogether. The challenge lies in actively questioning and reconfiguring the standards of philosophical inquiry, ensuring that a multiplicity of voices contributes to shaping the discipline. Recognizing and integrating these perspectives is essential for a more comprehensive understanding of philosophical debates today.
