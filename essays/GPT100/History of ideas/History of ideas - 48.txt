Newton argued that this initial understanding was tainted over time, obscured by cultural shifts and the rise of polytheism. He posited that as societies evolved, their grasp of the cosmos weakened, leading to corrupted interpretations of divinity that strayed from the original principles. This erosion of pure knowledge, he suggested, nurtured a disconnect between humanity and the divine order, which was once clearly visible through the natural world. Newton's exploration thus serves not merely as a historical account but as a reflection on the need to reclaim the ancient wisdom that aligned religion closely with the natural laws governing the universe.
