This plurality is reflected in the way humanism engaged with classical texts, leading to a renewed interest in themes such as individuality and secularism. The humanists’ approach fostered a dialogue between ancient philosophies and contemporary issues, encouraging thinkers to navigate moral and ethical questions through a lens that retained a distinctly modern sensibility. Thus, humanism served as a bridge, connecting the legacies of antiquity with the evolving intellectual landscapes of the Renaissance, ultimately reshaping European thought in profound ways.
