This linguistic turn transformed philosophical inquiry, shifting focus from abstract metaphysical questions to the concrete analysis of how language shapes our understanding of reality. The implications of this shift were profound, for it not only redefined what constituted a legitimate philosophical problem but also elevated the status of ordinary language as a legitimate subject of study. Philosophers began to scrutinize everyday expressions and propositions, revealing hidden assumptions and ambiguities that often led to confusion. Thus, the evolution of philosophy reflected a burgeoning recognition that clarity in expression was vital for meaningful discourse.
