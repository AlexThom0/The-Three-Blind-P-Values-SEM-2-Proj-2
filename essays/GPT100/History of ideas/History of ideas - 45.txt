This construct of nobility and rightful leadership suggests a yearning for order in a chaotic world. The characters within Tolkien's universe often grapple with existential threats, illustrating how their lineage and virtues bestow legitimacy upon them, reinforcing the reader's desire for coherence and morality in the face of turmoil. While the battles may be epic, the underlying contests revolve around the principles of right governance and the burden of leadership. In contrast to contemporary narratives that revel in destruction, Tolkien’s work posits a vision of restoration and hope, underpinning the moral fabric that binds his characters and their quests.
