This inquiry not only highlighted the disconnection between workers and the products of their efforts but also invited a reflection on the broader implications of such alienation within capitalist contexts. By framing labor as a source of alienation, the 'humanist Marx' illuminated the emotional and psychological toll that economic systems impose on individuals. This perspective transcended traditional economic discourse, providing a rich philosophical lens through which to evaluate the socio-economic landscape, ultimately advocating for a more humane and integrated understanding of human existence within capitalist societies.
