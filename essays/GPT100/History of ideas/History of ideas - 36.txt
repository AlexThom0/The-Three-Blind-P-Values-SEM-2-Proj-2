This perspective not only devalues rich philosophical traditions but also reinforces harmful stereotypes that perpetuate colonial attitudes. By framing non-European thought within narrow confines, Kant overlooks the complexities of cultural contexts and their varying forms of knowledge. Establishing hierarchies of intellect serves not just as a critique of these civilizations, but as a justification for imperial dominion, illustrating the intertwined nature of philosophy, power, and colonialism in shaping Western narratives.
