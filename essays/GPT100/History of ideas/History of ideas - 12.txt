Zhuangzi's perspective encourages a detachment from the emotional turmoil surrounding death, advocating instead for an acceptance of life's impermanence. This philosophy challenges the conventional approach to mourning, suggesting that familial grief may hinder rather than help the dying individual's journey. In Zhuangist thought, the natural flow of life and death should not be obstructed by societal expectations or emotional outbursts. Instead, the emphasis lies on fostering a tranquil environment that honors the individual's passage and embraces the transformative nature of the experience. Such an understanding reshapes the grief narrative from one of sorrow to one that celebrates continuity beyond mortal existence.
