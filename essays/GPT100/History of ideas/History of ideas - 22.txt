This reductionist viewpoint not only dehumanizes people but also suggests a moral vacuum where empathy and ethical consideration are sacrificed on the altar of efficiency. Such an environment fosters a culture where relationships are transactional and devoid of genuine connection, leading to a climate of fear and alienation. It is this pervasive scrutiny that perpetuates totalitarianism and reinforces a collective amnesia regarding the significance of the individual in moral discourse, a theme central to both Barthes and Adorno in their critique of modernity.
