A philosopher’s reliance on intuition invites scrutiny, as these seem subjective and potentially biased by personal experience. Hence, the tension arises: while intuitions may resonate on an individual level, their validity in philosophical discourse depends on broader acceptance and reasoned justification. This interplay between intuition and argumentation is crucial, as it not only shapes the formulation of premises but also determines the overall strength of the conclusions drawn within the philosophical landscape.
