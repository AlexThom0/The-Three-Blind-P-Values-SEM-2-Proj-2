Aristotle understood that the process of grasping complex ideas could lead to intellectual growth, much like mastering a craft. This belief led him to emphasize the importance of critical discourse, asserting that genuine understanding emerges not from passive consumption of ideas but from active participation in their exploration. In this sense, dense or challenging texts can stimulate the mind and encourage readers to move beyond superficial interpretations, facilitating a richer dialogue with the material. Thus, the difficulty of a text can ultimately enhance its value.
