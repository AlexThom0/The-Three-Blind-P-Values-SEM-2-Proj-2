Such critics often isolate utterances regarding divine existence or spiritual experiences from the rich tapestry of human encounters that infuse them with significance. In doing so, they neglect the communal practices, moral frameworks, and existential questions that underlie these beliefs. This neglect can lead to a misinterpretation of faith not as a static assertion, but as a dynamic, socially embedded phenomenon that shapes and is shaped by the cultural milieu. To fully comprehend religious conviction, one must appreciate the intricate interplay between language, action, and communal life.
