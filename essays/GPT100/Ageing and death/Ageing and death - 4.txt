In veterinary practice, the emotional landscape is different; the burden of choice weighs heavily on the veterinarians who stand at the crossroads of life and death. Each decision to euthanize is made not only in consideration of the animal’s suffering but also with an acute awareness of the impact on the owners. It is a profound ethical dilemma, where compassion intersects with professional duty, and each instance leaves a lasting imprint. This complexity shapes the identity of veterinarians and influences their emotional resilience in face of such weighty decisions.
