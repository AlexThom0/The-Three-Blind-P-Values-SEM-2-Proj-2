Water can not only be converted into hydrogen and oxygen for rocket fuel, reducing the need to transport these resources from Earth, but it can also support long-term human habitation in space. Establishing a sustainable in-situ resource utilization process for water would mitigate the logistical challenges of resupply missions from Earth and could make space exploration more economically viable. Consequently, investing in technologies that can autonomously harvest and process water from asteroids could yield rewards that far exceed current expectations.
