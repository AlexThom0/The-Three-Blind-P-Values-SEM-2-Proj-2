The evolution of mathematics seems intrinsically tied to our experiences and the language we employ to express them. This interrelationship implies that while mathematical truths may appear eternal, they are simultaneously shaped by human cognition and culture. Moreover, the historical context in which these concepts arise suggests a more dynamic interplay between abstract ideas and the physical world. Thus, any robust understanding of mathematics must involve not only these pure forms but also the active minds that engage with them.
