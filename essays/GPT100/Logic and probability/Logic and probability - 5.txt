Scholars began to explore not just the formal structure of arguments but also the persuasive techniques that made debates compelling. This shift recognized that logic is not merely about correct reasoning but also involves the art of persuasion, bringing Aristotle's insights into sharper focus. As thinkers like Peter Ramus challenged the established norms, the interplay between logic and rhetoric became a fertile ground for new methodologies. This evolution was pivotal, influencing how subsequent generations approached both teaching and practicing argumentation.
