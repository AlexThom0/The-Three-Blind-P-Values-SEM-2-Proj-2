Rorty’s decision to adopt a persona allowed her to engage with the philosophical discourse from a unique standpoint, enriching her exploration of Western thought. By stepping into these alternate identities, she transcended the limitations of her own background, allowing for a broader dialogue around philosophical themes. This act of dual authorship not only enabled her to present ideas in a culturally resonant manner but also highlighted the performative aspect of writing itself. Each persona she adopted provided a fresh lens through which to dissect complex concepts, framing her arguments with a diversity that is often absent in singularly defined narratives.
