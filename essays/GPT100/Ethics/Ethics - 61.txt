Isolation from societal norms transforms their realities, forcing them to adopt survival instincts that prioritize immediate needs over long-term aspirations. Many children develop coping mechanisms that shield them from psychological trauma but simultaneously hinder their ability to reintegrate into a nurturing environment. The bonds formed with peers on the streets can become both a refuge and a risk, as these relationships are often founded on mutual reliance in the harshest of circumstances. This duality complicates their existence, pushing them further into a cycle of despair that feels inescapable, where hope seems like a distant memory rather than a tangible goal.
