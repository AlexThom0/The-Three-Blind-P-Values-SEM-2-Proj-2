If we take the metaphor of the soul further, it compels us to examine how technology and artificial intelligences might evoke similar questions about identity and ethical responsibility. Just as we ascribe moral qualities to human souls, the line blurs when contemplating machines endowed with decision-making capabilities. Are we prepared to recognize anything resembling a soul in these constructs, and if so, what implications does that carry for accountability? Technology thus transforms the conversation around morality as it intersects with the concept of the soul, broadening the scope of our ethical frameworks.
