Morgen’s return marked a pivotal shift, as he now had a more direct mandate from Himmler, suggesting that the SS leadership was increasingly aware of the potential for self-destruction within its ranks. This duality of his role—as investigator and enforcer—heightened the stakes not only for Morgen but also for those entangled in the system of corruption. The concentration camps, rife with inefficiency and graft, demanded scrutiny, and Himmler's fear of dissent created a volatile environment. This was no longer just about loyalty to the Reich; it was about survival among the predators who thrived on the very chaos they were meant to control.
