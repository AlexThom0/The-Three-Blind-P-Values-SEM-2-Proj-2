This leads to a paradox where consumers, while expressing concern for humane treatment, inadvertently support practices that perpetuate suffering. The illusion of choice offered through labels like "free-range" or "humanely raised" often serves to placate ethical concerns without addressing the fundamental issue of animals as property. Furthermore, the marketing of these products exploits consumer guilt, allowing the industry to capitalize on a conflicted demand that ultimately maintains the status quo in animal exploitation. Consequently, true reform remains elusive.
