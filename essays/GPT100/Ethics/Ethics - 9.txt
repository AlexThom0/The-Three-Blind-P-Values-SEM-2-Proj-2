This dynamic reflects a broader trend throughout history where rulers exploit the very institutions meant to protect their citizens. In the case of Rome, as MacMullen suggests, the neglect of military preparedness underscored a troubling reality: wealth and power can blind leadership to the vulnerabilities of their realm. Similarly, the swift collapse in Afghanistan revealed how deeply entrenched corruption can erode even the most formidable forces, leaving nations open to unforeseen grabs for power by those willing to exploit chaos.
