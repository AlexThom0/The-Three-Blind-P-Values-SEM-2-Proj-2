Advocates argue that without common values, society risks fragmentation, leading to a chaotic pluralism where ethical standards become fully subjective. This perspective champions a form of universalism aimed at establishing foundational principles that transcend cultural differences. In contrast, those favoring moral relativism assert that diverse ethical systems reflect the richness of human experience and the contexts from which they arise. This clash underscores a fundamental tension: the struggle between the desire for unified moral guidance and the acknowledgment of individual and communal variations in ethical reasoning.
