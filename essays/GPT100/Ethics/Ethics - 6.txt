It challenges practitioners to confront not only the physical act but also the mental states that precede it. By advocating for mindfulness and the understanding of interdependence, the tradition encourages a reevaluation of our impulses and the societal structures that reinforce them. This approach shifts the focus from merely condemning violence to fostering a profound ethical reflection that can prevent it.
