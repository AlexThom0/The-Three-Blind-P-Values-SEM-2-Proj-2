This perception limits ethical discourse surrounding animal rights, reducing it to issues of treatment rather than questioning the validity of the use itself. The prevailing view reinforces a legal framework that privileges property rights over intrinsic moral considerations. Consequently, this framework leads to a blind acceptance of practices that prioritize economic gain, neglecting the acute suffering involved. For instance, breeding practices are designed to maximize productivity, often resulting in severe welfare compromises. Thus, while the argument for humane treatment may resonate emotionally, it ultimately sidesteps deeper moral implications related to the very nature of our relationship with animals as beings deserving consideration beyond mere property.
