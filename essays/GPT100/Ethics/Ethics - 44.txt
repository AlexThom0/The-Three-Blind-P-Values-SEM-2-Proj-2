This evolution in zombie portrayals highlights a broader cultural commentary reflecting society's internal fears and chaos. The transformation of these monsters parallels our psychological responses to trauma, driving home the unsettling notion that the biggest threats often stem from within. These narratives allow us to confront our anxieties—not merely as passive observers but as participants in a shared experience of survival. The undead become avatars of our darker impulses, enabling us to explore complex social issues like contagion, isolation, and identity. As our fears materialize on screen, they urge us to grapple with the specters of our reality in a visceral and often humorous way.
