While Asch focused on conformity, Milgram pushed the boundaries further, probing the extreme lengths individuals would go to in obeying authority figures, even when it meant causing harm to others. His experiments not only mirrored Asch’s foundational findings but also extended them into the morally ambiguous realm of obedience under duress, challenging our understanding of personal responsibility and moral agency. This shift from mere conformity to active complicity marked a pivotal turn in psychological research, underlining the ethical dilemmas that accompany such profound inquiries into human behavior.
