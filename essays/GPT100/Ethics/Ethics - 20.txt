The concept of honour was thus a balancing act; it dictated that the most minor transgressions could lead to significant repercussions. This tension created a culture where maintaining one’s honour often overshadowed reason, pushing men to defend their reputation through displays of courage and, at times, violence. Such practices were not merely personal but also reflected broader societal expectations, intertwining individual identities with collective notions of honour. The stakes were high, for to lose face could mean the tarnishing of one's name and legacy, ensuring that the pursuit of honour remained a critical facet of elite masculinity.
