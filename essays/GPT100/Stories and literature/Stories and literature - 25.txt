By elevating these medieval texts, Oxford encouraged a certain romanticization of the past, aligning literary study with nationalistic and imperial ideologies. This selective focus on chivalry and heroism fostered an understanding of Englishness that neglected the complexities of historical realities, thus allowing a simplified, often glorified image of the nation to permeate educational discourse. Such an approach positioned literature as a tool for both cultural pride and political justification in a broader imperial context.
