This representation not only reflects the geographic understanding of that time but also embodies the mythological essence of Ocean and Tethys. They were not merely contemplative figures but essential elements of the cosmos, shaping perceptions of nature. The fluidity of Ocean’s borders blurred the lines between reality and mythology, prompting ancient scholars to contend with both the allure of divine imagination and the necessity of empirical observation in map-making. Thus, the aesthetic choices in early cartography reveal deeper cultural narratives.
