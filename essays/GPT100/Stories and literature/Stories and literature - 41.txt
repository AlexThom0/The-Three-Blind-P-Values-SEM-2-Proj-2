He argues that a God capable of regret or retribution diminishes the notion of divine perfection. Instead of depicting God as a vengeful being reacting to human failings, Celsus elevates the concept of a rational and constant deity, who embodies ultimate goodness and harmony. This understanding allows for a world that experiences challenges without attributing them to God's caprice, thus framing human existence within a broader context that transcends arbitrary divine intervention. Hence, Celsus challenges believers to reconsider the very nature of divinity.
