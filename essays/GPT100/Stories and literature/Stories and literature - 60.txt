De Bury argued that the possession of books allowed individuals not only to amass knowledge but also to cultivate virtue and wisdom. This perspective reflects a broader cultural shift of the time, where the ownership of texts transitioned from a mere status symbol to a means of personal and intellectual growth. Such an evolution in the perception of books blurred the lines between materialistic obsession and genuine literary engagement, ultimately influencing the dynamics of authorship and readership in subsequent generations.
