The Mamluk deck's simplicity stood in stark contrast to the elaborate imagery favored by European nobles, who sought to elevate the game beyond mere pastime into a reflection of their societal values. This transformation also paralleled the rise of humanism, where individual virtues and allegorical figures began to take precedence, allowing card-makers to introduce complex narratives and philosophical themes that resonated with an aristocratic audience.
