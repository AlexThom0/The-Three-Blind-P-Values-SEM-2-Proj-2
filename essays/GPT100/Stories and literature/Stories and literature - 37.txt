While other poets reveled in the formalities of pastoral conventions and the aesthetics of beauty, Shakespeare seems intent on dismantling such facades. The sudden emergence of a character like the Dark Lady disrupts the harmony created by previous sonnets. It complicates the emotional landscape and plays with expectations, illustrating that the poet was not merely following established norms but was instead engaging with contemporary issues surrounding race, beauty, and the nature of love itself.
