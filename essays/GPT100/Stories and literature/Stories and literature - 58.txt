The challenge intensifies when considering the nonlinear presentation of time in complex narratives. Events might be interwoven with layers of flashbacks or foreshadowing, which require a nuanced understanding that algorithmic processes currently lack. Furthermore, the presence of unreliable narrators adds another layer of difficulty, as readers must decipher truth from manipulation. These intricacies often lead to misinterpretations by AI, which relies on surface-level cues rather than the deep contextual knowledge that human readers draw upon. The success of literary analysis, therefore, hinges not only on syntactic structures but also on the subtleties of semantic meaning embedded within the text.
