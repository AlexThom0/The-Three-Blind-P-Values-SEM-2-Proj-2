Invertebrates can mistake microplastics for food, leading to harmful ingestion that affects their growth and reproductive success. As these small organisms face increasing pressure, the repercussions ripple up the food chain, threatening larger species that rely on them as a primary food source. The once-vibrant marine ecosystems are now grappling with an alarming reality shaped by human consumption and waste.
