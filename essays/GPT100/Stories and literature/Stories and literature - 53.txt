McAdoo's inquiries revealed a rich tapestry of oral histories, underscoring the intricate relationship between community narratives and disaster preparedness. These tales often contained vital information about the landscape, warning signs, and traditional responses to emerging threats. As he engaged with the elders, it became clear that these stories were not merely folk tales but rather repositories of collective wisdom, refined over generations. The strength of these narratives lay in their ability to resonate emotionally, fostering a deep-rooted understanding among community members about their surroundings. This cultural context enriched McAdoo's scientific perspective, highlighting the importance of integrating indigenous knowledge into modern disaster risk management strategies.
