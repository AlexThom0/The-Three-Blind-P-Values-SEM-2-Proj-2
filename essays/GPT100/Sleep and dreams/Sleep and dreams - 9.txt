The sanctity of nighttime rest has been undermined, as its restorative qualities are often overlooked or dismissed. Instead of embracing the natural rhythms of sleep, we frequently resort to interventions that promise efficiency, thereby further alienating ourselves from the essential act of resting. As our connection to sleep weakens, so too does our understanding of its profound impact on mental and physical health. This estrangement raises critical questions about the cost of our relentless pursuit of efficiency.
