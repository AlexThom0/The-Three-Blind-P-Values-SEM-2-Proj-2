The interplay between sleeplessness and my aspirations has often left me in limbo, torn between the desire for connection and the instinct to retreat. This internal conflict feeds into a cycle where loneliness breeds restlessness, amplifying the effects of sleep deprivation. During these moments of self-reflection, I confront the underlying fears of intimacy and the vulnerability that companionship demands. The dichotomy of seeking closeness while feeling shackled by the consequences of my disorder lays bare a profound irony—longing for partnership while being ensnared by the very traits that threaten it. Such complexities have shaped my understanding of both friendship and solitude.
