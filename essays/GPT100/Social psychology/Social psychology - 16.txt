If we differentiate between harmful stereotypes that oversimplify and distort group characteristics and functional stereotypes that can aid in quick decision-making, we begin to navigate a more complex terrain. This bifurcation acknowledges that while certain stereotypes may perpetuate bias, others can stem from observable truths, albeit generalized. Thus, the conversation shifts from an all-or-nothing perspective to one that considers context, allowing for a more sophisticated dialogue on accuracy and representation within social frameworks.
