Such adherence fosters a dangerous conformity, where the depth of individual conscience is sacrificed for the perceived strength of belonging. This shift towards an absolutist mindset strips not only the individual of their agency but also distorts communal values. In this environment, empathy becomes anathema, a liability in a world defined by rigid ideological boundaries. Joiners, therefore, become catalysts for the chilling disinterest in the consequences of their actions, further entrenching the moral vacuity that Arendt so passionately critiqued.
