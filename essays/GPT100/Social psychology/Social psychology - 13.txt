The evolution of this concept has prompted various fields to seek deeper understanding and address microaggressions as both a psychological and sociocultural phenomenon. Researchers are now examining specific contexts in which these microaggressions occur, such as workplaces, educational institutions, and social interactions. By considering the cumulative effects these incidents can have, we begin to establish the broader implications on mental health and community well-being. This comprehensive approach enables a more nuanced assessment of microaggressions and their pervasive impact on marginalized populations, which is critical for formulating effective interventions.
