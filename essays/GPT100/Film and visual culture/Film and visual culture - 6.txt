This new dynamic meant that artists and entertainers were no longer just creators; they were also entrepreneurs, managing their own narratives to captivate audiences. The rise of the press and later, radio and television, amplified this phenomenon, providing platforms that transformed individual achievements into shared cultural experiences. Thus, creativity and public relations became intertwined, leading to a marketplace of personas that challenged traditional notions of artistry. Through careful curation of their identities, these figures not only shaped their careers but also influenced societal values surrounding celebrity.
