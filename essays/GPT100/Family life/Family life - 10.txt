In human evolution, the introduction of the childhood stage presents a critical shift, where the extended duration of nurturing allows for greater cognitive development. This extended childhood not only equips children with the necessary skills to thrive in complex social environments but also fosters stronger emotional bonds within familial structures. Consequently, this nurturing period paved the way for enhanced cooperation and social learning that are hallmarks of human societies, setting us apart from our closest relatives.
