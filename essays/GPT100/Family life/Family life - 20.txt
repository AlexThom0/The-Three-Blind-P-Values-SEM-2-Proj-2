I found myself grappling not just with the film, but with the stark contrast between my former freedom and my present anxiety. The carefree enjoyment of art and spontaneity now felt overshadowed by parental vigilance. What once was an escape turned into a reminder of the very fears I despised. How could I reconcile these conflicting identities? The child in me, unchained and adventurous, clashed with the adult whose every thought revolved around protection. I gripped my daughter tightly, as if that could shield her from every threat I'd conjured in my mind. Yet there I was, drawn into a story about young lives lost to reckless choices.
