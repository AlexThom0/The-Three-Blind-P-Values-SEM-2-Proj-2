The delicate balance of this ecosystem, rich with endemic species, hangs in the balance. In their struggle for survival and recognition, the Mayagna are faced with the dual challenge of preserving their ancestral lands while navigating the pressures exerted by external enterprises. Their cultural identity is inexorably linked to the health of the forest, meaning that every tree cut and every species displaced represents a loss far beyond the tangible.
