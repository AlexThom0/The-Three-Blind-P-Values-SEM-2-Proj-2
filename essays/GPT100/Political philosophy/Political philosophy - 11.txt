Gustav von Schmoller, who critiqued Schmitt's theories, emphasized that the invocation of emergency powers undermined the very fabric of constitutional democracy. He argued that this legal maneuvering not only stripped the Land of its autonomy but also emboldened executive overreach, paving the way for totalitarian regimes. The ramifications of this case serve as a cautionary tale about the fragility of rights and governance when legal instruments are wielded tactically to justify authoritarianism, a concern that remains pertinent in contemporary debates on human rights and constitutionalism.
