This shift in perspective was detrimental, as it fostered a sense of alienation among colonists who perceived their economic freedoms as increasingly constrained. Burke's advocacy for a balanced approach to colonial governance reflected his recognition that sustainable empire-building required a nuanced understanding of local demands and aspirations. By promoting trade without excessive taxation, he aimed to cultivate loyalty rather than resentment. This delicate equilibrium would ultimately be essential for maintaining the integrity of British interests across the Atlantic.
