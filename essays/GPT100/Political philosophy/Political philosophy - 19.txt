Even in societies that champion individual freedoms, the legal mechanisms that permit child marriages reveal deep-seated cultural biases that prioritize tradition over the autonomy and rights of young girls. These issues are compounded by socio-economic factors, where poverty and lack of education push families to see marriage as a financial transaction rather than a partnership built on mutual respect. Addressing these cultural norms is essential for dismantling the institutional frameworks that enable child marriage to persist.
