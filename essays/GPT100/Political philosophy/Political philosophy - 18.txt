Despite the superficial cohabitation that characterized these mixed societies, underlying tensions persisted, often flaring up as conflicts over trade, resources, and social status. This dynamic illustrates how structural arrangements alone could not eradicate prejudice or foster true understanding among the groups. Each community continued to cling to its identity, bolstered by centuries of distinct cultural and religious practices, which, instead of merging, further solidified their boundaries and beliefs. Consequently, the prospect of genuine coexistence remained elusive, ultimately leading to the tumultuous events of the Reformation.
