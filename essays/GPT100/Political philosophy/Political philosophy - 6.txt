Each party’s understanding of evidentiary standards and reasoning frameworks shapes their interpretation of events and ideas. Without a common ground, discussions can easily devolve into misunderstandings or even hostilities, overshadowing potential alliances. This disconnection creates barriers not only to agreement but also to empathy and collaboration. Engaging with those who hold conflicting viewpoints demands more than mere argumentation; it requires an exploration of the underlying values and beliefs that inform different justifications, thereby opening pathways to transformative discussions that transcend mere rhetoric.
