This manipulation of economic theory allowed them to craft policies that not only protected their profits but also solidified their influence over governmental decisions. By focusing on the accumulation of wealth in the form of precious metals, they obscured the broader implications of commercial activity, such as innovation, labor conditions, and market dynamics. The focus on metal wealth distorted economic priorities, diverting attention from more equitable forms of economic growth.
