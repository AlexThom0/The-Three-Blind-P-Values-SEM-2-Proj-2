Debs argued that the fight against judicial tyranny must extend to combating economic exploitation, as both forms of oppression restricted individual freedoms. The reality of being forced into subservience to corporate interests was as egregious as being jailed without trial. Workers began to unite, recognizing that true liberation required not just legal reforms but also a reimagining of the economic structures that dictated their lives and limited their autonomy.
