Evolution favors those pathogens that can strike a balance between virulence and transmissibility. While a highly aggressive virus may incapacitate its host quickly, a more moderate strain allows for increased social interactions, thereby enhancing the likelihood of spreading the infection. This delicate interplay highlights the importance of understanding the evolutionary strategies that pathogens employ. It reveals how the adaptative traits of different viruses can influence their ability to thrive in human populations, ensuring their presence in our ecosystem.
