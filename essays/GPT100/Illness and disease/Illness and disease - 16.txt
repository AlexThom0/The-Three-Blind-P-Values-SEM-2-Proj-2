By fostering biodiversity loss and increasing livestock density, these methods create ideal conditions for viruses to jump from animals to humans. Critics contend that such practices illustrate a deeper misunderstanding of ecology, where economic growth takes precedence over health and sustainability. This disjunction not only amplifies the risks associated with zoonoses but also complicates the naming conventions proposed by the WHO. As diseases are viewed through a political and economic lens, the challenge of accurately representing their origins and implications becomes ever more pressing.
