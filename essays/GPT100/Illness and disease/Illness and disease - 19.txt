Illness invites reflection on the self's limitations, fostering empathy not only for oneself but also for others experiencing similar trials. In this way, suffering acts as a catalyst for cultivating compassion and understanding. Individuals begin to perceive the biological body and the lived body as intertwined rather than opposed. Such insights can shift perspectives, allowing one to appreciate the inherent worth in every small action, igniting a desire to act with kindness. Through this lens, the journey through illness becomes a unique pathway to deeper moral engagement.
