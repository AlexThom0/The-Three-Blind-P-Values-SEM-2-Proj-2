This insistence on an internalized conflict suggests that the responsibility for healing lies solely within the patient’s psyche. However, such a perspective neglects the crucial external factors, including societal norms and familial relationships, that shape a person's reality. By focusing predominantly on the internal, Freud’s analysis risks downplaying how external traumas and systemic failures contribute to psychological distress. Instead of purely uncovering repressed memories, a holistic approach must incorporate an understanding of the relational context that influences an individual's emotional landscape.
