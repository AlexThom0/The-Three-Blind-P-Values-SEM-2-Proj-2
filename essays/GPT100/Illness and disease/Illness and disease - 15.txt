Climate change has allowed tick populations to expand, flourishing in environments that once remained inhospitable. The burgeoning warmth accelerates the ticks' life cycles, enabling rapid reproduction and increased infestations during crucial seasons for moose. As they grapple with this relentless parasite, moose must also contend with dwindling food sources and habitat fragmentation, intensifying their vulnerability and raising urgent questions about their survival in the face of a rapidly changing landscape.
