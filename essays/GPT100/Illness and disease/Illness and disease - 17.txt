This discovery opened a new frontier in understanding neurodegenerative diseases, highlighting the complex interactions between the immune response and microbial presence. Researchers began to explore how the pathways of infection and inflammatory response could inadvertently contribute to neural impairment. In particular, the presence of certain neurotrophic factors produced by these microbes suggested a possible mechanism linking infection to cognitive decline. This burgeoning field of study emphasizes the need for a comprehensive approach to Alzheimer’s, recognizing that the brain’s environment is influenced by a myriad of factors beyond mere genetic predisposition.
