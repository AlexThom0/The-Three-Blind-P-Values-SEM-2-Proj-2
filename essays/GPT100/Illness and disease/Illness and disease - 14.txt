As companies aggressively market lifestyle drugs toward conditions that are less life-threatening, the focus drifts further from chronic diseases needing urgent attention. This misalignment not only affects the availability of innovative treatments but also skews the perception of what constitutes a public health priority. The emphasis on profit margins leads to a disproportionate allocation of resources, where conditions like obesity receive attention while fundamental diseases languish. In this environment, tackling the comprehensive health needs of the population becomes increasingly complicated.
