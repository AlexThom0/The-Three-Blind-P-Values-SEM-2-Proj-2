Further complicating this dynamic is the diverse public opinion surrounding the purpose of national parks. While some promote the notion of serene, untouched landscapes, others advocate for increased infrastructure to cater to a growing number of visitors. This dichotomy can often lead to policy paralysis, as stakeholders vie for differing visions of what a national park should represent, thereby straining the National Park Service's ability to uphold its stewardship goals while simultaneously catering to economic pressures and public demand.
