Communities adjacent to protected areas frequently depend on the land for their livelihoods, facing restrictions that can threaten their basic needs. The imposition of conservation measures often results in a disconnect between local populations and their ancestral practices, leading to tensions and resistance. This situation is exacerbated when economic rewards of conservation, such as eco-tourism, do not filter down to these communities. As a result, a narrow focus on biodiversity protection can engender conflict and hinder the sustainability of both human and ecological systems.
