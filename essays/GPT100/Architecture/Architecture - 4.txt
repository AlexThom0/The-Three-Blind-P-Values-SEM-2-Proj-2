This drove architects to focus on optimal acoustics, blending artistry with engineering to enhance auditory experiences. Innovations included the use of curved surfaces and strategically placed balconies to foster clearer sound transmission. Spectators began to expect not just a visual feast, but an auditory one as well, compelling playwrights and composers to consider how their work would resonate in these grand spaces. The evolving performance environment thus became as crucial to the theatrical experience as the narratives themselves.
