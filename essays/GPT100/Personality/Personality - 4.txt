Freud's analysis unveils the psychological underpinnings of mass belief, which can be exploited by charismatic leaders. The phenomena of collective psychology illustrate how individuals, yearning for meaning, can surrender their rational faculties to a leader whom they perceive as both a savior and an embodiment of their national identity. This dynamic is not only pivotal to understanding the fervent devotion towards figures like Hitler but also reveals how easily a society can shift from rational discourse to fanaticism. In this context, the interplay of fear, desire, and group identity becomes crucial in grasping the allure and dangers of political messianism.
