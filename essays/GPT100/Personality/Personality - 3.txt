When individuals are unable to engage emotionally, as seen with psychopaths, their grasp of morality appears compromised. This disconnect demonstrates that ethical judgments may require more than logical reasoning; they necessitate a deep-seated emotional resonance with the experiences of others. This interplay between emotion and morality accentuates the complexity of human ethical behavior and underscores the limitations of purely rational approaches to understanding moral failures.
