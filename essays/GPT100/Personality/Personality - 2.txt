The implications of Kagan's findings reach beyond the laboratory, suggesting that early identification of reactivity can provide valuable insights for parents and educators. By understanding the inherent differences in temperamental responses, supportive strategies can be developed to nurture children’s growth, addressing their unique needs while fostering resilience. This nuanced perspective on temperament challenges traditional notions of a uniform developmental trajectory, emphasizing that variability in emotional expression is not merely a phase but a core aspect of individual identity.
