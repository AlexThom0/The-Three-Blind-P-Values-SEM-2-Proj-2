Tito's strategy aimed to balance the interests of various groups, but it ultimately sowed the seeds for future discord. As the federation struggled under the weight of central control, regional identities intensified, leading to a growing sense of nationalism among the republics. By the late 1980s, rising ethnic tensions threatened to unravel the fragile unity Tito had enforced. The death of the leader in 1980 marked a turning point; his absence caused a power vacuum that rival factions sought to fill, igniting long-suppressed grievances. The ideological divisions that had lain dormant began to resurface, setting the stage for the violent conflicts of the 1990s.
