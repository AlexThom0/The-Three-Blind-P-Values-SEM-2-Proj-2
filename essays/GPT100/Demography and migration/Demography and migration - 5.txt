This mismatch between genetics and environment can lead to increased vulnerability to various health issues, particularly when individuals settle in areas with different ecological pressures. For instance, individuals who are genetically predisposed to thrive in temperate climates may struggle in tropical regions where diseases thrive, reducing their overall health and adaptive success. Furthermore, the introduction of new pathogens can expose populations to diseases against which they have little or no immunity. As such, the blending of genetic traits from diverse populations may yield unexpected consequences, hindering our evolutionary resilience in the face of rapidly changing environments.
