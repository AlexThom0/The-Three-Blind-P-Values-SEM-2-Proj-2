While Mannheim acknowledged the importance of social contexts, he also warned against oversimplification. Today's usage of generational labels often lacks this nuanced understanding, leading to misconceptions that obscure the complex realities faced by various groups. Instead of viewing generations as monolithic entities, it is crucial to recognize the diverse experiences and challenges that shape each cohort’s identity and attitudes, particularly in a society marked by inequalities and varying access to resources.
