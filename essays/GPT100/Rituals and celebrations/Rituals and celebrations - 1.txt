Practices such as jumping the broom emerged as acts of defiance and resilience, allowing enslaved individuals to assert their identity amidst oppression. These ceremonies transformed mundane acts into powerful symbols of resistance and community, fostering bonds that transcended the harsh realities of enslavement. As these practices adapted over time, they became integral to an evolving cultural narrative, illustrating how marginalized groups reclaimed their agency. In this light, the act of jumping the broom signifies not only the union of two individuals but also the survival and enduring spirit of a culture under duress.
