Their research indicates that factors such as family dynamics, socioeconomic status, and community support systems contribute to the manifestation of antisocial behaviors. Notably, when they analyzed instances of juvenile delinquency, they observed that contextual elements often predicted outcomes even more accurately than genetic markers did. This nuanced understanding underscores the complexity of the nature-nurture debate, suggesting that while genetic propensities exist, they are inevitably shaped by the surrounding environment. This kind of comprehensive analysis is vital for developing targeted intervention strategies.
