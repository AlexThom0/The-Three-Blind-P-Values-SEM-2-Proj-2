This lack of comprehensive data complicates our understanding of the global autism landscape, making it challenging to address the needs of individuals across different regions and socio-economic contexts. Furthermore, cultural perceptions of autism can influence reporting and diagnosis, leading to significant disparities in recognized cases. In some communities, autism may be stigmatized or simply misunderstood, resulting in many children remaining undiagnosed. Bridging this knowledge gap is crucial for developing effective interventions and support systems tailored to diverse populations worldwide. Effective diagnosis and subsequent support strategies can vary considerably based on these factors.
