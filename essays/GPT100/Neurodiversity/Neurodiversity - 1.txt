The widening net of diagnoses may serve to increase pharmaceutical profits rather than enhance patient care. Consequently, many individuals find themselves labeled with conditions that may not fully capture their experiences, while effective treatments remain elusive. This distortion underscores the importance of not only examining the biological underpinnings of mental health but also questioning the sociopolitical frameworks that shape our understanding of these conditions. Thus, a critical reassessment of how we define and approach mental disorders is essential.
