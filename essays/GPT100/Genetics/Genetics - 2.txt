Each gene, in striving for replication, influences the evolutionary trajectory dictated by natural selection. This leads to the fascinating interplay between cooperation and competition among genes, challenging traditional notions of altruism and mutualism in evolutionary theory. The implications stretch beyond genetics, as they prompt questions about the nature of fitness and the roles of individual organisms within the larger ecological and evolutionary systems. As such, understanding these nuances is vital for grasping the essence of evolutionary processes.
