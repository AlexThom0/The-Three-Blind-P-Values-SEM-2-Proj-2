This breakthrough raised profound ethical questions about individuality and autonomy, particularly in the context of selecting traits. As cloning technology progressed, the implications of genetic manipulation became increasingly complex. Questions emerged about the nature of consent and the potential for social stratification based on genetic desirability. The allure of creating tailored human experiences clashed with fears of a rigidly engineered society, where differences were not only controlled but commodified. This duality encapsulated the tension at the heart of 20th-century biotechnological advancements.
