Gene editing brings the possibility of creating better models by enabling the modification of other organisms, such as pigs or non-human primates, which could offer closer physiological parallels to human biology. This not only diversifies the pool of research subjects but also enhances our understanding of disease mechanisms and potential treatments. The ability to tailor these models to reflect specific genetic traits or conditions offers unprecedented insights into therapeutic interventions and disease progression.
