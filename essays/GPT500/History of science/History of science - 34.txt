The ancient monuments serve not just as relics of a bygone era but also as an embodiment of collective memory. Experts warn that dismissing historical patterns in favor of recent data can lead to a false sense of security. The tragedy of the 2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami further illustrated that even modest adjustments, made in seemingly rational manners, can result in catastrophic failures.
