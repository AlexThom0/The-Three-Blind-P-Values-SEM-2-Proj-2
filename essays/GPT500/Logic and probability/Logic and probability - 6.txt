Furthermore, the weight of explanatory adequacy becomes crucial in assessing the contentions of multiple causal factors. In many instances, it may appear intuitively appealing to consider several potential causes like C1 and C2. However, each added variable introduces a risk of spurious correlations or misinterpretations. The maintenance of a parsimonious framework enables researchers not only to avoid these pitfalls but also to establish a clearer pathway to understanding causal relationships. In practice, this implies a rigorous scrutiny of each additional variable considered in explanatory frameworks. Practitioners should employ robust tests and methodologies to substantiate the inclusion of any new causes, aligning their findings with existing evidence. A hypothesis must be accompanied by a strong evidentiary basis for its acceptance, so as not to detract from the credibility of the model as a whole. As research progresses, the temptation to introduce new elements must be tempered with a strategic consideration of how those elements interact with established causes.
