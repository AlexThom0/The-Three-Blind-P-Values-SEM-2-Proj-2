In seeking answers, Yanky immersed himself in texts both sacred and secular, expanding his intellectual horizons. Each page turned revealed new layers of understanding, prompting him to consider perspectives he had previously dismissed. To this end, he began engaging in discussions with friends and colleagues who held differing viewpoints, which further illuminated the nuances of his dilemma. The transition from merely accepting religious doctrines to actively questioning and debating them was both exhilarating and unsettling. He felt the weight of this belief on his shoulders as he pondered how to address the contradictions that emerged when faced with scientific realities. This intellectual pursuit also brought emotional turmoil—grappling with the fear of losing the community that had been his foundation for so long. Sharing his evolving thoughts became an act of vulnerability, fearing the potential backlash from those who held orthodox beliefs. Yet, the tension between faith and reason coexisted within him, each reinforcing the other in unexpected ways.
