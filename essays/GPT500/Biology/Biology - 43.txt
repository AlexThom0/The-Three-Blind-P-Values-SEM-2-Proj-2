The vacuum left behind by the absence of such predators altered herbivore populations, leading to unchecked growth and further habitat degradation. This cascading effect illustrates how each species within an ecosystem is intricately linked, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts aimed at preserving not just individual species, but the entire ecological framework. Some species, like the jaguars and grey wolves, have exhibited remarkable adaptability in the face of ecological changes.
