While this presents opportunities for economic development, it also creates tensions over land use and resource allocation, compounding the vulnerabilities faced by pastoral communities. Traditional grazing territories, once safeguarded by ancestral knowledge and practices, are increasingly encroached upon by commercial interests. As the demand for land intensifies, disputes arise, leading to conflicts among local herders and between communities and corporate entities. This dynamic not only threatens the way of life of many families but also disrupts social structures that have held communities together for generations. The transformation of local economies due to oil extraction can shift priorities and alter the very fabric of Turkana society. Instead of focusing on sustainable practices, there is a risk that short-term gains from oil extraction may overshadow the long-term needs of the population, exacerbating food insecurity and environmental degradation. The juxtaposition of potential wealth against the harsh realities of drought and poverty creates a complex scenario where the benefits of natural resources may not trickle down to those who need it most. Such challenges warrant urgent attention and innovative solutions.
