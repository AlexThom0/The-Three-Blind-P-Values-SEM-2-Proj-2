The vividness with which people participate—whether through the preparation of the coffin or the physical act of sealing the tomb—creates a shared understanding that death is not to be shunned but embraced as a natural progression. Rituals serve both as a means of mourning and as a way to honor the deceased, fostering a connection between generations. This cultural engagement helps dissolve the fear surrounding mortality. In contrast, in places where death is cloaked in ambiguity and hidden from public view, individuals may grapple with a sense of isolation in their grief. The act of confronting the physical realities of death, as practiced in Italian culture, encourages profound reflection and acceptance. This proactive engagement might help individuals come to terms with their own mortality, rather than retreating into denial. By witnessing and participating in these rituals, one cultivates a more nuanced perspective on life’s transience, ultimately finding strength in the shared experience of loss and remembrance.
