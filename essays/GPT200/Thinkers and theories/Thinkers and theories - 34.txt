This duality highlighted her understanding that true advocacy is rooted in deep comprehension rather than mere emotional appeal. Stebbing believed that engaging with complex ideas and challenging one’s own assumptions fosters a robust intellectual environment that is essential for sustaining democracy. As she unraveled the intricacies of her arguments, she implored her audience to move beyond superficial engagements with political rhetoric. In her view, intellectual growth allows individuals to discern misleading narratives perpetuated by those in power, enabling them to make informed choices. To cultivate this ability, she encouraged cultivating a habit of inquiry—questioning established norms and seeking diverse perspectives. This ongoing process sharpens not only one’s reasoning skills but also empowers individuals to advocate for themselves and their communities. The act of thinking critically, Stebbing asserted, is liberating in itself; it allows individuals to forge connections between their thoughts and the broader societal context. Thus, she positioned the pursuit of knowledge as an essential component of authentic freedom, ultimately arguing that every person possesses the capacity to think clearly if they are willing to confront their own biases.
