Dewey's influence on Ambedkar went beyond mere intellectual guidance; it was a catalyst for transformative thought that resonated deeply within Ambedkar's own socio-political context. The principles of pragmatism encouraged him to prioritize practical solutions over ideological dogmas, facilitating a new way of approaching the deeply entrenched social issues in India. Dewey’s emphasis on experiential learning provided Ambedkar with a framework to understand not only his own experiences as an untouchable but also the broader struggles of marginalized groups. This pragmatic approach led Ambedkar to advocate for actionable reforms in education, law, and governance, which he believed were essential to dismantling the oppressive caste system. The synergy between Dewey’s teachings and Ambedkar’s lived reality highlighted the necessity of adapting philosophical ideas to local contexts, thus enriching the discourse on social justice. By internalizing such a dynamic perspective, Ambedkar forged a unique intellectual path, demonstrating how the integration of Western philosophical principles with a keen understanding of Indian socio-political conditions could yield a compelling vision for democracy and equality. Ultimately, Dewey provided not just a philosophical foundation but an imperative for action that Ambedkar would carry with him throughout his life.
