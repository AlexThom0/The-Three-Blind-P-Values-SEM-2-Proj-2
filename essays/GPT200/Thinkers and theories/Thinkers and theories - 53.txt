This disconnection between the law and the populace fostered a climate of fear and confusion, undermining justice itself. By critiquing the convoluted nature of legal language, Beccaria sought to pave a pathway toward a clearer, more accessible framework for justice. He argued that laws should be comprehensible to all citizens, not just a select circle of intellectuals or legal professionals. The alienation caused by such an impenetrable legal system often resulted in arbitrary punishments and a lack of accountability. Beccaria posited that a rational legal framework, grounded in a clear understanding of rights and obligations, would not only empower individuals but also promote social order. He suggested that when laws are transparent and reflect collective interests, citizens would be more likely to respect and adhere to them. Thus, he advocated for a legal reform that embraced Enlightenment ideals, emphasizing reason as the cornerstone of justice. This philosophical inquiry pushed the boundaries of contemporary thought, challenging the accepted norms and inciting a movement toward a more humane and systematic approach to law, ultimately laying the groundwork for future legal theorists to build upon.
