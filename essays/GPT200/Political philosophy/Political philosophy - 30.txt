This intertwining of class struggle with anti-imperialist and anti-racist sentiments reveals a complex landscape where diverse groups unite against shared antagonists. The early socialist movements understood that the fight for economic justice was inseparable from the battle against colonial exploitation and racial injustice. By framing their struggles in terms of collective liberation, they harnessed a broader social consciousness that transcended economic issues alone. This coalition-building was crucial, allowing varying factions within the working class to see their fates as interlinked. Despite the varying ideologies, such campaigns underscored the idea that the liberation of one group is fundamentally tied to the liberation of all. By elevating issues of race and colonialism alongside labor rights, these movements aimed not only to address immediate material conditions but also to challenge the systemic injustices embedded within societal structures. These efforts reflected an understanding that true solidarity required a holistic approach—addressing intersecting oppressions while striving for an inclusive vision of social transformation. In this context, solidarity became a powerful rallying cry for diverse activists across the globe, fostering a sense of unity in shared struggles.
