Many found themselves isolated, struggling to uphold the very freedoms that were touted as the foundation of societal well-being. The promise of autonomy became more elusive for those entrenched in poverty or navigating complex family dynamics. Meanwhile, the emphasis on self-sufficiency often overlooked the intricate interdependencies that shape human connections. This dichotomy created a sense of disillusionment among citizens who perceived themselves as trapped between the ideals of a progressive social contract and the harsh realities of their daily lives. As individuals sought to negotiate their personal relationships within this framework, they often encountered tensions between the values promoted by society and the communal needs that naturally arise in familial and social contexts. The shared understanding of independence consequently faltered amidst the rising frustration, prompting important questions about the balance between personal freedom and the essential bonds that underpin community life. In this way, the social contract began to lose its resonance, calling into question whether it could truly serve the diverse needs of a varied populace.
