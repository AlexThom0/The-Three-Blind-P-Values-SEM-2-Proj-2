This monumental shift was not only physical but also ideological, as traditional agrarian values were systematically supplanted by a corporate ethos that prioritised efficiency and profit maximisation. The reshaping of America’s economic landscape necessitated new ideologies, leading to the rise of advocates for protectionism and regulatory frameworks that attempted to provide a counterbalance to the unbridled expansion of corporate power. This was exemplified in labor movements and early elements of consumer advocacy, which emerged in response to the increasing alienation of the worker and consumer alike from the processes of production and distribution. The transition was stark: as massive corporations monopolised key industries, they monopolised narratives about economic prosperity too, painting a picture of progress that often dismissed the plight of many. Simultaneously, these changes forged new identities around issues of class, labor, and consumption, highlighting the complexities of the American dream amid a burgeoning landscape of economic stratification. The consequences of this evolution still resonate today, underscoring the intricate relationship between technological advancement and social upheaval.
