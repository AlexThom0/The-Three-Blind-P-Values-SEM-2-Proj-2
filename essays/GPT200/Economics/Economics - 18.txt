This disconnect fosters an environment where prevailing models can become entrenched, shielding them from scrutiny and inhibiting innovative ideas that might challenge established paradigms. The focus on complex mathematical frameworks often overshadows the importance of empirical validation, relegating observations and practical insights to a secondary status. Engaging a broader audience in economic discussions requires simplifying these models without sacrificing their analytical rigor. By ensuring that economic theories remain grounded in observable phenomena, scholars can create a more inclusive and accurate conversation that reflects the complexities of real-world economies. This shift is essential for fostering a more holistic understanding of economic dynamics.
