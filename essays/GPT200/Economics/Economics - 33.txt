As the number of workers outstripped job opportunities, competition heightened, exposing the most vulnerable to economic instability. They faced not only declining wages but also deteriorating working conditions as employers capitalized on the abundance of labor. With the influx of both native and immigrant populations, the sharing of resources became increasingly strained, intensifying poverty in these rapidly expanding metropolitan areas. The resulting socio-economic landscape set the stage for rising tensions, as those who found themselves desperate for work were forced to accept lower wages and less favorable terms. This dynamic not only affected individual workers but also had broader implications for community cohesion and social mobility. The intertwining of demographic shifts and labor market pressures, from the 19th century to medieval times, demonstrates a persistent challenge faced by societies undergoing rapid change. Addressing these issues requires a nuanced understanding of the historical context and economic forces at play.
