By tracing the evolution of economic systems from colonial extraction to modern neoliberal policies, we uncover the persistent legacies that inform present disparities. Amin emphasized that a critical analysis must incorporate historical contingencies; the past is not merely a backdrop but actively informs current realities and the structural relationships between nations. Moreover, he argued that acknowledging these historical foundations allows for a reimagining of economic relations and a potential realignment toward equitable systems. His work challenges us to transcend disciplinary confines and adopt a holistic lens that encompasses economics, sociology, and history, revealing the interconnectedness of various forms of exploitation. By situating capitalist practices within a broader historical framework, we can better comprehend the existential challenges faced by marginalized economies today. This nuanced understanding is essential for formulating responses that are not only informed by current conditions but also resonate with the deeper currents of historical inequity. Only through this lens can we hope to engage meaningfully with the complex fabric of global economic relations.
