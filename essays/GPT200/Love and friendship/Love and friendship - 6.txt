the traditional conception of marriage faces challenges from evolving social norms and lifestyles. Increasingly, individuals prioritize personal fulfillment and happiness over the notion of a lifelong commitment. The rise of cohabitation signifies a shift where couples opt for a flexible partnership, often viewing marriage as an outdated expectation rather than a desired goal. Economic factors also play a critical role; in uncertain financial times, the prospect of marriage can seem burdensome rather than beneficial. Moreover, the digital age has transformed relationships, with online dating reshaping how people meet and select partners, often leading to more transient connections. As individuals navigate through a landscape marked by shifting priorities, the historic notion of marriage becomes contentious. This evolving mindset raises questions about the essence of commitment and whether the institution itself is adapting or decaying. With many choosing to delay marriage or forgo it entirely, society must confront the reality that marriage, while deeply rooted in human culture, is undergoing profound transformation. The question remains: can marriage survive this period of change by redefining itself to meet contemporary needs, or is it destined to fade into history as a relic of a bygone era?
