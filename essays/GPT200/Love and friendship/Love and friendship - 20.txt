The cast became a constant reminder of my isolation, not just from the physical pain but also from the cultural chasm that seemed to widen each day. As English expressions escaped me and laughter in the hallways echoed around me, I felt like an outsider watching a performance from the sidelines. The nuances of conversation were lost on me, and I hesitated to join in, fearing my accent would draw attention. Desperate to fit in, I adopted the mannerisms of my peers and studied their interactions as though they were scripts I needed to memorize. The lunch table became both a battleground and a refuge. Here, I was bombarded by new slang, the latest music, and references that seemed to fly past me like incomprehensible fireworks. Tension built with each passing day, and social faux pas piled up in my mind, ever reminding me of my otherness. I became hyper-aware of the small things—how to laugh at jokes I didn’t understand and the way to casually toss my hair, hoping to blend into the background and disappear.
