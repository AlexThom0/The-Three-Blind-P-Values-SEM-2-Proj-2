For instance, the design of urban spaces can dictate social interactions; cramped environments may lead to tension, while well-structured parks can promote relaxation and community engagement. Similarly, the materials available in a given context can shape the possibilities of creation and innovation. A skilled artisan knows how to leverage the unique properties of wood, clay, or metal to evoke desired outcomes, demonstrating that even raw materials carry a potentiality that interacts with human creativity. This interaction underscores the interconnectedness of agency across different actors. Thus, it is crucial to examine how these elements interrelate, acknowledging that agency is not solely the province of the conscious but is richly woven into the fabric of the material world, where every object contributes to the collective agency that defines experiences and facilitates transformations.
