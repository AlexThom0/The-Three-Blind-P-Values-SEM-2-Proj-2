Reformers believed that a critical examination of these sources could provide the necessary tools for Muslims to navigate modern challenges. They emphasized the need to reinterpret Islamic teachings in light of current realities, arguing that flexibility and critical thinking were essential for addressing the complexities of the modern world. By advocating for ijtihad, or independent reasoning, they sought to empower a new generation of scholars and thinkers capable of addressing the multifaceted dilemmas facing Muslim societies. This intellectual rejuvenation aimed to establish a vibrant discourse that was both rooted in tradition and responsive to the aspirations of the time, bridging the gap between past and present. Additionally, these scholars acknowledged the necessity of incorporating modern sciences and philosophies, asserting that true Islamic principles were compatible with progress and innovation. Through this dynamic reinterpretation, they hoped to cultivate a sense of agency among Muslims, encouraging a proactive stance that would counteract colonial narratives and affirm their cultural identity and autonomy.
