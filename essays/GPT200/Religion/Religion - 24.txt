In Saudi Arabia, the monarchy's unwavering support for a single interpretation of Islam creates a unique environment where adherence to this institutionalized faith fosters unity among pilgrims during Hajj. This structure allows for a controlled space where diverse experiences can coexist safely, reducing the chances of sectarian conflict. In contrast, the multitude of beliefs evident in Indian society, he posited, leads to friction, as the absence of a definitive religious authority complicates the public discourse. Moreover, he cautioned that too much freedom in religious expression could breed instability, thus undermining the democratic ethos the republic aims to preserve. In his view, the Deobandi tradition flourishes under a framework that emphasizes order and authority rather than the chaotic independence seen in pluralistic democracies. Consequently, he challenged the notion that religious diversity inherently enhances social harmony, suggesting instead that it often incites discord even among those who might share core beliefs. Ultimately, his critique reflected a profound concern for the precarious balance between governance and faith, advocating for a model that prioritizes stability over the perceived benefits of unrestricted religious expression.
