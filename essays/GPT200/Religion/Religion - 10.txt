Many tourists arrive seeking spiritual understanding, while monks gain insights into the diverse perspectives of their visitors. This reciprocal engagement is facilitated by structured programmes that emphasize sharing Buddhist philosophy and practices in an accessible manner. By participating in meditation, mindfulness exercises, or even simple conversations about life and ethics, tourists often find themselves drawn deeper into the traditions of Buddhism. This integration of non-Buddhists into temple life not only enhances the visitors' experiences but also strengthens the sense of community among the monks as they articulate their teachings in a modern context. This dynamic interplay not only preserves the relevance of Buddhism but also helps adapt its practices to contemporary global challenges. Therefore, rather than viewing these exchanges as purely educational, they can be recognised as a form of living dialogue that bridges cultures and beliefs, cultivating a deeper understanding among participants. Through these interactions, the teachings of Buddha gain new life and resonance in a rapidly changing world.
