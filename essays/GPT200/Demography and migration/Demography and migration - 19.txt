He argues that the rapid economic changes have created a paradox where wealth is seen as a necessary means to an end but is free from the burdens of historical context. For these individuals, the pursuit of money often overshadows ethical considerations, leading to a stark generational divide. Children raised in a rapidly globalizing culture grapple with conflicting ideals: the aspiration for wealth clashing with a newfound awareness of social responsibility and ethical consumption. This dissonance is magnified by the expansive reach of technology and social media, which exposes them to diverse perspectives that challenge their upbringing. Increasingly, younger generations question the status quo, seeking a balance between successful careers and meaningful lives, often feeling trapped between their parents' pragmatic worldview and their own ideals. The struggle to reconcile these varying values can create tension within families, as conversations surrounding financial success often trigger deeper debates on identity, purpose, and legacy. In this context, the juxtaposition of material success and moral integrity becomes a defining challenge for the youth, influencing how they perceive their roles within society.
