The park was shrouded in late afternoon light, a bittersweet aura that made laughter and tears intertwine. My classmates shared stories of our time together, recalling the passion and hope that had driven us to protest despite the risks. Each anecdote felt like a lifeline, reminding me of the courage we nurtured in each other amid the chaos that surrounded us. As I looked around, I saw the determination etched on their faces, a reflection of our shared dreams for a better future. We hugged tightly, each embrace a silent promise to remain connected even through the distances that would soon stretch between us. The air was thick with the smell of freshly cooked injera, and the sounds of our favorite songs echoed softly in the background. I felt a tickle of joy mingle with the heaviness in my heart, knowing that leaving would mean carrying these memories as a source of strength. Yet, the uncertainty loomed large—what if tomorrow brought an end to our aspirations? The potential for immediate danger lingered, but so did the hope that our fight might someday lead to change. Each goodbye felt like a seed planted, ready to bloom in the future we all wished to see.
