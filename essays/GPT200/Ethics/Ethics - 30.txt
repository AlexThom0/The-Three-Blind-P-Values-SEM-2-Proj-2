This perspective allows for a more nuanced understanding of morality that harmonizes personal desires with altruistic impulses. By acknowledging that humans possess both selfish and altruistic instincts, Mengzi crafted a middle path that moves beyond the extremes of Yang Zhu’s egoism and Mozi’s rigid impartiality. He posited that true ethical behavior stems from nurturing our natural capacities for empathy, which then aligns with our self-interests rather than opposes them. Thus, the flourishing of individuals and the community is a shared endeavor, one that arises from a well-rounded appreciation of our inherent dispositions. This synthesis of self-love and care for others forms the core of Mengzi’s moral philosophy, asserting that genuine virtue arises when personal well-being is intertwined with collective welfare. Ultimately, recognizing the complexity of human motivations leads to a more comprehensive understanding of ethical living, where compassion becomes a natural expression of our humanity.
