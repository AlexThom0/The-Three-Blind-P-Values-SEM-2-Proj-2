Yet, the very framework that treats animals as mere property clouds our ethical responsibility towards their suffering. This perspective entrenches a disconnect between our moral intuitions and the reality of animal lives, leading to rationalizations that overlook the intrinsic value of sentient beings. For instance, factory farming practices epitomize this disjunction; animals are often raised in deplorable conditions with the sole purpose of maximizing production, treated as objects rather than as living entities with needs and emotions. As a result, the suffering they endure is often trivialized in discussions around efficiency and economic gain. Moreover, societal norms and institutional structures reinforce this mindset, making it challenging to envision alternatives that prioritize animal welfare. This commodification of life not only perpetuates cruelty but also stifles conversations about the necessity of such practices in our diets. When we consider the profound impact of this worldview, it becomes clear that advocating for significant changes in our relationship with animals requires not only a reassessment of specific practices but also a fundamental shift in how we conceptualize their existence within our economic systems. The recognition of animals as sentient beings deserving of rights is crucial for dismantling these oppressive structures.
