In both historical instances, a trend emerges: the erosion of loyalty among those tasked with safeguarding the realm. As resentment and disillusionment fester, soldiers become less inclined to risk their lives for leaders whose primary interest lies in self-enrichment. This realization leads to a deeper examination of not just the military, but also the political frameworks that allow such corruption to thrive unchecked. In the absence of accountability, the fabric of society unravels, leaving wide gaps that are easily exploited by insurgent groups or foreign powers. The rapid ascent of non-state actors, bolstered by local grievances, amplifies the chaos created by elite malfeasance. Consequently, the very foundations of governance rot from within, as indifference to duty and moral decay permeate all levels. This grim tableau serves as a reminder that unchecked ambition and greed can dismantle robust systems of defense, leading to swift and catastrophic failures in the face of adversity. Such historical parallels should compel contemporary societies to scrutinize their own governance structures before they face similar disintegration at critical moments.
