The transient nature of street life forces children into a world of survival, where trust is a luxury and vulnerability can be fatal. Many form tight-knit bonds with other street children, creating a makeshift family that offers support in a harsh environment. These relationships, though born out of necessity, are fraught with pressure and competition. The need to prioritize one's own safety can lead to betrayal, as resources are scarce and moments of vulnerability can be exploited. As they navigate this gritty landscape, their childhood is violently stripped away, replaced by a constant battle against hunger, exposure, and violence. The street becomes an unforgiving teacher, where lessons in survival overshadow the joys of youth. All of this shapes their identities, as they grapple with alienation from society and the stigma that accompanies their existence. While some might dream of a return to a home they barely remember, others adopt the street as their only reality, accepting their fate with a mix of resignation and resilience. In the shadows of every abandoned building, there lies a story of struggle, echoing the urgent need for systemic changes to address the root causes of these children’s displacement.
