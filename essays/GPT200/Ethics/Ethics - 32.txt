The experiences of the sentient cow contribute to a richer life; its memories, emotions, and capacity for joy add intrinsic value that cannot be overlooked. This distinction becomes vital when evaluating the ethical frameworks surrounding animal rights and welfare. If our focus shifts solely to the outcomes of death, we risk neglecting the quality of life prior to that inevitable end. Advocates for animal welfare argue that creating environments where animals can thrive, experience pleasure, and form bonds is fundamentally better than promoting existence devoid of meaningful interaction. In this context, the decision to favor sentience over non-sentience isn't merely academic; it challenges us to reconsider the ethical responsibilities we carry towards all living beings. Thus, when weighing the benefits of happy versus zombie cows, one must also account for the value of a life lived, however transient it may be. This perspective fosters a deeper understanding of our relationships with animals, pushing toward practices that honor their experiences rather than diminish them to mere commodities.
