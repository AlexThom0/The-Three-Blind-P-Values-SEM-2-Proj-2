Additionally, the presence of unique traits, like colloblasts—specialized cells that capture prey—pointed towards an advanced level of specialization not seen in other early animals. This led to a reconsideration of their position within the animal kingdom, emphasizing the need to carve out a new narrative that accounted for the unique features of ctenophores. As more evidence accumulated, the idea that ctenophores represented the original model of nervous system evolution began to recede, giving way to a deeper appreciation for their role in elucidating the complexity of early animal life. This newfound understanding shifted perspectives, inviting researchers to reevaluate other branches of the evolutionary tree with the same critical lens.
