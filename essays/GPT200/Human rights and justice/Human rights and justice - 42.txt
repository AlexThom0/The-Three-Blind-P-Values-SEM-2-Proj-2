Some older Uighurs shared fleeting stories, their voices dropping to whispers, hinting at resentment beneath layers of imposed cheer. It became increasingly clear that the true complexity of Xinjiang's identity was being overlooked, reduced to a spectacle designed for an audience that craved simplicity over nuance. The resulting cry for authenticity and recognition echoed in the spaces where hands clapped and smiles were fabricated, an unsettling reminder of the realities simmering just beneath the surface of choreographed merriment.
