Professional development sessions often leave educators overwhelmed, providing theoretical frameworks without the necessary tools for implementation. Furthermore, teachers face the challenge of reconciling students' diverse backgrounds and experiences, which can significantly influence their perceptions of effort and success. Particularly in under-resourced schools, the prevailing risk of failure exacerbates students' anxieties, making them hesitant to embrace challenges. In this complex environment, the simplistic application of growth mindset principles can backfire, perpetuating a cycle of disengagement rather than fostering resilience. For educators, the task is not merely about inculcating a positive attitude toward learning but also about equipping students with specific skills to navigate setbacks. To create meaningful change, a dialogue must form between educational research and classroom practices, ensuring that theories are adapted to meet the nuanced needs of students. Without this alignment, well-intentioned initiatives risk becoming little more than slogans, failing to reach the very students they aim to uplift. Adopting a more integrated approach will be essential for bridging the gap between theory and practice.
