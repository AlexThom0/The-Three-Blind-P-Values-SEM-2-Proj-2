Can a painting, a novel, or a song truly shift the needle in the grand calculus of happiness? While the EAs focus on measurable impacts, the cultural and emotional contributions of art resist quantification. Artistic works can provoke thought, inspire action, and foster connections that transcend rational calculations of happiness. This subjective value might be dismissed by some as trivial, yet it forms the bedrock of human experience. People often turn to art during times of crisis or uncertainty; it provides solace, a sense of belonging, or merely a moment of beauty in an otherwise chaotic world. Engaging with art often encourages empathy and understanding, extending beyond simple metrics of wellbeing to touch on the deeper motivations that drive human behavior. Without acknowledging the role of art in cultural discourse, one risks overlooking an entire dimension of our shared humanity, which underpins the very essence of what it means to live a fulfilled life.
