By presenting a signed copy by an artist’s assistant, we posited that the connection to the original creator would still resonate, allowing participants to engage with the work as though it were the artist’s own. This setup highlights the complex dynamics surrounding authorship and authenticity in art, inviting discussions about the intrinsic value of creativity and intention. Furthermore, it prompts us to consider whether the emotional and intellectual experiences tied to viewing art are somehow altered based on its origin, regardless of the flawless execution of the piece. Would viewers still perceive the spirit of the artist in a work created in collaboration with or under the direct guidance of a master? The depth of our connection to a piece may rest heavily on factors beyond mere aesthetics, intertwining narratives of intention, labor, and the historical context of creation. As we explored these dimensions, it became clear that the nuances of authorship play a pivotal role in shaping our responses to art and the inherent dialogues they inspire.
