Collecting art thus became a means for the Attalids to assert their legitimacy and to intertwine their identity with that of the great Hellenistic heritage. They didn't merely amass pieces for aesthetic appreciation; each artwork was a deliberate statement about their sophistication and connections to illustrious traditions. This practice reflected a broader motif in which art transcends mere decoration, transforming into a tapestry of claims to power, identity, and historical lineage. As such, the pieces in their collection became symbols of their aspirations and achievements, serving to align their status with the revered legacies of the past. Moreover, this act of collecting created a conduit through which the Attalids could narrate their own story against the backdrop of historical upheaval, positioning themselves as legitimate successors to a rich cultural history. By actively engaging with the visual arts, they not only populated their kingdom with beauty but also crafted an enduring narrative that linked their lineage to that of esteemed predecessors, ultimately reinforcing their authority in a tumultuous era.
