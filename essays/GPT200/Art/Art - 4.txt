This dissolution of categories can lead to a form of artistic relativism, where the criteria for excellence and authenticity are called into question. As artists borrow freely from disparate styles and traditions, they may inadvertently dilute the very essence of their chosen medium. This syncretism, while often celebrated for its ingenuity, can obscure critical responses that are rooted in an understanding of historical context and established conventions. Furthermore, audiences may find themselves adrift in this landscape of hybridized art, unsure of how to engage meaningfully with pieces that seem to resist classification. Curating thoughtful discussions around these mixed-authorship works requires a delicate balance between acknowledging innovation and preserving the integrity of artistic traditions. By failing to articulate the foundational elements that give rise to artistic relevance, we risk losing not only our appreciation for the artistry itself but also the essential dialogues that enable us to connect with and learn from these evolving forms. Ultimately, the challenge lies in recognizing the potential for both enriched expression and compromised artistry in this complex interplay.
