Women like Steevens, who might have wielded influence through their affluence, were instead reduced to subjects of grotesque folklore, their stories twisted to underscore societal fears. This interplay of wealth, beauty, and rejection transcended centuries, echoing in contemporary discussions about women in positions of power and the relentless scrutiny they face. The implications of these tales reveal a troubling pattern: a persistent unease about women's autonomy, manifested through myths that continue to cultivate insecurities in modern narratives.
