This eccentricity, far from deterring Kafka, highlighted the profound disconnect between the conventional expectations of health and the radical approaches emerging at that time. Müller’s emphasis on wholesome living and physical discipline resonated with Kafka’s inherent desire to transcend the frailties of existence, even as he grappled with his own ill health. The allure of Müller’s methodology lay in its promise of a transformative experience, one that suggested the possibility of reclaiming agency through personal rigor. Kafka, caught in the throes of societal expectations, found in Müller’s teachings a fleeting hope that self-improvement could bridge the chasm separating his ideals from his reality. This interplay between admiration and skepticism reflected broader anxieties of the era, where modernity relentlessly pressed against traditional values. As both men navigated this turbulent landscape—Kafka through his literary pursuits and Müller through his fitness doctrines—they created a cultural dialogue that reverberated well beyond their individual lives, shaping contemporary views on health, identity, and the quest for meaning.
