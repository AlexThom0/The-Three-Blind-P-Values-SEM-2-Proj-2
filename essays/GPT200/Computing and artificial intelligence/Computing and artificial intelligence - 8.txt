The fear of being punished by an entity that does not yet exist challenges traditional notions of free will and ethical responsibility. Such debates not only reflect anxieties about AI's capabilities but also illuminate a deeper existential unease about human agency and the choices we make in the face of an uncertain future. As these discussions unfold, they provoke further questions about the role of morality in shaping technological advancement and the responsibilities that come with it. The exploration of these ideas serves as a cautionary reflection on how we might navigate our relationship with impending advancements.
