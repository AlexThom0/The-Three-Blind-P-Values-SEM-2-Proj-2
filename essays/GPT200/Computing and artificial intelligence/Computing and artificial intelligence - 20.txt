This reductionist approach has proven useful, especially in artificial intelligence and machine learning applications, where simplified models can achieve remarkable tasks. However, relying solely on these connections misses the intricacies of how glia and other supporting cells influence neural dynamics. Recent research suggests that glial cells are not merely passive supporters but play active roles in neurotransmitter uptake, modulation of synaptic efficacy, and maintenance of homeostasis. This intricate interplay between glia and neurons may subtly alter learning processes and memory formation, indicating that any comprehensive model of the mind must include these dimensions. Additionally, the biochemical environment shaped by glial activity could influence how information is processed and stored at the synaptic level. By underestimating glia, existing models may provide a skewed representation of cognitive functions. Thus, a more holistic approach that integrates the contributions of glia alongside traditional neural paradigms is necessary to truly grasp the complexity of thought and consciousness. This could lead to breakthroughs not only in neuroscience but also in the development of more sophisticated artificial intelligences that are capable of nuanced understanding and interaction.
