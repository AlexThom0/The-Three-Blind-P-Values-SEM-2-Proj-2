Users are encouraged to tailor their authentic voices to the expectations of an algorithm, playing a game where engagement metrics dictate value. This compromises not just individual expression but the very fabric of community discourse, as voices that deviate from the algorithm's preferences are drowned out. The emphasis on quantifiable success leads to a culture of superficiality and emotional detachment, where genuine dialogue is sacrificed at the altar of virality. Users become not only participants but also products, shaped and reshaped by the demands of their audience and the overarching structures that govern online visibility. The notion that one's self-worth is tied to the number of followers or likes can be disheartening, as it encourages conformity and discourages authentic engagement. This phenomenon highlights a crucial tension: the desire to be heard clashes with the necessity to adapt to a landscape where popularity often overshadows substance. In this context, the tools of rhetoric evolve into instruments of manipulation rather than meaningful communication.
