Motivated by the desire to create a vibrant cultural scene, the three friends began brainstorming ways to expand Cingel's idea into a sustainable initiative. They recognized that the arts community in Kingston, though rich in talent, often struggled with financial uncertainties. Many artists grappled with precarious living conditions, unable to afford necessary medical services. Drawing from their personal experiences and connections, they envisioned a system where creativity could directly counter health care costs. This led to the concept of O+, a model where artists could receive health care in exchange for performances, gallery exhibitions, or workshops. They began reaching out to local practitioners, hoping to forge connections that would benefit both communities. Initial conversations with healthcare providers revealed a willingness to participate, intrigued by the idea of giving back while also supporting local art. This synergy offered an opportunity to redefine the relationship between art and wellness, emphasizing mutual support over transactional exchange. As they developed the framework for O+, they realized they weren’t just addressing an immediate need; they were also laying the groundwork for a transformative community bond that would resonate well beyond the confines of Kingston itself. Such partnerships could potentially alter the landscapes of both the arts and health care sectors.
