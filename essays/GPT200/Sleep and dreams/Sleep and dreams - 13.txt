The constant yearning for a good night’s sleep contrasted sharply with the emotional weight of potential motherhood. I often found myself grappling with the decision of whether to plunge into the chaos of nurturing a new life while wrestling with my own unresolved anxieties. Each sleepless night served as a reminder of my limitations, heightening my awareness of how such fatigue could compound with the demands of child-rearing. This spiraled into an internal dialogue where I questioned not just my readiness for motherhood but also the nature of my relationships. Friends and partners alike seemed to wade through the murky waters of my nightmarish sleep struggles, often both exhausted and unsure if their support was truly helpful. Loneliness crept in during the quiet hours when my thoughts raced and my body craved rest. I clung desperately to the idea that avoiding the responsibilities of parenting could shield not just myself but also my potential children from the burdens I carried. In that fragile state of mind, I narrowly began to equate sleepless nights with the failure to find balance, fearing that bringing a child into my world would only deepen the struggles I fought daily.
