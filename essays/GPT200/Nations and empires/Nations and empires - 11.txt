This surge in populism was not merely a cry for sovereignty but also a response to perceived threats to cultural purity, with figures like Modi and Farage harnessing economic grievances and social fears to galvanize support among disenchanted voters. The imagery of a besieged homeland resonated deeply, prompting communities to rally around the promise of restoration to a former glory that they believed had been lost to globalism and migration. The manipulation of historical trauma served to consolidate power, as those in leadership positions utilized imagery of past conflicts and the rhetoric of revival to foster a sense of urgency. Both in India and Europe, the interplay between historical narrative and contemporary identity politics created a potent explosive force, turning insecurities into a rallying cry for action. This was a deliberate strategy to frame the political debate, ensuring that those who dared to question the return to a purported nationalistic ideal were marginalized as traitors to their own identities. Consequently, loyalty to the nation-state was transformed into a battleground where the stakes were framed as existential.
