Furthermore, the failure of observers, including Du Bois, to fully grasp the implications of settler colonialism reflects a broader trend in scholarship where attention is diverted to the apparent triumphs of liberation movements while neglecting the complex realities on the ground. Such historical oversights render scholars vulnerable to adopting superficial understandings of race and power, undermining their efforts to analyze contemporary settler colonial contexts effectively. The lessons gleaned from these misapprehensions reveal not only the intricacies of identity politics but also the necessity for a more nuanced exploration of how oppressive legacies continue to shape geopolitical landscapes in the Global South. Addressing these gaps in critical discourse is essential for a comprehensive understanding of the long-lasting impact of colonial ideologies on both Indigenous populations and broader socio-political dynamics.
