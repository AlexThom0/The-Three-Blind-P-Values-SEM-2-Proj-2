These incomplete stories can generate feelings of abandonment or guilt, as children struggle to understand what happened and why. Cain’s research indicates that a nuanced approach is necessary, one that considers the child's developmental stage and emotional readiness. For many, an oversimplified explanation can feel unsatisfying, while a more tailored narrative fosters a space for emotional processing. This complexity is exacerbated when societal stigmas discourage open conversations about suicide, forcing families into silence. In Maddy’s case, the absence of dialogue not only hindered her ability to grieve but also perpetuated a cycle of isolation and misunderstanding. Children need reassurance that their feelings are valid and that they can express them without fear of judgment. Offering a safe environment for discussing difficult topics creates an opportunity for healing and integration of loss into their lives, thereby providing a foundation upon which they can build resilience as they navigate their grief.
