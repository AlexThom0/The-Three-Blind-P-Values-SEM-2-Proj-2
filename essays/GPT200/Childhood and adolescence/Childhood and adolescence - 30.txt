Each box held a fragment of Marie's life, remnants of her recent move, whispering secrets of a past she was still in the process of sorting. I chuckled softly at the thought of the girls, who thrived on familiarity, navigating this new territory filled with kale chips and quinoa. Even in this moment of rest, I could sense their underlying curiosity about Marie's world. While our travels had been spontaneous, this small pause offered a chance to absorb the quirks of another life—especially in the way Marie approached food, as a vessel for health rather than indulgence. The girls, accustomed to a diet full of pizza and sweets, would need time to adjust. I imagined the conversations we might have over breakfast, each bite revealing more about our distinct preferences and the shared laughter that could bridge our differences.
