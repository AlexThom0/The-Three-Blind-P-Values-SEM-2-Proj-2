The connection between the emotional weight that Nelson felt during our visit and his professional focus on younger children struck me as particularly profound. The stark transition from his academic realm into the harsh realities faced by older orphans highlighted a dissonance that many professionals encounter when confronted with the consequences of their work. For Nelson, these older teenagers represented a future unfulfilled, a stark reality contrasted against his experiences with infants, who typically evoke instinctual feelings of protection and hope. The emotional burden intensified as he recognized not just the loss of childhood experiences for these teens, but also the potential paths that could have been available had circumstances been different. This awakening to the plight of older orphans raises critical questions about societal responsibilities and the roles of various stakeholders—parents, educators, policymakers—in ensuring that these children are not left behind. As someone deeply engaged in the science of human development, Nelson’s perspective serves as a reminder that advocacy can emerge from unexpected encounters, compelling one to reflect on broader systemic issues that continue to marginalize those who are most vulnerable. Such realizations can ignite a passion for change that transcends professional boundaries.
