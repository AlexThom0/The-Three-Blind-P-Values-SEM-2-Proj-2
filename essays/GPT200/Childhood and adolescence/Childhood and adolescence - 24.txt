Instead of engaging with feminist literature or contemporary debates, their perceptions were shaped disproportionately by social media snippets and viral trends, which simplified complex issues into catchy phrases or memes. Furthermore, their interpretations were frequently colored by peers and influencial figures in their lives, who may not fully comprehend or represent feminist ideologies. This societal backdrop fostered a sense of apathy, as many young people viewed the fight for gender equality through a lens of nostalgia, thinking historical struggles were relics of the past. As a result, the sense of urgency and relevance that characterized ongoing battles for equality seemed diminished. The lack of critical dialogue in their environments contributed not only to this gap in understanding but also to a missed opportunity for empowerment through knowledge. Exploring these dynamics reveals the need for educational reform that actively incorporates diverse feminist perspectives, encouraging students to question, analyze, and engage meaningfully with the evolution of gender rights in contemporary society.
