Breeders began to prioritize specific traits that highlighted the poodle's elegance and unique silhouette over its working capabilities. This shift towards aesthetic preferences spurred a breeding boom that favored appearance over health, leading to an increase in various genetic disorders. As competition grew, the quest for the ideal show dog became ruthless, with breeders often resorting to inbreeding practices to maintain desirable traits. This has raised ethical questions within the dog breeding community, as many of these practices disregard the well-being of the animals in pursuit of perfection. Additionally, the poodle's evolving look influenced perceptions of other breeds, prompting a trend among owners to conform their pets to similar standards of beauty. As a result, not only did the breed's appearance transform, but these changes sparked broader discussions on the length to which breeders and owners will go for recognition within dog shows, often at the expense of genetic diversity and animal health. Such dilemmas encapsulate the complexities associated with the modern handling of pedigree breeds.
