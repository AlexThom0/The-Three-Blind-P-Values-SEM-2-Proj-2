This creates a hierarchical framework where some lives are deemed more valuable based on cognitive similarities to human thought processes. The implications extend beyond legal and ethical boundaries, influencing societal attitudes towards mental health, disability, and even animal rights. When we privilege certain types of cognition, we inadvertently foster an environment where beings that do not conform to narrow definitions of 'rationality' are devalued or neglected. In doing so, we reinforce a misconception that to think is to be superior, overlooking the rich tapestry of experiences that define existence across different forms of life. Such beliefs not only undermine the interconnectedness shared among all organisms but also perpetuate the false narrative that our humanity thrives solely in the realm of reason. Recognizing the limitations of this perspective encourages a more inclusive understanding of life and agency, a recognition of the complex interplay between instinct and thought. The challenge lies in embracing the entirety of our being, where both body and mind coexist in a dynamic relationship.
