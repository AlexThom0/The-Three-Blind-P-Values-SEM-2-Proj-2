Notably, behaviors such as growling, barking, and even physical confrontation were more frequent in the presence of a familiar rival, indicating that these reactions are tied to established social hierarchies and past interactions. These findings suggest that the emotional landscape for dogs may be layered and complex, influenced by their social experiences and relationships with humans and other dogs alike. The research points to a fundamental capacity for comparison and social awareness, which might draw parallels to human emotional responses in competitive contexts. The use of various experimental controls not only highlighted the non-random nature of aggression but also signaled that these behaviors could stem from a deeper cognitive processing of social cues. Thus, understanding canine jealousy might not solely illuminate the emotional capabilities of dogs but could also shed light on the evolution of social cognition across species. As we explore these intricate interpersonal dynamics, it becomes crucial to differentiate between instinctual reactions and complex emotional interpretations.
