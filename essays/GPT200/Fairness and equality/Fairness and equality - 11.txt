In this context, the shared experiences of marginalized individuals become foundational to the movement's identity. Leaders emerged not through conventional means but through their ability to mobilize others around shared values and lived experiences, indicating a radical departure from typical authority structures. The focus shifted from doctrinal orthodoxy to lived faith, as participants sought to embody the teachings of compassion, justice, and communal responsibility that Jesus exemplified. This also meant embracing diverse voices and perspectives, where every individual’s story added richness to the collective narrative. Just as Jesus used parables to challenge societal norms, the participants in Occupy employed creative forms of protest and dialogue to critique the status quo, illustrating that action often speaks louder than words. The subversion of established norms mirrors the transformational potential found in Jesus' interactions with the marginalized. By engaging directly with those on the fringes, Occupy not only echoed the radical ethos of Jesus but also redefined the parameters of what it means to be a community focused on social justice.
