Critics pointed out that while certain genetic predispositions may exist, they interact dynamically with environmental factors, cultural narratives, and personal experiences. Such critiques call into question the reductionist view that disregards the multifaceted nature of identity. It prompts a re-evaluation of the chorus celebrating preordained identities and shifts focus toward understanding the intricate interplay of influences that define a person.
