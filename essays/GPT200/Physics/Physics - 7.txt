This method leverages the concept of decision-making under uncertainty, where the observer makes a comparative judgment rather than an absolute one. By framing the question in this way, we diminish the subjective bias inherent in the traditional ‘did you see it?’ format. Moreover, it allows researchers to quantify the observer's sensitivity to the stimuli presented. This experimental design can be further refined by adjusting the intensity of the light and exploring different conditions, such as varying background noise or introducing distractions. Each adjustment helps illuminate the thresholds of human perception concerning single-photon detection. These explorations not only enhance the understanding of our visual system but also refine the methodologies used in quantum optics. As we accumulate data from these trials, we can better understand the intricate dance between perception and the physical properties of light, revealing deeper insights into the fundamental interactions that underpin our experience of reality.
