This shift in focus reflects a broader cultural trend where the role of management becomes synonymous with the relentless pursuit of efficiency and profit maximization. The educational frameworks within business schools reinforce this mindset, often neglecting the moral and ethical dimensions of leadership and decision-making. As a result, emerging managers are trained to navigate complex systems without considering the ecological and social consequences of their choices. This narrow approach not only risks perpetuating systemic inequalities but also undermines the very fabric of community and collaboration necessary for true progress. The disconnect between managing for profit and stewarding resources responsibly raises critical inquiries about the role of managers in shaping societal values. In a world increasingly driven by technological innovation and consumerism, the challenge lies in redefining management to encompass a broader definition of success—one that includes ecological stewardship, social responsibility, and the well-being of future generations. The potential for transformative change exists, but it requires a radical realignment of priorities, urging current and future managers to think beyond traditional metrics of success.
