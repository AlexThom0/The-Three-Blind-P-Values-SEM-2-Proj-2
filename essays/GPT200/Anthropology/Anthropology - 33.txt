The nuances of adat permeated not only the art itself but also the very act of creation and exchange. In his hands, the wood seemed to come alive, and I realized that each cut and sway of his chisel were not merely strokes but a dialogue with the ancestors. To him, crafting a piece was an invocation, a responsibility to maintain the balance within the community. There was an unspoken rhythm that guided his movements, each strike signaling respect for the material, the tradition, and the unseen presence of those who had come before. I began to grasp that my participation was not simply passive observation; it required a level of understanding, as everything within these walls resonated with the collective memory of the Tanimbarese people. It dawned on me that my initial ignorance, my lack of grasp on the depth of adat, placed a layer over the experience, one which I would need to peel back through patience and engagement. Thus began my deeper journey into the intertwining of artistry and ancestral obligation.
