Many people think of autism solely in terms of impairments, overlooking the diverse ways it can manifest. This leads to a disconnect between my reality and public perception. My experiences are woven with complexities that might not conform to traditional frameworks; they are imbued with strengths and capabilities that coexist alongside challenges. Moments of social engagement, which others might interpret as typical, are often incredibly taxing for me, requiring an intricate dance of self-regulation and cognitive effort. Furthermore, the insistence on a narrow view of what autism “looks like” reinforces not only the stigma but also the notion that individuals on the spectrum should conform to expected behaviors. The pressure to conform often manifests as a dual struggle: navigating the world as a person with autism while managing the external expectations of those who cannot see beyond their assumptions. It's a constant battle between authenticity and acceptance, and amidst it all, there lies a profound desire for understanding and connection.
