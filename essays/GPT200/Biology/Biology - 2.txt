For adherents of Merleau-Ponty's philosophy, engaging the body more fully invites a re-evaluation of our interaction with the environment. Every texture we touch, every surface we walk upon, contributes to the narrative of our embodied existence. The intertwining of touch and sight fosters a multisensory perception that allows us to perceive not just the appearance of things, but their essence and connectivity to us. In this light, a walk through a forest or a meadow becomes a complex interplay between sensory modalities—each contributing to an expansive awareness. Engaging with the nonhuman world through touch leads to a profound recognition of the interdependence of all beings. By allowing ourselves to experience the world viscerally, we move beyond mere observation into a realm where empathy and reciprocity thrive. Understanding our place in this shared existence encourages us to acknowledge the nonhuman actors in our lives and fosters a more sustainable and ethical relationship with the environment that supports us all.
