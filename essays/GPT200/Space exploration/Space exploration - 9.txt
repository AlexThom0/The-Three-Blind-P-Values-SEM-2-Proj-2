As the demand for water increases, particularly for long-duration space missions and potential colonization of other celestial bodies, the concept of in-situ resource utilization becomes critical. Water not only serves as a vital resource for drinking but can also be split into hydrogen and oxygen, creating fuel and breathable air. This dual-purpose value transforms water into a strategic asset. Numerous proposals have emerged regarding the development of robotic mining systems that can identify and extract water ice from asteroids and comets, with reformation techniques that can convert ice into liquid water and gases for energy storage and life support. However, the process is not without its hurdles; the technology needed to efficiently locate, extract, and process water in the harsh environment of space is still in its infancy. Moreover, the economics of transporting water or its processed forms back to Earth must also be considered carefully. The balance between technological development and economic feasibility will ultimately dictate the success of this venture, paving the way for sustainable human presence beyond our planet.
