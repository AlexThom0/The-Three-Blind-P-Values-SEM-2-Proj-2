Cities that were once vivid tapestries of culture and possibility transformed into mere backdrops for my internal struggles, a kaleidoscope of experiences that felt distant and unattainable. Each new place offered a fleeting thrill, yet the thrill quickly faded, leaving me anchored in a perpetual state of longing. I began to notice how the sounds and sights of bustling streets became white noise against the cacophony of my thoughts; the clinking of glasses, the laughter of strangers, and the hum of conversations blurred into an unchained echo of disconnection. Attempts to engage with the flora of human connection were thwarted by emotional barriers I had unwittingly erected. I was often the observer, staring through a glass wall that kept the warmth of community at bay, an outsider in my own journey. It became clear that the act of travel, meant to liberate, had inadvertently wrapped its chains of solitude tighter around me, further entrenching my desire for belonging in a world designed for departure.
