The colors and lines of the Nefertiti bust evoke a sense of divinity, suggesting that her image served not only as a model for reproduction but as an embodiment of royal power and presence. This artistic choice aligns with the broader artistic movements of the time, which favored stylization over realism to convey the subject's majesty and status. Additionally, the choice of materials signifies her elevated role within the artistic and religious narratives of the period. While the physical form captures attention, the subtleties in her expression convey an aura of mystery and strength that speaks to her influence as a queen. The idealization of her features and the vibrant detailing reflect Thutmose’s desire to create a lasting legacy, imbuing each bust with significance beyond the artisan's craft. This intention reinforces the interpretation of these sculptures as not merely artistic endeavors, but also as vehicles for political propaganda, solidifying Nefertiti's place within the pantheon of ancient Egyptian deities and ensuring her remembrance for generations. Thus, the psychology behind the creation of these busts reveals layers of meaning intertwined with both personal and collective identity.
