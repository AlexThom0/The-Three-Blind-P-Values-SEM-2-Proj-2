In this context, the religious symbol becomes a rallying point, selectively invoked to foster an exclusivist national identity that marginalizes those deemed outside its bounds. Orbán’s rhetoric draws on historical myths, suggesting that the defense of Christianity is synonymous with the defense of the nation itself, effectively merging religious allegiance with national loyalty. This conflation serves to legitimize policies that target minorities and dissenting voices, reinforcing an atmosphere of intolerance under the guise of preserving cultural heritage. As such, the reimagining of St Stephen’s legacy not only celebrates a historical narrative but also repositions it as a template for contemporary power dynamics. The manipulation of religious sentiment in this manner underscores the broader implications of how authority can exploit deeply rooted beliefs to justify actions that might otherwise be considered reprehensible. The consequences ripple outward, fostering environments where division and enmity are sown in the name of unity and piety.
