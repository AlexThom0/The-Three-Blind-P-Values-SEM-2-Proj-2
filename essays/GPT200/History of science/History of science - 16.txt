They were designed to accommodate adjustments quickly, allowing the observers to recalibrate the lenses in response to the shifting shadows cast by the Moon during the eclipse. The advent of lighter materials and telescopic technology meant that these portable options were far more feasible under the constraints of wartime logistics. Additionally, employing multiple smaller telescopes increased the likelihood of capturing the event from various angles, offering a richer dataset for analysis. While the reliability of the coelostats remained a concern, the enthusiasm of the astronomers to push the boundaries of their work was evident. The team understood that during such rare astronomical events, innovative thinking was required to adapt to the circumstances. They also began discussing potential backup observational sites in case any of their original plans were thwarted by unexpected weather conditions or equipment failures. Their commitment to documenting the eclipse demonstrated not only scientific ambition but also a camaraderie that transcended the difficulties posed by the larger conflict surrounding them.
