Photographers started adopting a "truthful" lens, aiming to document life as it unfolded rather than manipulating it for aesthetic purposes. However, this new direction did not erase the complex interplay between reality and representation. The mere act of selection, whether conscious or subconscious, dictated what stories were told and how they were perceived. Thus, even in a documentary framework, the choices made by photographers held considerable influence over the narrative. The tension between objective documentation and subjective interpretation became increasingly evident, highlighting the photographer's role not just as an observer but as a curator of reality. Scenes that might seem candid were often still steeped in the photographer's vision and intent. This negotiation between artistic expression and ethical responsibility marked a critical evolution in the philosophy of photography, where the line between documentation and creation remained tantalizingly blurred.
