I could sense the air thickening with judgment, the kind that lingers unspoken yet resonates in the glances exchanged around me. Each laughter felt like a blade, slicing through the façade of normalcy I carefully constructed. The casual conversations morphed into a symphony of whispers, a chorus that struck at my core. People shared stories, yet I felt as though I was an outsider, eavesdropping on a narrative I could neither grasp nor join. My mind raced through the hallways of self-doubt, questioning what I had contributed to warrant such scrutiny. Was it my openness about living with schizophrenia that had prompted this response? Or perhaps it was the lingering stigma that impeded their ability to see me as anything other than my diagnosis. The dichotomy of perceived normality and my own experience unfolded in stark juxtaposition, leaving me grappling for footing amidst a crowd that felt increasingly alien. What was supposed to be a celebration of awareness instead became a reminder of isolation, where the bonds meant to connect us unraveled into threads of misunderstanding and fear. Each moment under their gaze weighed heavily, an invisible boundary between our worlds that felt impossible to cross.
