We felt as though key elements regarding my father's personality, preferences, and past medical history had been overlooked. Although the team exhibited professionalism and expertise, there was a sense that they viewed the case primarily through a clinical lens, which often overlooks the more nuanced aspects of patient-centered care. Each of us knew my father, not just as a patient, but as a husband, father, and friend. The challenge lay in articulating those dimensions in a way that resonated with the medical staff, bridging the gap between their data-driven decisions and our emotional understanding of his situation. Engaging in this dialogue would be essential not only for his treatment but also for preserving the dignity and respect he deserved throughout his care journey.
