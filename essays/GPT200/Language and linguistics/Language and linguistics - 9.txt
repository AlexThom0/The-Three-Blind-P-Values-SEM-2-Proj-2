The juxtaposition of these unknown words with the known universe of meaning proposes a compelling tension. Hildegard’s lexicon, with its roots in her lived experiences and observations, seems to speak not just in the abstract, but to the tangible struggles of her time. This dynamic interplay between the celestial and the earthly world echoes throughout her writings, presenting a multifaceted linguistic landscape. As she crafts her hymns, Hildegard navigates this blurred boundary, perhaps as a means to assert her own authority in a male-dominated society. By interspersing words unrecognizable to many, she calls into question existing power structures and religious doctrines, urging her audience to grapple with the inherent chaos of existence. In doing so, she crafts a text that demands active engagement—an invitation to decode and interpret a divine experience as controversial as it is sacred. Through her unique linguistic choices, Hildegard not only communicates her spirituality but also embodies the struggle to articulate the sublime in a world rife with earthly concerns.
