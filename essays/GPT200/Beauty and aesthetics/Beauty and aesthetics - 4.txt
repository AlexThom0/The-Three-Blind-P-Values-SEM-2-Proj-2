This framework maintained that purity and dominance were tied intrinsically to racial identity, leading to a hierarchy where mixed races were deemed inferior. Such ideologies not only perpetuated social divisions but also justified imperialist endeavors as they cast colonization as a noble project aimed at 'preserving' the so-called superior races. This race-based narrative found its way into various societal structures, influencing policies and fueling systemic discrimination. Scholars and leaders who subscribed to these beliefs underscored the cultural superiority of their own nations, leveraging pseudo-scientific claims to bolster xenophobic attitudes. As a result, the concept of racial purity not only served as a theoretical basis for societal structures but also manifested in practical applications that marginalized large sections of populations deemed 'impure.' The dangers of these ideologies extended beyond academia, permeating everyday life and contributing to relationships filled with mistrust and animosity. The repercussions endured, embedding themselves in the consciousness of societies through laws, customs, and even national identities that were built on the myth of racial superiority and purity, further entrenching the cycle of inequality.
