This gaze cultivates a professional distance, fostering a disconnection from the lived realities of patients. The therapeutic process, often framed as a technical interaction, risks becoming an algorithmic series of steps devoid of genuine understanding. Such frameworks operatively prioritize diagnosis and intervention over the nuanced narratives of individuals. Therefore, the diverse experiences and histories that shape a patient's mental state are frequently minimized or overlooked altogether. This reductionist approach not only neglects the rich context of each patient's life but also perpetuates a power imbalance, where the clinician assumes the role of expert and the patient is relegated to a passive object of study. Consequently, the therapeutic alliance, an essential element in fostering healing and understanding, can be compromised. By relegating subjective experience to mere data points, these models overlook what might contribute significantly to recovery: the complexities of human relationships, socio-cultural factors, and the profound influence of patient narratives. Ultimately, this underscores a critical need for a paradigm shift, one that embraces a more integrative understanding of mental health, recognizing the interplay of internal and external factors in the therapeutic process.
