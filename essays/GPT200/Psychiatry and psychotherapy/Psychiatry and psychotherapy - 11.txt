These strategies included mindfulness practices and grounding techniques, which helped to anchor them in the present moment. We encouraged them to reconnect with their bodily sensations, fostering a sense of safety that had often been absent in their lives. Slowly, some began to articulate their experiences in less overwhelming contexts, allowing for incremental exposure rather than full immersion in traumatic memories. This gradual approach aimed to build resilience, enabling patients to face their histories without becoming engulfed by them. The use of creative expression—through art or writing—also served as a therapeutic outlet, enhancing self-awareness and offering a window into their inner worlds. With the right support, individuals previously caught in cycles of avoidance and numbing began to find new pathways toward understanding their trauma. This shift marked a vital turning point, as they started to reclaim power over their narratives and, by extension, their lives. By integrating these tools, they not only developed coping mechanisms but also fostered a sense of agency, gradually restoring their capacity to navigate the complexities of their emotional landscapes.
