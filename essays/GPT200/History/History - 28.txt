This loyalty grants the leader a potent source of legitimacy, one rooted in shared needs rather than individual power struggles. Machiavelli emphasizes the stark contrast between the aspirations of the common people and the self-serving desires of the nobility. While the latter often seeks to manipulate the political landscape for personal gain, the former simply desires security and the opportunity to thrive. In this light, a wise prince cultivates a connection with the people, addressing their grievances and reinforcing their sense of community. He can utilize this bond as a shield against the intrigues of the nobility. Moreover, when faced with crises or challenges, a ruler who has garnered sincere public support can mobilize significant resources, both material and emotional, to navigate turbulent times. Thus, the leader’s focus should be predominantly on fostering a deep-seated allegiance with the populace, ensuring that their collective strength becomes an indomitable force against any factional dissent. This foundation secures both power and a legacy that endures.
