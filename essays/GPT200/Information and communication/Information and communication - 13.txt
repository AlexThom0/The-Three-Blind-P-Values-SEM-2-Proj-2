Eyal's four-step model consists of the trigger, action, reward, and investment. Each step is designed to create a feedback loop that reinforces user behavior. The triggers can be external, such as notifications or reminders, compelling users to engage with the platform. Once the user takes action, they encounter a reward that varies in nature, stimulating dopamine release and enhancing the likelihood of repeated behavior. This unpredictable reward mechanism is crucial; it keeps users returning to the platform, as they are conditioned to seek out the gratification that may come from a new and exciting experience. The final component, investment, encourages users to invest time or effort into the platform, thereby increasing their commitment. This investment often leads to more personalized experiences, making it even more challenging for users to disengage. Over time, as users invest more into the platform—whether through creating content, liking posts, or connecting with friends—the perceived value of the service increases, deepening their attachment. The cyclical nature of this model creates a compelling case for understanding how digital platforms capture and retain attention, reinforcing the patterns of compulsive engagement that many users experience.
