Startups flooded the market, promising platforms where individuals could express themselves, collaborate, and innovate without the barriers of traditional hierarchies. The mantra became one of inclusivity and democratization, pushing the idea that anyone could become a creator, an influencer, or a thought leader. As these platforms flourished, they also blurred the lines between professional and personal life, fostering an environment where authenticity was prized and the notion of a singular, curated self began to dissolve. Users were encouraged to engage in self-experimentation, initiating a tidal wave of mixed-authorship documents that reflected the collaborative nature of this new digital ecosystem. Content was no longer the product of isolated genius but a tapestry woven from diverse voices and experiences, leading to a redefinition of authorship itself. In this emergent landscape, contributions were no longer bound by traditional parameters, paving the way for a new era of collective creativity.
