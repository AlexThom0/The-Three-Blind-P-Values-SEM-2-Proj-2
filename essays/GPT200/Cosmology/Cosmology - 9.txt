This intertwining of quantum mechanics and general relativity complicates the narrative surrounding the Big Bang, as each theory describes reality from radically different perspectives. Attempts to unify these theories often lead to more questions than answers; for instance, how can time exist without a preceding framework? Moreover, some physicists propose models that suggest the possibility of a pre-Big Bang universe, challenging the notion of absolute nothingness. In this context, ideas such as cyclic models or multiverse theories have emerged, suggesting that our universe might be just one of many, shaped by diverse laws of physics. These radical hypotheses raise further philosophical inquiries about existence and the nature of reality itself. If time and space are born at the moment of the Big Bang, should we consider what came “before” as an entirely different construct, unbound by traditional understanding? Bridging the gap between these various views may hold the key to unraveling the mysteries of cosmology, prompting scientists to reconsider our foundational assumptions about the universe and its origin. Such explorations not only fuel scientific inquiry but also resonate with age-old philosophical debates.
