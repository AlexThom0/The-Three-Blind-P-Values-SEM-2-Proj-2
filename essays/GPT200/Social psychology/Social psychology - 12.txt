Traditional structures that once shaped political allegiance and social belonging have weakened, leaving individuals vulnerable to extremist ideologies that promise clarity and connection. The allure of both far-right and jihadist narratives lies in their capacity to provide a sense of belonging, purpose, and, crucially, an identity that feels solid amidst the chaos. Access to diverse, instantaneous communication platforms facilitates the rapid spread of these ideologies, enabling the seamless exchange of grievances and recruitment messages. Young people, in particular, are drawn to these movements as they search for meaning in a world that often feels indifferent to their struggles. As they engage with like-minded individuals online, the boundary between real-life interactions and virtual communities blurs, reinforcing a collective consciousness that rejects mainstream narratives. This phenomenon creates echo chambers, where the reinforcement of radical views thrives, making it increasingly difficult for counter-narratives to penetrate. Without concerted efforts to address these underlying social fractures, the cycle of radicalisation is likely to continue, further complicating the landscape of modern political and cultural discourse.
